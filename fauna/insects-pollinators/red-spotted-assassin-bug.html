Red-spotted Assassin Bug

Triatoma rubrofasciata

🔵 Beneficial Predator 🩸 Do Not Handle

Common Names: Red-spotted Assassin Bug, Kissing Bug, Cone-nose Bug

Local Name: சிவப்பு புள்ளி கொண்ட கொலை வண்டு

Family: Reduviidae

Order: Hemiptera

Size: 16–24 mm

Activity: Nocturnal

🔍 Identification
AppearanceLarge, flattened bug with dark brown to black body; distinctive red/orange markings on abdomen margins and legs; elongated curved beak (proboscis)
ColourDark brown/black with red-orange spots or bands on abdomen edges
Distinguishing FeaturesLong curved proboscis folded under head; flattened oval body; red markings on abdomen; attracted to lights at night
🌿 Habitat & Behaviour
HabitatOrchards, gardens, agricultural areas, human habitations; hides in crevices, under bark, in stored materials during day
BehaviourNocturnal predator; attracted to lights; solitary hunter; hides in dark crevices during day; can enter homes
DietPrimarily predatory — feeds on insects (flies, mosquitoes, beetles, caterpillars); may occasionally feed on blood of sleeping animals or humans
Seasonal PresenceYear-round in tropical climates; 1–2 generations per year
🔄 Lifecycle
Egg StageEggs laid in clusters in sheltered locations; hatch in 2–3 weeks
Nymph Stage5 instars over 2–3 months; nymphs resemble adults but lack wings
Adult Stage6–12 months lifespan; 1–2 generations per year
🌾 Orchard Impact
Impact TypeBeneficial — predator of pest insects
BenefitControls flies, mosquitoes, beetles, caterpillars, and other pest insects through predation
RiskPainful defensive bite if handled or accidentally disturbed; potential disease vector in some regions
🛡️ Pest Status & Safety
Pest ClassificationNot a plant pest; beneficial predator; human safety concern due to bite
Danger to HumansMedium-High — delivers a very painful bite if handled or disturbed; do not handle with bare hands; bite can cause localised swelling and pain
Disease RiskIn the Americas, related species transmit Chagas disease; risk is lower in Asia but caution is advised
Danger to LivestockLow — may bite sleeping animals
🚫 Prevention & Safety
Personal ProtectionAlways wear gloves when working in areas where this bug may hide; never handle with bare hands
Habitat ManagementRemove debris, bark piles, and stored materials where bugs hide; seal cracks in structures
Night WorkUse a torch when working at night; check clothing before wearing if left outdoors
If BittenClean wound with antiseptic; apply cold compress for swelling; seek medical attention if severe reaction occurs
LightingReduce outdoor lighting near living areas as adults are strongly attracted to lights

Lychee Shield Bug

Chrysocoris stollii

🚨 Pest

Common Names: Lychee Shield Bug, Jewel Bug, Metallic Shield Bug

Local Name: நகைப் பூச்சி (Nagaip Pūchchi)

Family: Scutelleridae

Order: Hemiptera

Size: 18–25 mm

Activity: Diurnal

🔍 Identification
AppearanceBroad, shield-shaped bug with a greatly enlarged scutellum covering the entire abdomen and wings. Brilliant metallic coloration gives it a jewel-like appearance.
ColourMetallic blue, green, or purple dorsum; yellow abdominal underside broadly margined with purple; black spots around spiracles
Distinguishing FeaturesEnlarged scutellum forming a continuous shield over abdomen; iridescent metallic sheen; often mistaken for a beetle
Similar SpeciesOther Chrysocoris species and Calliphara nobilis; distinguished by specific colour pattern and abdominal markings
🌿 Habitat & Behaviour
HabitatOrchards, gardens, agricultural fields, and forest edges across continental Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent
BehaviourFeeds on plant sap using piercing-sucking mouthparts; often found in aggregations on host plants; releases foul odour when disturbed
Activity PatternActive during daytime; often seen basking on leaves in sunlight
DietPolyphagous sap-feeder — pigeon pea, pongamia, jatropha, arecanut, coffee senna, and various other plants
Seasonal PresenceYear-round in tropical regions; most abundant during warm, humid months
Host PlantsPigeon pea (Cajanus cajan), Pongamia, Jatropha, Arecanut, Coffee Senna, Lychee
🔄 Lifecycle
Egg StageEggs laid in clusters on the underside of host plant leaves; barrel-shaped and pale green
Nymph Stage5 nymphal instars; nymphs are less colourful than adults, gradually developing metallic sheen with each moult
Adult StageAdults are long-lived; feed and reproduce on host plants throughout the warm season
Generations per YearMultiple generations per year in tropical climates
🌾 Orchard Impact
Impact TypePest — sap-feeding causes plant stress and yield loss
Crops AffectedPigeon pea, pongamia, jatropha, arecanut, coffee senna, lychee, and various ornamental plants
Damage DescriptionSap-sucking weakens plants, causes wilting and reduced vigour; heavy infestations lead to flower and fruit drop
Damage SeverityModerate; can be significant in pigeon pea and arecanut plantations
🛡️ Pest Status & Safety
Pest ClassificationPolyphagous pest of multiple crops; most damaging to pigeon pea and arecanut
Danger to HumansNone — harmless to humans; may release a foul-smelling defensive secretion when handled
Invasive StatusNative to continental Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent; not invasive
🚫 Prevention & Control
Physical ControlHand-pick adults and nymphs from plants; destroy egg clusters on leaf undersides
Biological ControlEncourage egg parasitoids and predatory insects; maintain habitat for natural enemies
Organic SpraysNeem oil spray to deter feeding; insecticidal soap for nymph control
Early Warning SignsAggregations of brightly coloured bugs on plant stems and leaves; wilting or yellowing of infested shoots

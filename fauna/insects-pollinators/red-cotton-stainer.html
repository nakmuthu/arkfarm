Red Cotton Stainer

Dysdercus cingulatus

🚨 Pest

Common Names: Red Cotton Stainer, Red Cotton Bug

Local Name: சிவப்பு பருத்தி நாசினி (Sivappu Paruthi Nāsini)

Family: Pyrrhocoridae

Order: Hemiptera

Size: 12–18 mm

Activity: Diurnal

🔍 Identification
AppearanceElongate-oval bug with a predominantly red body, white collar band, and black markings on the forewings. Abdomen has white stripes ventrally.
ColourBright red with a white collar; forewings red with black hemelytral spots; ventral abdomen with white stripes
Distinguishing FeaturesBright red and black coloration with white collar; femora have varying amounts of black; gregarious habit
Similar SpeciesDysdercus koenigii — very similar but slightly smaller with completely red femora (D. cingulatus has black-marked femora)
🌿 Habitat & Behaviour
HabitatCotton fields, orchards with malvaceous plants, gardens with okra and hibiscus across South and Southeast Asia
BehaviourGregarious; forms large aggregations on host plants; migrates between host plants as seeds ripen; feeds on nectar and fruit between hosts
Activity PatternActive during daytime; adults migrate to new hosts when current food source is depleted
DietSap-feeder — cotton bolls and seeds, okra, hibiscus, jute, silk cotton tree, kapok, teak, and citrus fruits
Seasonal PresenceYear-round in tropical regions; peak populations during cotton boll formation
Host PlantsCotton (Gossypium), Okra, Hibiscus, White Jute, Silk Cotton Tree (Bombax ceiba), Kapok, Teak, Citrus
🔄 Lifecycle
Egg StageFemales lay 60–90 eggs per batch in chambers dug in soil; eggs hatch in about 5 days
Nymph Stage4 nymphal instars over 30–40+ days; nymphs are bright red and gregarious
Adult StageAdults live several weeks; females lay multiple egg batches during their lifetime
Generations per YearMultiple overlapping generations per year in tropical climates
🌾 Orchard Impact
Impact TypePest — causes direct feeding damage and transmits staining fungi
Crops AffectedCotton, okra, hibiscus, jute, silk cotton tree, kapok, teak, citrus, and other malvaceous crops
Damage DescriptionPierces cotton bolls to feed on developing seeds, introducing fungi that stain the lint yellow-brown; seeds wither, fibres fail to expand, and bolls may abort
Damage SeverityHigh — most serious pest of cotton in South and Southeast Asia; causes significant economic losses
🛡️ Pest Status & Safety
Pest ClassificationMajor pest of cotton; also damages okra, hibiscus, and other malvaceous crops
Danger to HumansNone — harmless to humans
Invasive StatusNative to South and Southeast Asia; widespread across tropical regions
🚫 Prevention & Control
Physical ControlHand-collect adults and nymphs from plants; remove and destroy fallen bolls; clean up crop residues after harvest
Biological ControlEncourage predatory insects and parasitoids; egg parasitoids can reduce populations
Organic SpraysNeem seed kernel extract; neem oil spray; insecticidal soap for nymph control
Early Warning SignsAggregations of bright red bugs on cotton bolls or okra pods; stained or discoloured lint; aborted bolls

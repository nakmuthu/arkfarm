Herbal - Henna / Maruthani

Lawsonia inermis

Common Names: Henna, Maruthani, Mehndi, Mignonette Tree

Local Name: Maruthani (Tamil), Mehndi (Hindi)

Family: Lythraceae

Origin: North Africa, Western and Southern Asia

Plant Type: Shrub / Small tree, 2–6 meters tall

Variety: Single species with regional cultivars

Average Lifespan: 25–50 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMulti-stemmed, densely branched shrub or small tree
Plant Size2–6 meters tall
LeavesSmall, opposite, elliptical, 1.5–5 cm long, glabrous, green turning yellow-green
FlowersSmall, fragrant, creamy-white to pink, borne in terminal panicles
Flower CharacteristicsHighly fragrant, 4-petaled, blooms in clusters; attracts pollinators
FruitSmall, round capsule (4–8 mm), brown when ripe, containing many seeds
Fruit ColorGreen turning brown at maturity
Seed CharacteristicsTiny, angular, brownish seeds; numerous per capsule
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots
BarkGreyish-brown, smooth when young, becoming rough and flaky with age
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; thrives in hot, arid conditions
Temperature Range25–45°C ideal; tolerates extreme heat; sensitive to frost
Rainfall Requirement200–600 mm annually; highly drought-tolerant
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy to loamy soil; tolerates poor and alkaline soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing1.5–3 meters between plants; closer for hedge planting
Irrigation MethodDrip or flood irrigation; minimal once established
Water RequirementLow; very drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large pots (20+ liters); prune regularly to maintain shape
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleTwice yearly — before monsoon and after harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFarmyard manure, compost, neem cake
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil spray if needed
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base to retain moisture in early growth
Training SystemCan be trained as hedge, bush, or small tree form
Pruning TimeAfter leaf harvest; annual pruning encourages bushy growth
How to PruneCut back to 30–50 cm from ground level for leaf production; shape as needed for hedges
Common PestsGenerally pest-free; occasional leaf-eating caterpillars, aphids
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, powdery mildew in humid conditions
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, avoid waterlogging, remove infected leaves
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsRajasthan (Pali, Sojat), Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh in India; Egypt, Sudan, Pakistan
Market UsesBody art (mehndi), hair dye, textile dye, cosmetics
Processing UsesDried and powdered leaves for paste; essential oil from flowers; leather tanning
Market Value (Approx.)₹50–200 per kg of dried leaf powder; premium organic henna commands higher prices
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismSymbol of celebration, beauty, and good fortune in South Asian and Middle Eastern cultures
Traditional UsesBridal mehndi art in weddings; applied during festivals like Karva Chauth, Eid, and Diwali; cooling agent in hot climates; used in religious ceremonies
Historical SignificanceUsed for over 5,000 years across North Africa, Middle East, and South Asia; mentioned in ancient texts and found in Egyptian mummies
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Skin CareLeaf paste applied to treat skin conditions, burns, and wounds; natural cooling agent
Hair CareNatural hair conditioner and dye; strengthens hair, reduces dandruff
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesLeaf paste reduces inflammation; traditionally applied to soles of feet to reduce body heat
Antifungal & AntibacterialLawsone compound has proven antifungal and antibacterial properties
Traditional MedicineUsed in Ayurveda and Unani medicine for headaches, fever, and joint pain

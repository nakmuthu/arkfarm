Karpuravalli / Indian Borage

Plectranthus amboinicus

Common Names: Indian Borage, Country Borage, Mexican Mint, Cuban Oregano

Local Name: Karpuravalli / Omavalli (Tamil), Patharchur (Hindi)

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: Southern and Eastern Africa; naturalized throughout tropics

Plant Type: Perennial succulent herb

Average Lifespan: Perennial; 3–5 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSpreading, succulent herb; fleshy stems; strongly aromatic
Plant Size30–60 cm tall; spreads 60–90 cm wide
LeavesThick, fleshy, ovate; hairy; strong camphor-oregano scent; 4–7 cm
FlowersSmall, pale purple to white; in terminal spikes
Root SystemShallow, fibrous; stems root at nodes easily
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates heat and drought
Temperature Range15–38°C; frost-sensitive
Soil TypeWell-drained; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementLow; drought-tolerant; avoid overwatering
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes; excellent container herb
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMinimal; occasional compost; very low maintenance
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant due to strong aromatic oils; occasional mealybugs
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root very easily; place in soil or water; roots in 1–2 weeks
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Immune SupportLeaves used for coughs, colds, and respiratory conditions; widely used in Siddha medicine
Digestive HealthUsed for digestive issues, flatulence, and as a carminative
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesContains carvacrol and thymol; antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties

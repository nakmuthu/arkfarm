Jackfruit - Panruti

Artocarpus heterophyllus 'Panruti'

Common Names: Panruti Jackfruit, Panruti Palaa

Local Name: Panruti Palaa Pazham (Tamil), Panruti Kathal (Hindi)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: Panruti, Cuddalore District, Tamil Nadu, India

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Panruti — renowned Tamil Nadu selection known for sweet, aromatic, firm bulbs

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge evergreen tree with dense, rounded canopy
Plant Size12–20 meters tall; canopy spread 8–14 meters
LeavesAlternate, elliptic, 10–25 cm, dark green, leathery, glossy upper surface
FlowersMonoecious; cauliflorous inflorescences on trunk and major branches
Flower CharacteristicsMale spikes club-shaped; female spikes barrel-shaped; appear on older wood
FruitLarge, 10–25 kg; oblong to irregular; thick sweet bulbs with excellent aroma and firm texture
Fruit ColorGreen rind with prominent spines; deep golden-yellow bulbs
Seed CharacteristicsLarge oval seeds (2–3 cm); edible when boiled or roasted
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system; excellent anchorage
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; thrives in hot, humid conditions of coastal Tamil Nadu
Temperature Range25–38°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1000–2000 mm annually; tolerates seasonal dry periods
Soil TypeRed laterite, alluvial, or sandy loam; well-drained
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–12 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin irrigation; drip for young trees
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees only; requires ground planting for production
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (30–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, vermicompost, bone meal
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, Beauveria bassiana
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch with coconut fronds or leaf litter
Training SystemModified central leader; maintain accessible lower branches
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (August–September)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; thin canopy for light
Common PestsShoot borer, fruit borer, mealybug, bark-eating caterpillar, spittle bug
Common DiseasesRhizopus fruit rot, leaf spot, pink disease, dieback
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood sanitation, proper spacing, copper fungicides, prompt removal of infected material
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–March (Tamil Nadu)
Fruiting SeasonApril–August; Panruti jackfruit festival held annually in June–July
Days to Harvest100–150 days from pollination
Harvest IndicatorsHollow sound when tapped, strong sweet aroma, spines flatten, last leaf on stalk yellows
Average Yield per Plant100–200 fruits per tree per year (mature); Panruti is a reliable bearer
Pollination TypeWind and insect pollination
Pollination NotesMonoecious; wind is primary pollinator; good natural fruit set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsWind-pollinated primarily; gall midges also contribute
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 3–8 weeks; not true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible but not common commercially
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood or approach grafting on seedling rootstock; preferred for maintaining Panruti quality
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate once established; deep roots access groundwater
Soil Conservation PracticesLarge canopy reduces erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedPanchagavya, jeevamrutha, composting, mulching
Companion PlantsPepper vine, banana, turmeric, ginger as intercrops
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides habitat for birds, bats, and insects; large shade canopy
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsPanruti (Cuddalore), Villupuram, and surrounding districts in Tamil Nadu
Market UsesFresh fruit, unripe vegetable, seeds as snack; Panruti jackfruit has GI tag recognition efforts
Processing UsesChips, papad, jam, halwa, dried jackfruit, canned bulbs
Market Value (Approx.)₹30–100 per kg (whole fruit); premium during Panruti jackfruit season
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismPanruti is known as the "Jackfruit Capital" of Tamil Nadu; annual jackfruit mela celebrates the harvest
Traditional UsesWood for furniture and temple carvings; unripe fruit as vegetable in Tamil cuisine; seeds in curries

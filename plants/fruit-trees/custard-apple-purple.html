Custard Apple - Purple / Red

Annona squamosa (purple variety)

Common Names: Purple Custard Apple, Red Sugar Apple, Purple Sitaphal

Local Name: Seetha Pazham (Tamil), Sitaphal (Hindi), Sharifa (Urdu)

Family: Annonaceae

Origin: Tropical Americas; purple variety selected in cultivation

Plant Type: Perennial semi-deciduous tree

Variety: Purple/Red — striking purple-red skin with sweet, creamy white pulp

Average Lifespan: 15–25 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall semi-deciduous tree with open, irregular canopy
Plant Size3–7 meters tall; canopy spread 3–5 meters
LeavesAlternate, simple, oblong-lanceolate, 5–15 cm long; thin, pale green
FlowersGreenish-yellow, fleshy, pendulous, 2–3 cm long; solitary or in small clusters
Flower Characteristics3 thick outer petals; protogynous; emit sweet fragrance to attract beetle pollinators
FruitRound to heart-shaped, 6–10 cm diameter; knobby surface with distinct areoles; purple-red skin
Fruit ColorDeep purple to reddish-purple when ripe; white creamy pulp inside
Seed CharacteristicsNumerous dark brown to black seeds, 1–1.5 cm; hard, smooth, toxic if crushed
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; needs warm, dry period for fruiting
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; frost-sensitive; needs warm winters
Rainfall Requirement500–1500 mm annually; dry period needed for flowering
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; tolerates poor, rocky soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing4–6 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; reduce during dormancy
Water RequirementModerate during fruiting; drought-tolerant when dormant
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in 20–40 liter pots; compact size makes it suitable
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year; pre-flowering, during fruiting, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, bone meal, wood ash
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor regularly; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for mealybugs and scale; fruit bagging for borer protection
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemOpen center; keep low for easy harvest and hand pollination
Pruning TimeDuring dormancy (January–February in India)
How to PruneHard prune to promote new flowering wood; remove dead and crossing branches
Common PestsMealybug, fruit borer, scale insects, fruit fly
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, black canker, dieback, fruit rot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, fruit bagging, copper fungicides, remove infected parts
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsApril–July (India); on new growth after dormancy
Fruiting SeasonAugust–December; fruits ripen progressively
Days to Harvest100–140 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple, areoles separate slightly, yields to gentle pressure
Average Yield per Plant20–50 fruits per tree (mature); 15–40 kg
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by beetles; hand pollination recommended
Pollination NotesHand pollination dramatically improves fruit set (from 5% to 60%+)
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsProtogynous flowers; beetle-pollinated; hand pollination highly recommended
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 3–6 weeks; may not produce true purple fruit
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood grafting on A. squamosa seedling rootstock; fruits in 2–3 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; drought-tolerant
Soil Conservation PracticesGrows on marginal and rocky soils; helps utilize wasteland
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; panchagavya, jeevamrutha
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, short-duration vegetables as intercrops
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers support beetle pollinators; fruit feeds birds and bats
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Maharashtra, AP, Gujarat), Thailand, Philippines
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit, specialty markets (purple variety commands premium)
Processing UsesPulp, milkshakes, ice cream, traditional sweets
Market Value (Approx.)₹80–200 per kg (purple variety premium); grafted plants ₹200–500
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismCustard apple (Sitaphal) named after goddess Sita; considered auspicious in Hindu culture
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh; seeds used as insecticide; bark and leaves in traditional medicine

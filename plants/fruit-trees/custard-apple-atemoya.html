Custard Apple - Atemoya

Annona × atemoya

Common Names: Atemoya, Custard Apple (Australia), Pineapple Sugar Apple

Local Name: Atemoya (universal), Hybrid Sitaphal (Hindi)

Family: Annonaceae

Origin: Hybrid — cross of Cherimoya (A. cherimola) × Sugar Apple (A. squamosa); first created in 1908

Plant Type: Perennial semi-deciduous tree

Variety: Atemoya — combines cherimoya's flavor with sugar apple's tropical hardiness

Average Lifespan: 20–35 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall to medium semi-deciduous tree with spreading, open canopy
Plant Size4–8 meters tall; canopy spread 4–6 meters
LeavesAlternate, simple, ovate to elliptic, 8–15 cm long; medium green, slightly hairy
FlowersGreenish-yellow, fleshy, pendulous, 3–4 cm long; larger than sugar apple flowers
Flower Characteristics3 thick outer petals; protogynous; fragrant; beetle-pollinated
FruitHeart-shaped to round, 8–15 cm diameter; bumpy surface; creamy white, custard-like pulp
Fruit ColorPale green to yellowish-green when ripe; smooth to slightly bumpy skin
Seed CharacteristicsFewer seeds than sugar apple; dark brown, 1.5–2 cm; hard, smooth
Root SystemShallow to moderately deep fibrous root system
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; more cold-tolerant than sugar apple
Temperature Range20–32°C ideal; tolerates brief frost to -2°C (more than sugar apple)
Rainfall Requirement700–2000 mm annually; dry period beneficial for flowering
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily); protect from strong winds
Spacing5–7 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; consistent moisture during fruiting
Water RequirementModerate; increase during flowering and fruiting
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in 30–50 liter pots; manageable size for container growing
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule4 times per year; quarterly applications during growing season
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, FYM, bone meal, seaweed extract, potash
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor regularly; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for mealybugs and scale; fruit bagging for borer protection
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch to retain moisture and protect shallow roots
Training SystemOpen center; maintain low canopy for hand pollination access
Pruning TimeDuring dormancy; before new growth flush
How to PruneModerate pruning to shape; remove dead wood; tip-prune for branching
Common PestsMealybug, chalcid wasp (seed borer), scale insects, fruit fly
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, purple blotch, root rot, dieback
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, air circulation, copper fungicides, fruit bagging
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMay–August (India); spring to summer
Fruiting SeasonSeptember–January; fruits ripen progressively
Days to Harvest120–160 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns pale green-yellow, skin gives to gentle pressure, sweet aroma develops
Average Yield per Plant30–80 fruits per tree (mature); 25–60 kg
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by beetles; hand pollination essential for good yield
Pollination NotesHand pollination critical — collect pollen from male-phase flowers, apply to female-phase flowers
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsProtogynous; hand pollination increases fruit set from under 10% to over 70%
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 3–6 weeks; seedlings highly variable (hybrid)
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible but slow
Propagation by GraftingVeneer or cleft grafting on A. squamosa rootstock (preferred); fruits in 2–4 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation and mulching
Soil Conservation PracticesRoot system holds soil; grows on marginal lands
Organic Practices UsedComposting, panchagavya, biological pest control
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, herbs, short-duration vegetables
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers support beetle pollinators; fruit feeds birds and bats
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsAustralia (Queensland), Florida (USA), Israel, Brazil, India, Taiwan
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit, specialty markets
Processing UsesPulp, ice cream, smoothies, desserts, frozen pulp
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–400 per kg (premium fruit); $5–15 per fruit (USA/Australia)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismRepresents the best of both parent species; valued as a premium tropical fruit worldwide
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh as a delicacy; considered one of the finest tropical fruits; gaining popularity in India

Olosapo

Couepia polyandra

Common Names: Olosapo, Zapote de Mico, Monkey Sapote

Local Name: Olosapo (Spanish/Central American)

Family: Chrysobalanaceae

Origin: Central America (Mexico, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Wild species; very few cultivated selections

Average Lifespan: 50–80 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large evergreen tree with dense rounded canopy
Plant Size10–20 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesAlternate, simple, elliptic to oblong, 8–15 cm long, leathery, dark green above with pale underside
FlowersSmall, white to cream, 5-petaled, in terminal or axillary racemes
Flower CharacteristicsMildly fragrant; attract bees and insects; numerous stamens
FruitOval drupe, 5–8 cm long, thin skin with sweet aromatic flesh surrounding a single large seed
Fruit ColorYellow to orange-brown when ripe; flesh yellow-orange
Seed CharacteristicsSingle large seed with hard shell; edible kernel when roasted
Root SystemDeep taproot with lateral roots; wind-resistant
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical lowland to mid-elevation; frost-intolerant
Temperature Range22–35°C ideal; does not tolerate frost
Rainfall Requirement1500–3000 mm annually; tolerates seasonal dry periods
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained, fertile soil; tolerates various soil types
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodRain-fed in native habitat; supplemental watering in dry periods
Water RequirementModerate; somewhat drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Not recommended — grows too large
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleTwice yearly with organic matter
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, well-rotted manure, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil if needed
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base to retain moisture
Training SystemNatural form; minimal training needed
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting season
How to PruneRemove dead branches; light shaping as needed
Common PestsFruit fly, birds, monkeys (fruit predators)
Common DiseasesGenerally disease-free; occasional fungal leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; remove fallen fruit; rarely needs treatment
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May (dry season to early rainy season)
Fruiting SeasonJuly–October; coincides with rainy season
Days to Harvest90–120 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns yellow-orange, softens, aromatic scent; falls when fully ripe
Average Yield per Plant40–80 kg per tree per year (estimated for mature tree)
Pollination TypeInsect pollination (bees)
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; may benefit from cross-pollination
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; bees attracted to fragrant flowers
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 3–6 weeks; seedlings slow-growing; fruit in 5–8 years
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible but slow; limited information available
Propagation by GraftingGrafting on Chrysobalanaceae rootstock possible; limited commercial practice

Water UsageModerate; adapted to seasonal tropical rainfall

Orange - Dekopon

Citrus reticulata 'Dekopon'

Common Names: Dekopon, Sumo Citrus, Shiranuhi, Hallabong

Local Name: Dekopon (Japanese), Hallabong (Korean)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: Japan (Kumamoto Prefecture, 1972); cross of Kiyomi tangor × Ponkan mandarin

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Dekopon — seedless mandarin hybrid with distinctive top knob and exceptional sweetness

Average Lifespan: 25–50 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall to medium evergreen tree with rounded, moderately dense canopy
Plant Size3–5 meters tall; canopy spread 3–4 meters
LeavesAlternate, elliptic-lanceolate, 5–10 cm, dark green, aromatic when crushed
FlowersSmall, white, 5-petaled, highly fragrant; borne singly or in small clusters
Flower CharacteristicsSelf-fertile; intensely fragrant; attracts bees and butterflies
FruitLarge, 200–350 g; distinctive protruding bump (top knob); easy-peel, seedless, intensely sweet (13+ Brix)
Fruit ColorDeep orange rind; bright orange juicy segments inside
Seed CharacteristicsSeedless (parthenocarpic); occasional seeds if cross-pollinated
Root SystemFibrous to moderately deep; depends on rootstock used
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical to warm temperate; needs cool winters for color development
Temperature Range15–30°C ideal; tolerates brief frost to -2°C; needs winter chill
Rainfall Requirement800–1500 mm annually; supplemental irrigation in dry periods
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; slightly acidic preferred
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily)
Spacing3–5 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation preferred; consistent moisture during fruit development
Water RequirementModerate; avoid waterlogging; reduce before harvest for sweetness
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent in large containers (30–50 liters) on dwarf rootstock
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — spring, summer, early autumn; citrus-specific NPK
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, fish emulsion, seaweed extract, bone meal, neem cake
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor monthly; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, horticultural oil, insecticidal soap, kaolin clay
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (5–8 cm); keep away from trunk to prevent collar rot
Training SystemOpen vase or central leader; maintain compact form
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (late winter/early spring)
How to PruneLight pruning; remove dead wood, water sprouts, and crossing branches; shape for light penetration
Common PestsCitrus leaf miner, aphids, scale insects, citrus psyllid, spider mites
Common DiseasesCitrus canker, greening (HLB), root rot, melanose, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesDisease-free nursery stock, copper sprays, good drainage, remove infected material
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May (spring)
Fruiting SeasonDecember–March; fruit held on tree to develop sweetness
Days to Harvest270–300 days from flowering; requires extended maturation
Harvest IndicatorsDeep orange color, easy detachment, Brix reading above 13°, low acidity
Average Yield per Plant50–150 fruits per tree per year (mature)
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination (parthenocarpic fruit set)
Pollination NotesSeedless without cross-pollination; keep away from seeded citrus to avoid seed formation
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsParthenocarpic; produces seedless fruit without pollination
Propagation by SeedsNot applicable (seedless variety)
Propagation by CuttingsPossible but slow; not commercially preferred
Propagation by GraftingT-budding or cleft grafting on trifoliate orange or Cleopatra mandarin rootstock
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation
Soil Conservation PracticesCover crops between rows; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedCompost, biological pest control, cover cropping
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, marigolds, basil
Biodiversity BenefitsFragrant flowers attract pollinators; evergreen canopy provides habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsJapan (Kumamoto, Ehime), South Korea (Jeju), USA (California), Brazil
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit; gift fruit in Japan; marketed as "Sumo Citrus" in USA
Processing UsesFresh juice, marmalade, dried segments; primarily consumed fresh
Market Value (Approx.)₹500–1500 per kg (premium import); $3–6 per fruit in USA/Japan
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismConsidered a luxury fruit in Japan; popular New Year gift; symbol of sweetness and good fortune
Traditional UsesCitrus peel used in traditional medicine; fruit given as premium gift in East Asian cultures

Coconut

Cocos nucifera

Common Names: Coconut, Coconut Palm, Coco

Local Name: Thennai (Tamil), Nariyal (Hindi), Thengu (Malayalam)

Family: Arecaceae

Origin: Indo-Malayan region

Plant Type: Perennial palm tree, 20–30 meters tall

Variety: Tall, Dwarf, and Hybrid varieties

Average Lifespan: 60–100 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitTall, unbranched, single-stemmed palm with terminal crown of leaves
Plant Size20–30 meters tall (Tall variety); 5–10 meters (Dwarf variety)
LeavesPinnately compound, 4–6 meters long with 200+ leaflets; spirally arranged crown of 25–35 fronds
FlowersMonoecious; small yellow flowers in branched spadix enclosed in a woody spathe
Flower CharacteristicsMale flowers numerous and small at branch tips; female flowers larger and fewer at base of spadix
FruitLarge drupe, 20–30 cm long; fibrous husk (mesocarp), hard shell (endocarp), white meat (endosperm), and coconut water
Fruit ColorGreen when immature, turning brown when mature
Seed CharacteristicsSingle large seed; the coconut itself is the seed with endosperm (white meat) and liquid endosperm (coconut water)
Root SystemFibrous adventitious root system arising from the bole; no taproot; roots spread 2–4 meters laterally
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; thrives in humid coastal regions
Temperature Range20–32°C ideal; does not tolerate frost or temperatures below 12°C
Rainfall Requirement1500–2500 mm annually; well-distributed throughout the year
Soil TypeSandy loam, laterite, or alluvial soils with good drainage
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily); intolerant of shade
Spacing7.5–9 meters between palms (triangular planting)
Irrigation MethodBasin irrigation or drip irrigation; husk burial in basins improves moisture retention
Water RequirementHigh; 30–40 liters per palm per day in dry season
Can it Grow in Pots?Dwarf varieties can be grown in very large containers (100+ liters) but will limit yield significantly
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleTwice yearly — pre-monsoon (June) and post-monsoon (December)
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (25–50 kg/palm/year), green manure, neem cake, bone meal, wood ash
Pesticide FrequencyPreventive treatment 2–3 times per year; monitor crown regularly
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, neem cake in basin, pheromone traps for rhinoceros beetle
Mulching PracticeCoconut husk, coir pith, or dried fronds around the basin
Training SystemNot applicable — single-stemmed palm; remove dead fronds regularly
Pruning TimeYear-round removal of dried fronds and spent bunches
How to PruneRemove dead and hanging fronds; cut spent flower bunches; keep crown clean to prevent pest harborage
Common PestsRhinoceros beetle, red palm weevil, coconut mite, black-headed caterpillar
Common DiseasesBud rot, stem bleeding, leaf blight, root wilt (Kerala wilt)
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, crown cleaning, Bordeaux paste on wounds, biocontrol agents (Metarhizium)
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in tropical conditions; produces 12–14 spadices per year
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; continuous production once mature
Days to Harvest11–12 months from pollination for mature coconut; 6–7 months for tender coconut
Harvest IndicatorsMature: brown husk, sloshing water sound; Tender: green, 6–7 months old
Average Yield per Plant60–100 nuts per palm per year (Tall); 80–150 (Dwarf/Hybrid)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination predominantly (Tall); self-pollination common in Dwarf varieties
Pollination NotesWind and insects (bees, ants) are primary pollinators; male flowers open before female (protandry)
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndonesia, Philippines, India (Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka), Sri Lanka, Brazil
Market UsesFresh coconut, copra, coconut oil, coconut water, coir fiber, shell charcoal
Processing UsesCoconut oil, desiccated coconut, coconut milk, coconut cream, virgin coconut oil, activated carbon from shells
Market Value (Approx.)₹15–40 per nut; copra ₹80–150 per kg; coconut oil ₹150–250 per liter
🍈 Nutritional Information
Calories354 kcal per 100g (fresh meat)
Dietary Fiber9 g per 100g
Vitamin C3.3 mg per 100g
Vitamin ANegligible
Iron2.4 mg per 100g
Potassium356 mg per 100g
AntioxidantsPhenolic compounds, gallic acid, caffeic acid
Other Key NutrientsManganese (60% DV), copper, selenium, medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs)
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Immune SupportLauric acid in coconut oil has antimicrobial and antiviral properties
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesVirgin coconut oil shows anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects in studies
Heart HealthMCTs may raise HDL cholesterol; coconut water is rich in electrolytes
Digestive HealthHigh fiber content aids digestion; coconut water helps rehydration
Skin & Hair CareCoconut oil widely used as moisturizer, hair oil, and in traditional Ayurvedic treatments

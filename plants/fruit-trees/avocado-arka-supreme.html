Avocado - Arka Supreme

Persea americana 'Arka Supreme'

Common Names: Arka Supreme Avocado, Butter Fruit

Local Name: Vennai Pazham (Tamil), Makhan Phal (Hindi)

Family: Lauraceae

Origin: India — developed by IIHR (Indian Institute of Horticultural Research), Bangalore

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Arka Supreme — IIHR selection adapted to Indian tropical conditions with high oil content and good yield

Average Lifespan: 30–50+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large evergreen tree with spreading, dense canopy
Plant Size8–15 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesAlternate, elliptic to lanceolate, 10–25 cm, dark green, leathery; aromatic when crushed
FlowersSmall, greenish-yellow, 5–10 mm; borne in terminal panicles; dichogamous (Type A or B)
Flower CharacteristicsProtogynous dichogamy; flowers open twice — first as female, then as male; requires cross-pollination
FruitMedium to large, 200–400 g; pear-shaped; smooth green skin; creamy yellow-green flesh with high oil content (18–22%)
Fruit ColorGreen skin remaining green when ripe; creamy yellow-green buttery flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSingle large seed (3–5 cm); round to oval; tightly held in cavity
Root SystemShallow, spreading root system; sensitive to waterlogging; most roots in top 60 cm
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; adapted to Indian conditions; needs humidity
Temperature Range20–30°C ideal; tolerates up to 35°C; frost-sensitive below 0°C
Rainfall Requirement1000–2000 mm annually; well-distributed; dry period before flowering beneficial
Soil TypeWell-drained red laterite, sandy loam, or volcanic soil; avocado is extremely sensitive to poor drainage
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily); young trees need partial shade
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation essential; consistent moisture but never waterlogged
Water RequirementModerate to high; consistent moisture critical; root rot from overwatering is #1 killer
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees in large containers (50+ liters) with excellent drainage
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule4 times per year; balanced NPK with zinc and boron micronutrients
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, neem cake, bone meal, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor monthly; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, copper fungicides (for root rot prevention), Trichoderma
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch (10–15 cm) essential; keep away from trunk; mimics forest floor
Training SystemOpen center; maintain manageable height for harvest
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; light annual pruning
How to PruneTip-prune to control height; remove dead wood; maintain open canopy for light
Common PestsFruit fly, stem borer, mites, thrips, scale insects
Common DiseasesPhytophthora root rot (major threat), anthracnose, scab, cercospora leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesExcellent drainage is critical; Trichoderma application, phosphonate trunk injection, avoid overwatering
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsOctober–January (South India)
Fruiting SeasonMarch–June; Arka Supreme has good fruit retention
Days to Harvest180–240 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit reaches full size, skin color slightly dulls, stem end yields to gentle pressure, seed coat turns dark
Average Yield per Plant200–400 fruits per tree per year (mature); Arka Supreme is a good yielder
Pollination TypeCross-pollination required (dichogamous)
Pollination NotesPlant Type A and Type B varieties together for best fruit set; bees are primary pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsDichogamous; flowers open as female first, then male; cross-pollination with complementary type improves yield
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 2–6 weeks; seedlings not true-to-type; used as rootstock
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; not commercially viable
Propagation by GraftingVeneer or softwood grafting on seedling rootstock; preferred method for Arka Supreme
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate to high; efficient with drip irrigation and mulching
Soil Conservation PracticesThick mulching essential; cover crops between rows
Organic Practices UsedComposting, Trichoderma, mulching, biological pest control
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, banana (as windbreak), coffee (as understory)
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract bees; canopy provides habitat; leaf litter enriches soil
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsKarnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Maharashtra (India); IIHR varieties gaining popularity
Market UsesFresh fruit, health food, smoothies, salads
Processing UsesGuacamole, avocado oil, cosmetics, smoothie ingredient
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–400 per kg (India); growing demand in urban markets
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismKnown as "butter fruit" in India; increasingly popular as a superfood
Traditional UsesFruit consumed fresh or in milkshakes; oil used in cosmetics; leaves in traditional medicine

Lemon - Native

Citrus limon

Common Names: Native Lemon, Indian Lemon, Country Lemon

Local Name: Elumichai (Tamil), Nimbu (Hindi), Cherunaranga (Malayalam)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: South Asia (Northeast India, Myanmar)

Plant Type: Small evergreen tree, 3–6 meters tall

Variety: Native Indian — smaller, more aromatic than commercial varieties

Average Lifespan: 20–30 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall spreading tree with irregular, thorny branches
Plant Size3–6 meters tall; canopy spread 3–4 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, elliptic-ovate, 5–10 cm long; aromatic when crushed; light green with winged petioles
FlowersSmall, white to pinkish-purple on outside, 2–3 cm diameter; borne singly or in small clusters
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant, 5-petaled; blooms in flushes throughout the year
FruitSmall oval berry, 4–6 cm; thin rind, very juicy, highly acidic with strong aroma
Fruit ColorGreen when immature, turning bright yellow when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsSmall, oval, white seeds; polyembryonic; 2–6 seeds per fruit
Root SystemModerately deep taproot with spreading lateral roots; sensitive to waterlogging
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates heat well
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; frost-sensitive below 0°C
Rainfall Requirement750–1500 mm annually; well-distributed
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing4–5 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation preferred; basin irrigation also suitable
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture but avoid waterlogging
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent for containers (20–30 liters); one of the best citrus for pot culture
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — February, June, and October
Recommended Organic FertilizersVermicompost, neem cake, bone meal, FYM, fish emulsion
Pesticide FrequencyMonthly monitoring; spray as needed during flush periods
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, horticultural oil, insecticidal soap, sticky traps for leaf miners
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (dried leaves, straw) around base; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center system for good air circulation and light penetration
Pruning TimeAfter main harvest; light pruning year-round
How to PruneRemove dead wood, water sprouts, and crossing branches; thin interior for airflow
Common PestsCitrus leaf miner, aphids, citrus psylla, scale insects, fruit fly
Common DiseasesCitrus canker, gummosis, greening (HLB), powdery mildew, root rot
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper-based sprays, proper drainage, remove infected parts, use disease-free planting material
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in tropical conditions; main flushes in January–February and June–July
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; peak harvest June–August and November–January
Days to Harvest120–150 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns light green to yellow; slight softening; strong citrus aroma
Average Yield per Plant200–500 fruits per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination; parthenocarpy also occurs
Pollination NotesBees improve fruit set; single tree is sufficient for fruiting
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsAndhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Rajasthan
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, pickle, garnish, beverages
Processing UsesLemon juice concentrate, citric acid, lemon oil, pickle, dried lemon
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–120 per kg; prices spike in summer months

🌿 Medicinal Uses
Immune SupportVery high vitamin C content boosts immunity; traditional remedy for colds and flu
Digestive HealthWarm lemon water aids digestion; citric acid stimulates gastric juices
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesFlavonoids in lemon peel exhibit anti-inflammatory effects
DetoxificationUsed in Ayurveda and Siddha medicine for liver cleansing and detox
Skin CareLemon juice used topically for skin brightening; vitamin C supports collagen synthesis

Mango - Neelam

Mangifera indica 'Neelam'

Common Names: Neelam, Neelum, Neelam Mango

Local Name: Neelam (Tamil), Neelum (Kannada/Telugu)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: South India

Plant Type: Large evergreen tree, 8–12 meters tall

Variety: Neelam — late-season, small sweet fruit, excellent regular bearer

Average Lifespan: 40–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large tree with compact, upright to rounded canopy
Plant Size8–12 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 15–25 cm long; new leaves coppery-pink turning dark green
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white in terminal panicles (15–30 cm long); late-blooming
Flower CharacteristicsBoth male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; mildly fragrant; flowers later than most varieties
FruitSmall to medium, 150–250 g; oval-oblong, smooth skin, sweet aromatic fiberless pulp with distinctive floral flavor
Fruit ColorGreen turning bright yellow-orange when ripe; uniform color without blush
Seed CharacteristicsPolyembryonic seed; commonly used as rootstock; good seed-to-flesh ratio despite small size
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system; very hardy and drought-tolerant
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; highly adaptable to various tropical and subtropical conditions
Temperature Range24–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement750–2500 mm annually; dry spell before flowering beneficial
Soil TypeAdaptable to most well-drained soils — laterite, red loam, alluvial, sandy loam
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing7–9 meters between trees (slightly less than larger varieties)
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; reduce watering during flowering
Water RequirementLow to moderate; very drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, grafted trees in large pots (40+ liters); more compact than other mango varieties
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (20–40 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, vermicompost
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray, methyl eugenol traps for fruit flies
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center or modified leader system
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (August–September, later than other varieties)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; tip-prune for compact growth
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealybug, leaf webber
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial canker, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper-based fungicides, proper spacing, remove fallen debris, good orchard sanitation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April (South India); flowers 4–6 weeks later than early varieties
Fruiting SeasonJune–August; late-season variety — available when most other mangoes are finished
Days to Harvest110–130 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns bright yellow-orange; strong sweet floral aroma; slight softening
Average Yield per Plant200–400 fruits per tree (mature); one of the most regular bearers among Indian mangoes
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees); also self-fertile
Pollination NotesExcellent fruit set; polyembryonic seeds make it a preferred rootstock; used as pollinator for other varieties
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsTamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala; widely grown across South India
Market UsesTable fruit, juice, pulp; extends the mango season into late summer
Processing UsesPulp, juice, jam, squash, dried mango; good processing quality
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–100 per kg; commands good prices due to late-season availability

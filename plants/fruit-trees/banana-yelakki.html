Banana - Yelakki

Musa acuminata

Common Names: Yelakki Banana, Elaichi Banana, Cardamom Banana, Lady Finger Banana

Local Name: Yelakki Vazhai (Tamil/Kannada), Elaichi Kela (Hindi)

Family: Musaceae

Origin: South and Southeast Asia; Yelakki is a South Indian selection

Plant Type: Perennial herbaceous monocot; pseudostem-forming plant

Variety: Yelakki — small, sweet, aromatic banana; cardamom-like fragrance; AAA genome group

Average Lifespan: Perennial; individual plant fruits once; ratoon system for 5–10+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitHerbaceous perennial with pseudostem formed by overlapping leaf sheaths; clump-forming
Plant Size2–3 meters tall (shorter than most banana varieties)
LeavesLarge, oblong; 1.5–2 meters long; bright green; midrib prominent; tear easily in wind
FlowersPurple-red inflorescence (banana heart); female flowers develop into fruit; male flowers at tip
Flower CharacteristicsParthenocarpic; no pollination needed; inflorescence emerges from pseudostem center
FruitSmall, finger-shaped; 8–12 cm long; thin skin; sweet, aromatic flesh with cardamom-like fragrance
Fruit ColorGreen turning yellow when ripe; creamy white flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSeedless (parthenocarpic); occasional vestigial seeds
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; rhizome produces suckers for propagation
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical and subtropical; warm, humid conditions preferred
Temperature Range20–35°C; frost-sensitive; growth stops below 15°C
Rainfall Requirement1200–2500 mm; high moisture requirement; irrigation essential in dry areas
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained fertile loam; rich in organic matter; good water retention
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours); tolerates partial shade
Spacing2–3 meters between plants; compact variety suitable for smaller spaces
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; regular watering essential; avoid waterlogging
Water RequirementHigh; water stress reduces yield and fruit quality
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in very large pots (100+ liters); compact size makes it suitable
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly; high potassium and nitrogen; 3 major applications at planting, 3 months, and 6 months
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, wood ash, banana peel compost, green manure
Pesticide FrequencyMonthly preventive; treat immediately if pests appear
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for aphids and thrips; pseudostem injection for weevil; Trichoderma for Fusarium
Mulching PracticeHeavy organic mulch (dry banana leaves, straw) to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemDesuckering — keep 1 mother plant + 1 follower sucker; remove excess suckers
Pruning TimeRemove dry leaves regularly; cut pseudostem after harvest
How to PruneCut pseudostem at ground level after harvest; remove dry leaves; desuck regularly
Common PestsBanana weevil (Cosmopolites sordidus), thrips, aphids, nematodes
Common DiseasesPanama wilt (Fusarium), Sigatoka leaf spot, bunchy top virus, banana streak virus
Disease Prevention & RemediesUse disease-free suckers; Trichoderma for Fusarium; copper fungicide for Sigatoka; remove infected plants
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering Months8–10 months after planting; year-round in tropics
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; 3–4 months after flowering
Days to Harvest90–120 days from flowering to harvest
Harvest IndicatorsFruit angles fill out; skin turns light green to yellow; fingers plump; harvest before fully ripe
Average Yield per Plant8–15 kg per bunch; 10–15 hands per bunch
Pollination TypeParthenocarpic (no pollination needed)
Pollination NotesSeedless; develops without pollination; male flower (banana heart) can be harvested as vegetable
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsParthenocarpic; no pollination needed for fruit development
Propagation by SeedsNot applicable; seedless variety
Propagation by CuttingsSword suckers (preferred) or water suckers from rhizome; plant at 30–45 cm depth
Propagation by GraftingTissue culture plantlets available for disease-free planting; preferred for commercial cultivation
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageHigh; requires consistent moisture; drip irrigation reduces water use
Soil Conservation PracticesDense planting reduces erosion; leaf litter improves soil organic matter
Organic Practices UsedVermicompost, green manure, mulching with banana leaves; jeevamrutha
Companion PlantsGinger, turmeric, taro as understory; leguminous cover crops
Biodiversity BenefitsBanana heart attracts birds and bats; supports orchard biodiversity
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala), Sri Lanka
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit; temple offerings; health food market
Processing UsesFresh consumption; banana chips; baby food; dried banana
Market Value (Approx.)₹60–120 per kg; premium variety; suckers ₹30–80 each
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismHighly prized in South Indian culture; used in religious offerings and festivals
Traditional UsesBanana flower used as vegetable; unripe banana for digestive health; leaves as plates in South Indian cuisine

🌿 Medicinal Uses

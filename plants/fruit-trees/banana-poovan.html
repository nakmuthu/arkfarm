Banana - Poovan

Musa acuminata

Common Names: Poovan Banana, Mysore Banana, Lady Finger Banana, Pisang Mas

Local Name: Poovan Vazhai (Tamil), Mysore Banana (Kannada/English)

Family: Musaceae

Origin: South India; AAB genome group

Plant Type: Perennial herbaceous monocot; pseudostem-forming plant

Variety: Poovan — medium-sized, sweet-tart banana; thin skin; widely grown in Tamil Nadu; AAB genome

Average Lifespan: Perennial; ratoon system for 5–10+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium-height herbaceous perennial with pseudostem; clump-forming
Plant Size3–4.5 meters tall
LeavesLarge, oblong; 2–2.5 meters long; bright green; midrib prominent
FlowersPurple-red inflorescence; female flowers develop into fruit; parthenocarpic
Flower CharacteristicsParthenocarpic; no pollination needed; banana heart edible as vegetable
FruitMedium-sized; 10–14 cm long; thin skin; sweet-tart flavor; slightly acidic; 12–16 per hand
Fruit ColorGreen turning yellow when ripe; creamy white flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSeedless (parthenocarpic)
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; rhizome produces suckers
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical and subtropical; warm, humid conditions preferred
Temperature Range20–35°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1200–2500 mm; high moisture requirement
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained fertile loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours)
Spacing2.5–3 meters between plants
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; regular watering essential
Water RequirementHigh; water stress reduces yield and fruit quality
Can it Grow in Pots?Possible in very large pots (100+ liters)
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly; high potassium and nitrogen; 3 major applications
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, wood ash, banana peel compost
Pesticide FrequencyMonthly preventive; treat immediately if pests appear
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for aphids; Trichoderma for Fusarium wilt
Mulching PracticeHeavy organic mulch to retain moisture
Training SystemDesuckering — keep 1 mother plant + 1 follower sucker
Pruning TimeRemove dry leaves regularly; cut pseudostem after harvest
How to PruneCut pseudostem at ground level after harvest; remove dry leaves; desuck regularly
Common PestsBanana weevil, thrips, aphids, nematodes
Common DiseasesPanama wilt (Fusarium), Sigatoka leaf spot, bunchy top virus
Disease Prevention & RemediesUse disease-free suckers; Trichoderma for Fusarium; copper fungicide for Sigatoka
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering Months10–12 months after planting; year-round in tropics
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; 3–4 months after flowering
Days to Harvest100–120 days from flowering to harvest
Harvest IndicatorsFruit angles fill out; skin turns light green; fingers plump
Average Yield per Plant15–25 kg per bunch; 12–16 hands per bunch
Pollination TypeParthenocarpic (no pollination needed)
Pollination NotesSeedless; develops without pollination
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsParthenocarpic; no pollination needed
Propagation by SeedsNot applicable; seedless variety
Propagation by CuttingsSword suckers from rhizome; preferred method
Propagation by GraftingTissue culture plantlets available for disease-free planting
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageHigh; drip irrigation reduces water use significantly
Soil Conservation PracticesDense planting reduces erosion; leaf litter improves soil organic matter
Organic Practices UsedVermicompost, green manure, mulching with banana leaves
Companion PlantsGinger, turmeric, taro as understory crops
Biodiversity BenefitsBanana heart attracts birds and bats; supports orchard biodiversity
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka)
Market UsesFresh fruit; temple offerings; health food market; widely available in South India
Processing UsesFresh consumption; banana chips; baby food; dried banana
Market Value (Approx.)₹25–50 per kg; suckers ₹20–50 each
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismWidely used in South Indian religious offerings and festivals; everyday fruit of Tamil Nadu
Traditional UsesBanana flower used as vegetable; unripe banana for digestive health; leaves as plates

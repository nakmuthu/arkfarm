Blueberry

Vaccinium corymbosum

Common Names: Blueberry, Highbush Blueberry, Northern Highbush Blueberry

Local Name: Neelabadari (Hindi)

Family: Ericaceae

Origin: Eastern North America

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous shrub

Variety: Highbush, Rabbiteye, and Southern Highbush types

Average Lifespan: 20–50 years with proper care

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitUpright to spreading deciduous shrub with multiple stems
Plant Size1–3 meters tall (highbush); compact varieties 0.5–1 m
LeavesSimple, alternate, elliptic, 3–8 cm long; bright green turning brilliant red in autumn
FlowersWhite to pale pink, bell-shaped (urceolate), 6–12 mm long, in clusters
Flower CharacteristicsPendulous clusters of 5–10 flowers; mildly fragrant; bloom before full leaf-out
FruitRound berry, 5–16 mm diameter; sweet with slight tartness; waxy bloom on skin
Fruit ColorDeep blue to blue-black with powdery white bloom
Seed CharacteristicsNumerous tiny seeds inside berry; barely noticeable when eating
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; no root hairs — relies on mycorrhizal fungi
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTemperate; requires winter chill; Southern Highbush for warmer areas
Temperature Range16–25°C ideal; requires 400–1000 chill hours (highbush)
Rainfall Requirement750–1250 mm annually; consistent moisture needed
Soil TypeAcidic, well-drained, sandy loam rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference4.5–5.5 (strongly acidic — critical requirement)
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily); tolerates light afternoon shade
Spacing1–1.5 meters between plants; 2.5–3 meters between rows
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; consistent moisture essential
Water RequirementModerate to high; 25–50 mm per week; sensitive to drought
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent in 20–40 liter pots with acidic potting mix
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleEarly spring; use acid-forming fertilizers; avoid lime
Recommended Organic FertilizersCottonseed meal, pine needle compost, sulfur, acidic compost
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor regularly; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, Bt for caterpillars, bird netting for fruit protection
Mulching PracticePine bark, pine needles, or sawdust mulch (5–10 cm) to maintain acidity
Training SystemOpen vase shape; remove oldest canes annually
Pruning TimeLate winter while dormant (January–February)
How to PruneRemove oldest canes (6+ years); thin weak growth; tip-prune for larger berries
Common PestsBlueberry maggot, spotted wing drosophila, birds, Japanese beetle
Common DiseasesMummy berry, botrytis blight, phytophthora root rot, anthracnose
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, mulching, remove mummified fruit, fungicide sprays if needed
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May (depending on variety and region)
Fruiting SeasonJune–August; harvest over 2–5 weeks
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsBerries turn fully blue, detach easily; wait 2–3 days after turning blue for best sweetness
Average Yield per Plant2–7 kg per bush per season (mature)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination greatly improves yield
Pollination NotesPlant 2+ varieties for best fruit set; bumblebees are most effective pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsPartially self-fertile; cross-pollination with different variety increases yield 50–100%
Propagation by SeedsPossible but very slow; cold stratification needed; not true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsSoftwood cuttings in summer; hardwood cuttings in winter; root in 6–8 weeks
Propagation by GraftingNot commonly grafted; tissue culture used for commercial propagation
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation and mulching
Soil Conservation PracticesDense root system holds soil; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedPine mulching, composting, biological pest control, cover cropping
Companion PlantsAzaleas, rhododendrons (similar acid needs), clover, ferns
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers support native bees; fruit feeds birds and wildlife; autumn foliage adds beauty
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsUSA (Michigan, Oregon, New Jersey), Chile, Peru, Canada, Spain
Market UsesFresh fruit, frozen berries, dried berries
Processing UsesJam, juice, wine, muffins, pancakes, smoothies, supplements
Market Value (Approx.)₹800–2000 per kg (India); $3–8 per pint (USA)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNative American "star berry" — star-shaped calyx; symbol of health and vitality
Traditional UsesNative Americans used berries fresh, dried, and in pemmican; leaf tea for blood sugar support
🍈 Nutritional Information
Calories57 kcal per 100g
Dietary Fiber2.4 g per 100g
Vitamin C9.7 mg per 100g
Vitamin A54 IU per 100g
Iron0.28 mg per 100g
Potassium77 mg per 100g
AntioxidantsExtremely high in anthocyanins, pterostilbene, resveratrol, quercetin
Other Key NutrientsVitamin K, manganese, vitamin E, vitamin B6
🌿 Medicinal Uses

Digestive HealthFiber supports gut health; anthocyanins may benefit gut microbiome

Jamun Thai King

Syzygium cumini 'Thai King'

Common Names: Thai King Jamun, Thai Java Plum, King Jamun

Local Name: Thai King Naval (Tamil), Thai King Jamun (Hindi)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Thailand; selected cultivar of Syzygium cumini

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Thai King — large fruit (3–5 cm), sweeter than common jamun, less astringent

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge evergreen tree with dense, rounded canopy
Plant Size10–18 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesOpposite, elliptic-oblong, 10–18 cm long; leathery, glossy dark green
FlowersSmall, fragrant, creamy-white, in terminal and axillary clusters
Flower CharacteristicsBrush-like with numerous stamens; fragrant; attract bees and flies
FruitLarge oblong drupe, 3–5 cm long; significantly larger than common jamun; juicy, sweet
Fruit ColorDeep purple to black when ripe; thick, dark juice
Seed CharacteristicsSingle seed, proportionally smaller than common jamun; more pulp ratio
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; thrives in hot, humid conditions
Temperature Range25–38°C ideal; frost-sensitive; needs warm climate
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal drought
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained loamy to alluvial soil; tolerates clay
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; regular watering during fruiting for larger fruit
Water RequirementModerate; increase during fruiting season
Can it Grow in Pots?Young grafted trees in large pots (50+ liters); will limit size
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year; pre-flowering, during fruiting, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, bone meal, potash
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; monitor during fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, fruit fly traps, pheromone traps
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch to retain moisture during fruiting
Training SystemModified central leader; maintain accessible height
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (August–September)
How to PruneRemove dead wood; thin canopy; maintain shape for easy harvest
Common PestsFruit fly, leaf miner, white fly, bark borer
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, anthracnose, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, copper sprays, remove fallen fruit
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May
Fruiting SeasonJune–August; large fruit clusters
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black, large size, sweet with mild astringency
Average Yield per Plant60–200 kg per tree (mature); heavy bearer
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects
Pollination NotesBees and flies are primary pollinators; self-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; fragrant flowers attract diverse pollinators
Propagation by SeedsPolyembryonic seeds germinate in 1–2 weeks; seedlings variable
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood or veneer grafting on common jamun rootstock (preferred); fruits in 3–5 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; excellent shade tree
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; panchagavya, jeevamrutha
Companion PlantsShade-tolerant crops, turmeric, ginger
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit feeds birds, bats, and wildlife; large canopy provides habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsThailand, India, Southeast Asia
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit, juice, specialty markets
Processing UsesJuice, jam, wine, vinegar, dried fruit
Market Value (Approx.)₹80–200 per kg (premium variety); grafted plants ₹300–600
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismJamun is sacred in Hinduism and Buddhism; "Jambudvipa" (land of jamun) is an ancient name for India
Traditional UsesFruit, bark, and seeds used in Ayurveda; seed powder for blood sugar management; bark for oral health

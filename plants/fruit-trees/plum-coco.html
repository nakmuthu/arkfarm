Plum - Coco

Chrysobalanus icaco

Common Names: Coco Plum, Icaco, Paradise Plum, Fat Pork

Local Name: Icaco (Spanish), Coco Plum

Family: Chrysobalanaceae

Origin: Tropical Americas (coastal regions) and West Africa

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen shrub to small tree

Variety: Coastal and inland forms; fruit colour varies from white to pink to dark purple

Average Lifespan: 20–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitDense, spreading shrub or small tree; often multi-stemmed
Plant Size1–6 meters tall (coastal form shorter); inland form up to 10 meters
LeavesAlternate, broadly oval, 3–8 cm long, leathery, dark glossy green with rounded tip
FlowersSmall, white, 5-petaled, in terminal or axillary clusters
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant; attract bees and butterflies; bloom in flushes throughout warm months
FruitRound drupe, 2–5 cm diameter, thin skin, white cottony flesh surrounding a large ridged stone
Fruit ColorWhite, pink, red, or dark purple depending on variety
Seed CharacteristicsLarge, hard, ridged stone; edible kernel inside tastes like almond
Root SystemExtensive lateral roots; tolerates sandy and saline soils
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; salt-tolerant coastal species
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; frost-sensitive; tolerates brief cold to 0°C
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal flooding
Soil TypeSandy, well-drained; thrives in poor, sandy coastal soils
Soil pH Preference5.0–8.0 (very adaptable)
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing3–5 meters between plants
Irrigation MethodMinimal once established; supplemental watering in dry periods
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, compact form suitable for large containers (25+ liters)
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; light feeding sufficient
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, fish emulsion, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil if scale insects appear
Mulching PracticeLight organic mulch; leaf litter provides natural cover
Training SystemNatural form; can be trained as hedge or small standard tree
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting; shape as needed
How to PruneLight shaping; remove dead wood; can be hedged for landscaping
Common PestsScale insects, occasional caterpillars; generally pest-free
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, root rot in poorly drained soils; generally disease-resistant
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage; minimal intervention needed
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring through summer; year-round in tropics
Fruiting SeasonSummer to autumn; 60–90 days after flowering
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns from green to white/pink/purple; softens slightly; detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant10–30 kg per plant per year
Pollination TypeInsect pollination; self-fertile
Pollination NotesBees and butterflies are primary pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; insect pollination improves fruit set
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 4–8 weeks; crack hard shell for faster germination
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings root in 6–8 weeks with rooting hormone
Propagation by GraftingNot commonly grafted; seedlings and cuttings are standard methods
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; naturally adapted to coastal and sandy environments
Soil Conservation PracticesExcellent for coastal erosion control; stabilises sand dunes
Organic Practices UsedNaturally low-maintenance; minimal inputs required
Companion PlantsSea grape, coconut palm, other coastal species
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit eaten by birds and wildlife; flowers support pollinators; important coastal habitat plant
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsCaribbean, Central America, South Florida, West Africa, Brazil
Market UsesFresh fruit, preserves, landscaping, coastal restoration
Processing UsesJams, jellies, preserves; kernel oil extraction
Market Value (Approx.)₹80–200 per kg (niche tropical fruit market)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismImportant food source for indigenous Caribbean peoples; symbol of coastal abundance
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh; kernel oil used for cooking and cosmetics; bark used in folk medicine as astringent

Sweet Luvi

Flacourtia inermis

Common Names: Sweet Luvi, Lovi-Lovi, Batoko Plum, Lobi-Lobi

Local Name: Lovi-Lovi (Indonesian/Malay), Thamatta (Sinhala)

Family: Salicaceae (formerly Flacourtiaceae)

Origin: Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Indonesia, Philippines)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen to semi-deciduous tree

Variety: Species type — thornless variety (inermis = unarmed) with sweet-tart, cherry-like fruit

Average Lifespan: 20–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall to medium tree with dense, rounded canopy; thornless (unlike related species)
Plant Size5–10 meters tall; canopy spread 4–6 meters
LeavesAlternate, simple, ovate to elliptic, 5–15 cm, glossy green; new growth often reddish-bronze
FlowersSmall, greenish-yellow, inconspicuous; borne in short axillary racemes
Flower CharacteristicsDioecious or polygamous; small and not showy; wind and insect pollinated
FruitRound, 1.5–2.5 cm diameter; cherry-like; thin skin; juicy, sweet-tart flesh with 6–10 small seeds
Fruit ColorGreen turning dark red to deep purple-black when ripe
Seed Characteristics6–10 small, flat, hard seeds per fruit; embedded in juicy flesh
Root SystemModerately deep with spreading lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; warm and humid; frost-intolerant
Temperature Range22–35°C ideal; damaged below 5°C
Rainfall Requirement1200–2500 mm annually
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy to sandy loam; adaptable to various soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade (5+ hours daily)
Spacing4–6 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodRegular watering; drip or basin irrigation
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture preferred
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (30+ liters); compact size suits container growing
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; light feeding sufficient
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil if needed; generally pest-free
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; leaf litter recycling
Training SystemNatural form; minimal training needed
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting; light annual pruning
How to PruneRemove dead wood; shape as desired; thin interior for light
Common PestsGenerally pest-free; occasional fruit fly, birds
Common DiseasesLeaf spot (minor); generally disease-resistant
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; rarely needs treatment
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in tropics; main flush during rainy season
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; main harvest during wet season
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep red to purple-black, softens slightly, sweet aroma develops
Average Yield per Plant20–50 kg per tree per year (mature)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination and self-pollination
Pollination NotesSome trees are self-fertile; planting multiple trees improves fruit set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsDioecious or polygamous; wind and insect pollinated
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; seedlings fruit in 3–5 years
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings possible; air layering effective
Propagation by GraftingCleft grafting on seedling rootstock; allows sex selection for fruiting trees

Water UsageModerate; naturally sustained by tropical rainfall

Organic Practices UsedNaturally low-maintenance; minimal inputs needed
Companion PlantsBanana, coconut, other tropical fruit trees in mixed orchards
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit eaten by birds and wildlife; provides habitat

Cherry of the Rio Grande

Eugenia involucrata

Common Names: Cherry of the Rio Grande, Cerejeira-do-Rio-Grande, Rio Grande Cherry

Local Name: Cerejeira-do-Rio-Grande (Portuguese)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Southern Brazil (Rio Grande do Sul, Paraná, Santa Catarina)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen to semi-deciduous tree

Variety: Species type — deep purple cherry-like fruit with excellent flavour

Average Lifespan: 30–50 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitUpright, columnar to spreading evergreen tree; attractive smooth bark
Plant Size5–10 meters tall; canopy 3–6 meters wide
LeavesOpposite, elliptic-lanceolate, 5–10 cm long, glossy dark green; semi-deciduous in cooler climates
FlowersWhite, 4-petaled, 1.5–2 cm across with prominent stamens; borne in clusters along branches
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant; attract bees; bloom profusely in spring; flowers emerge from old wood
FruitOval to oblong berry, 2–3 cm long; smooth thin skin, juicy sweet flesh reminiscent of dark cherry
Fruit ColorGreen turning deep purple to almost black when ripe
Seed Characteristics1–2 seeds per fruit; medium-sized, round
Root SystemModerately deep taproot with lateral roots; non-invasive
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical to warm temperate; more cold-tolerant than most tropical Eugenia
Temperature Range15–30°C ideal; tolerates brief frost to −5°C (mature trees)
Rainfall Requirement800–1800 mm annually; prefers consistent moisture
Soil TypeRich, well-drained loamy soil; prefers fertile conditions
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing4–6 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodRegular watering; drip irrigation during dry periods
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture for best fruiting
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (30+ liters); slow growth suits container culture
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; spring, summer, and early autumn
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, well-rotted manure, bone meal, acidifying fertilizers
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; generally pest-free
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional scale; fruit fly traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch to retain moisture and enrich soil
Training SystemCentral leader or natural form; minimal training needed
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; remove dead wood any time
How to PruneLight pruning for shape; remove crossing and dead branches; thin interior for air flow
Common PestsFruit fly, birds, occasional scale insects
Common DiseasesGenerally disease-resistant; occasional anthracnose in humid conditions
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; avoid overhead watering; copper-based fungicide if needed
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring (September–November in Southern Hemisphere)
Fruiting SeasonLate spring to early summer; 30–45 days after flowering
Days to Harvest30–45 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black; softens; sweet cherry aroma; detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant10–30 kg per tree per year (mature)
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; insect-pollinated
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; cross-pollination may improve yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bees attracted to fragrant white flowers
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 4–8 weeks; seedlings fruit in 3–5 years
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings with rooting hormone; moderate success
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on Eugenia rootstock for selected cultivars
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; adapted to subtropical rainfall
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots stabilize soil; leaf litter enriches topsoil
Organic Practices UsedLow-input species; minimal pest and disease management needed
Companion PlantsOther Myrtaceae, subtropical fruit trees, ground covers
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit attracts birds and wildlife; flowers support pollinators; native to Atlantic Forest biome

Commercial Production RegionsSouthern Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay; niche market in USA, Australia
Market UsesFresh fruit, ornamental tree, rare fruit collections
Processing UsesJams, jellies, juice, wine, ice cream flavouring

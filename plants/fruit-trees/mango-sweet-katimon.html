Mango Sweet Katimon

Mangifera indica 'Sweet Katimon'

Common Names: Sweet Katimon, Katimon Mango, Katimon Manis

Local Name: Katimon Manis (Malay), กะทิมน (Thai)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: Thailand / Malaysia border region

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Sweet Katimon — medium-sized, aromatic, honey-sweet Thai-Malaysian mango

Average Lifespan: 40–80+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium-sized tree with moderately dense, rounded canopy
Plant Size8–12 meters tall; canopy spread 6–9 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 12–25 cm long; young leaves pinkish-bronze turning dark green
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white to pinkish in terminal panicles (15–30 cm long)
Flower CharacteristicsMixed male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; lightly fragrant
FruitOval to oblong, 250–400 g; thin skin with creamy, fiberless, intensely sweet pulp
Fruit ColorGreen turning golden-yellow with occasional orange blush when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsMedium monoembryonic seed; thin, papery husk
Root SystemDeep taproot with spreading lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; thrives in hot, humid lowlands with seasonal dry period
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 40°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; dry spell of 6–8 weeks before flowering
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy or sandy loam soil rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or micro-sprinkler irrigation; reduce watering before flowering
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees can be grown in large pots (50+ liters); will limit fruiting
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (20–40 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, vermicompost
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray, fruit fly traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center system for good light penetration
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (July–August)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; tip-prune for compact growth
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealybug, scale insects
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial canker, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper fungicides at flowering, proper spacing, remove fallen debris, fruit bagging
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–February
Fruiting SeasonApril–July
Days to Harvest100–120 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsSkin turns golden, fruit gives slightly to pressure, strong sweet aroma
Average Yield per Plant80–150 fruits per tree (mature); relatively consistent bearer
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees)
Pollination NotesLow fruit set rate (1–3%); warm dry weather during bloom improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; flies and small bees are primary pollinators
Propagation by SeedsMonoembryonic; seedlings variable; used for rootstock
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible but not common commercially
Propagation by GraftingVeneer or cleft grafting on polyembryonic rootstock (preferred method)
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate once established; deep roots access groundwater
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep root system prevents erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedPanchagavya, jeevamrutha, mulching with coconut fronds
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, banana (as windbreak when young), turmeric
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides habitat for birds, bats, and insects; large canopy offers shade
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsSouthern Thailand, Peninsular Malaysia, Indonesia
Market UsesPremium table fruit, fresh consumption, dessert mango
Processing UsesMango puree, dried mango, mango juice, frozen slices
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–400 per kg; premium pricing in Southeast Asian markets
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismMango is the national fruit of India and revered across Southeast Asia as a symbol of prosperity
Traditional UsesPrized in Thai and Malay cuisine; served fresh as dessert; leaves used in traditional ceremonies

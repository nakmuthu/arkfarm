White Jamun

Syzygium cumini (white variety)

Common Names: White Jamun, Safed Jamun, White Java Plum

Local Name: Vellai Naval (Tamil), Safed Jamun (Hindi)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Indian subcontinent; rare natural variant of Syzygium cumini

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: White — pale white to pinkish fruit, sweeter and less astringent than purple

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge evergreen tree with dense, spreading canopy
Plant Size10–18 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesOpposite, elliptic-oblong, 8–15 cm long; leathery, glossy dark green (identical to common jamun)
FlowersSmall, fragrant, white, in clusters at branch tips
Flower CharacteristicsBrush-like with numerous stamens; fragrant; attract bees
FruitOblong drupe, 2–3 cm long; pale white to pinkish flesh; sweeter, less astringent
Fruit ColorWhite to pale pinkish-green when ripe; does not stain like purple variety
Seed CharacteristicsSingle seed; similar to common jamun
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; same requirements as common jamun
Temperature Range25–38°C ideal; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement800–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal drought
Soil TypeAdaptable; prefers deep alluvial or loamy soil
Soil pH Preference5.5–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing8–12 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin irrigation; minimal once established
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young grafted trees in large pots (50+ liters); not ideal long-term
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; pre-monsoon and post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for leaf miner; fruit fly traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base
Training SystemModified central leader; natural form
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (August–September)
How to PruneRemove dead and crossing branches; thin canopy for light
Common PestsLeaf miner, fruit fly, white fly, bark borer
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, anthracnose, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood sanitation, copper fungicides, remove infected parts
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May
Fruiting SeasonJune–August
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns pale white to pinkish, softens, sweet flavor develops
Average Yield per Plant40–120 kg per tree (mature)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; self-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; fragrant flowers attract bees and flies
Propagation by SeedsSeeds may not produce true white-fruited plants; grafting preferred
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible but slow
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood grafting on common jamun rootstock (essential for true-to-type); fruits in 4–5 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; very drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; excellent shade tree
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; naturally hardy
Companion PlantsShade-tolerant crops, turmeric, ginger
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit feeds birds and wildlife; large canopy provides habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (rare variety), Southeast Asia
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit, novelty/specialty markets
Processing UsesFresh consumption preferred; juice, jam
Market Value (Approx.)₹100–300 per kg (rare variety commands premium); grafted plants ₹400–800
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismRare white variety considered auspicious; prized by collectors and fruit enthusiasts
Traditional UsesSame medicinal properties as common jamun; seeds used in Ayurveda for diabetes management

Jaboticaba / Brazilian Grape

Plinia cauliflora

Common Names: Jaboticaba, Jabuticaba, Brazilian Grape Tree

Local Name: Jabuticaba (Portuguese/Brazilian)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Southern Brazil (Minas Gerais, São Paulo, Goiás)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Sabará (most common), Paulista, Rajada

Average Lifespan: 100+ years; extremely long-lived

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSlow-growing evergreen tree with smooth, mottled bark that peels in patches
Plant Size5–12 meters tall; compact rounded canopy
LeavesOpposite, lanceolate, 2–7 cm long, salmon-pink when young turning glossy dark green
FlowersSmall, white, 4-petaled, borne directly on trunk and major branches (cauliflorous)
Flower CharacteristicsCauliflorous — flowers emerge directly from bark; numerous stamens; fragrant
FruitRound berry, 1.5–4 cm diameter, thick dark skin with translucent gelatinous pulp
Fruit ColorDark purple to nearly black; resembles large grapes
Seed Characteristics1–4 flat seeds per fruit; lose viability quickly
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; sensitive to root disturbance
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical to tropical; tolerates mild frost when mature
Temperature Range18–32°C ideal; mature trees tolerate brief frost to -3°C
Rainfall Requirement1000–2000 mm annually; consistent moisture needed
Soil TypeRich, acidic, well-drained soil with high organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.0–6.5 (prefers acidic)
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing5–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; consistent moisture essential
Water RequirementModerate to high; does not tolerate drought
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes — excellent container tree; slow growth makes it ideal for large pots (40+ liters)
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly during growing season with acidic fertilizer
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, coffee grounds, pine bark mulch, fish emulsion
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; generally pest-free
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for scale insects if needed
Mulching PracticeAcidic mulch (pine needles, coffee grounds) around base
Training SystemNatural form; minimal training — beautiful ornamental shape
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; light pruning only
How to PruneMinimal pruning; remove dead branches and suckers only
Common PestsScale insects, birds, fruit flies (minimal)
Common DiseasesRust, anthracnose (rare); root rot in waterlogged soil
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, avoid overwatering, maintain acidic soil pH
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMultiple times per year in tropics; spring and autumn in subtropics
Fruiting SeasonCan fruit 2–5 times per year in ideal conditions
Days to Harvest20–30 days from flowering (remarkably fast)
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns dark purple-black, slightly soft; falls easily when ripe
Average Yield per Plant50–100 kg per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination; insect-assisted
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile; bees improve fruit set; trunk-borne flowers are easily accessible
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; cauliflorous flowers easily accessed by small insects
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; seedlings take 8–15 years to fruit
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible but slow; semi-hardwood cuttings with rooting hormone
Propagation by GraftingGrafting onto seedling rootstock reduces fruiting time to 3–5 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with mulching
Soil Conservation PracticesDense canopy and leaf litter protect soil
Organic Practices UsedNaturally pest-resistant; minimal chemical inputs needed
Companion PlantsAzaleas, blueberries, other acid-loving plants
Biodiversity BenefitsAttracts birds, bees; beautiful ornamental tree for gardens
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBrazil (Minas Gerais, São Paulo), Bolivia, Paraguay, Argentina
Market UsesFresh fruit, wine, liqueur, jelly
Processing UsesWine, liqueur, jam, jelly, vinegar, dried fruit
Market Value (Approx.)R$15–40 per kg (Brazil); premium pricing due to short shelf life
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNational treasure of Brazil; symbol of Brazilian biodiversity and gastronomy
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh; bark used in traditional medicine for diarrhoea and asthma; wine-making tradition

Apple Rose

Syzygium jambos

Common Names: Rose Apple, Malabar Plum, Plum Rose, Jambos

Local Name: Panneer Apple (Tamil), Gulab Jamun (Hindi), Champakka (Malayalam)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Southeast Asia (Malay Peninsula); widely naturalized in tropics

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Rose Apple — round, pale yellow fruit with distinctive rose fragrance and crisp flesh

Average Lifespan: 40–80+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large tree with dense, spreading, dome-shaped canopy
Plant Size7–12 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesOpposite, lanceolate, 12–25 cm long, 3–5 cm wide; glossy dark green, leathery; new leaves pinkish
FlowersShowy, 5–8 cm across, with numerous long white to greenish-white stamens
Flower CharacteristicsPompom-like clusters of stamens; sweetly fragrant; borne in terminal clusters of 2–8
FruitRound to oval, 3–5 cm diameter; crisp, white, hollow center with 1–2 loose seeds; strong rose aroma
Fruit ColorPale yellow to creamy-white with pink blush when ripe
Seed Characteristics1–2 round, brown seeds (1–1.5 cm); polyembryonic; loose inside hollow fruit cavity
Root SystemModerate taproot with spreading lateral roots; can be somewhat invasive
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; tolerates mild cool periods
Temperature Range20–30°C ideal; tolerates brief periods down to 5°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal dry periods
Soil TypeAdaptable; prefers moist, well-drained loamy soil; tolerates sandy and clay soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade (6+ hours daily)
Spacing6–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; regular watering during dry season
Water RequirementModerate; tolerates short dry spells once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large pots (40+ liters); makes an attractive ornamental container tree
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; pre-flowering and post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (15–30 kg/tree/year), compost, bone meal, vermicompost
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional aphids or scale; fruit fly traps during fruiting
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; leaf litter recycling
Training SystemCentral leader or open center; minimal training needed
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting; shape as needed
How to PruneRemove dead and crossing branches; thin canopy for air circulation; manage spread
Common PestsFruit fly, aphids, scale insects, Caribbean fruit fly
Common DiseasesRust (Puccinia psidii), anthracnose, leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, copper fungicides, remove infected leaves, avoid overhead watering
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May (main season); sporadic flowering possible year-round
Fruiting SeasonMay–August
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns pale yellow, strong rose fragrance, slight give when pressed
Average Yield per Plant200–400 fruits per tree (mature); consistent bearer
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; cross-pollination by bees improves yield
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; showy stamens attract many insect visitors
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile but cross-pollination improves fruit set and quality
Propagation by SeedsPolyembryonic seeds; germinate in 2–6 weeks; seedlings come true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering successful; semi-hardwood cuttings with rooting hormone
Propagation by GraftingApproach grafting possible; not commonly practiced as seeds are reliable
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDense canopy and root system prevent erosion; good shade tree
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; naturally low-maintenance tree
Companion PlantsCoconut, banana, spice plants (traditional homestead planting)
Biodiversity BenefitsAttracts bees, butterflies, and birds; provides shade and habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsSoutheast Asia, India, Caribbean, Central America, Pacific Islands
Market UsesFresh fruit, ornamental tree, shade tree, windbreak
Processing UsesRose water flavoring, jam, jelly, candied fruit, wine
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–120 per kg; primarily local market fruit
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismAssociated with fragrance and beauty; the Hindi name Gulab Jamun reflects its rose-like aroma
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh; flowers and bark used in traditional medicine; wood for fuel and small construction

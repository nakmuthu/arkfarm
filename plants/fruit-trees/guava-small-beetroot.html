Guava - Small Beetroot

Psidium guajava (beetroot variety)

Common Names: Beetroot Guava, Red Guava, Pink Guava, Lal Amrood

Local Name: Sivappu Koiya (Tamil), Lal Amrood (Hindi)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Central America; beetroot variety selected in India

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Beetroot — small to medium fruit with deep red-pink flesh, intense flavour

Average Lifespan: 25–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitCompact to medium-sized evergreen tree; productive and hardy
Plant Size3–6 meters tall; canopy 3–5 meters wide
LeavesOpposite, oblong-elliptic, 8–12 cm long, dull green with prominent veins; aromatic
FlowersWhite, 4–5 petaled, 2–3 cm across with numerous stamens
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant; attract bees; bloom on new growth
FruitSmall to medium round fruit, 80–150g; thin skin, deep red-pink flesh with intense sweet-tart guava flavour
Fruit ColorGreen-yellow skin; deep beetroot-red to dark pink flesh
Seed CharacteristicsNumerous small hard seeds in central cavity; edible
Root SystemShallow to moderately deep fibrous root system
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; tolerates brief frost to −2°C
Rainfall Requirement800–2000 mm annually; drought-tolerant once established
Soil TypeAdaptable; well-drained loamy soil preferred; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference5.0–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing4–5 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; regular during fruiting
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant but fruits better with regular water
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in 25–30 liter pots; compact size suits containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleEvery 2–3 months during growing season
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor regularly; fruit bagging recommended
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, fruit fly traps, pheromone traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemOpen center system; maintain good air circulation
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; tip-prune to induce new fruiting shoots
How to PruneTip-prune for branching; remove water sprouts and dead wood; thin fruit for quality
Common PestsFruit fly, guava moth, mealybug, bark-eating caterpillar
Common DiseasesGuava wilt, anthracnose, fruit rot, algal leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, resistant rootstock, copper fungicide, remove infected parts
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in tropics; main flush in rainy season
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; peak in winter (India) for best quality
Days to Harvest90–120 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsSkin turns yellowish-green; fruit softens slightly; strong guava aroma
Average Yield per Plant20–50 kg per tree per year
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; insect-pollinated
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; self-fertile
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bees and wind assist pollination
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; seedlings may not be true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering preferred; stem cuttings with rooting hormone
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on seedling rootstock for true-to-type plants
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesRoot system holds soil; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedFruit bagging, organic fertilization, biological pest control
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, marigolds, herbs
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers support pollinators; fruit feeds birds
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Bihar), Southeast Asia, Brazil
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, processing
Processing UsesJuice, nectar, jam, jelly, guava cheese, dried guava; red flesh preferred for processing
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–120 per kg; red-fleshed varieties command premium for processing
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismGuava is called "apple of the tropics"; red varieties symbolize health and vitality
Traditional UsesGuava leaf tea widely used in Ayurveda and folk medicine; fruit eaten fresh as common street food
🍈 Nutritional Information
Calories68 kcal per 100g
Dietary Fiber5.4 g per 100g
Vitamin C228 mg per 100g
Vitamin A624 IU per 100g
Iron0.26 mg per 100g
Potassium417 mg per 100g
AntioxidantsVery high lycopene (red flesh — higher than tomatoes), beta-carotene, polyphenols
Other Key NutrientsFolate, manganese, copper, niacin
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepGuava leaf tea has mild calming properties
Immune SupportVery high Vitamin C content boosts immunity
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesLycopene and leaf extracts show anti-inflammatory effects
Heart HealthLycopene, potassium, and fiber support cardiovascular health
Digestive HealthHigh fiber aids digestion; leaf tea used for diarrhoea and blood sugar management

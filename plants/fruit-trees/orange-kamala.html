Kamala Orange

Citrus reticulata

Common Names: Kamala Orange, Mandarin Orange, Tangerine, Loose-skin Orange

Local Name: Kamala Pazham (Tamil), Santra (Hindi), Kamala (Kannada)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: Southeast Asia (China, India); widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen small tree or shrub

Variety: Kamala — Indian mandarin variety; loose, easy-peel skin; sweet-tart flavor

Average Lifespan: 20–50 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall evergreen tree with rounded, spreading canopy; slightly thorny branches
Plant Size3–6 meters tall; canopy spread 3–4 meters
LeavesAlternate, lanceolate to ovate; 5–10 cm long; glossy dark green; aromatic when crushed; winged petiole
FlowersWhite, 5-petaled; 1.5–2 cm across; intensely fragrant; borne singly or in small clusters
Flower CharacteristicsHighly fragrant; self-fertile; blooms in spring; attracts bees
FruitOblate to round; 5–8 cm diameter; loose, easy-peel skin; 8–12 segments; sweet-tart pulp
Fruit ColorOrange to deep orange when ripe; green in tropical climates even when ripe
Seed Characteristics5–15 seeds per fruit; oval, white; polyembryonic
Root SystemModerately deep fibrous root system; sensitive to waterlogging
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical to tropical; needs cool dry period for fruit coloring and sweetness
Temperature Range15–35°C; best fruit quality with cool winters (10–15°C nights)
Rainfall Requirement750–1500 mm; dry period needed for flowering and fruit quality
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing4–6 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; regular watering; reduce before harvest for sweetness
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture during fruit development
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in 40–60 liter pots; dwarf rootstocks preferred for container growing
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3–4 times per year; pre-flowering, fruit set, fruit development, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, bone meal, wood ash, citrus-specific compost
Pesticide FrequencyMonthly preventive; treat immediately if pests appear
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for citrus psylla, scale, and mites; copper fungicide for fungal diseases
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch 10–15 cm deep; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center or modified leader; keep canopy open for light penetration
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; remove dead, diseased, and crossing branches
How to PruneLight pruning to shape; remove water shoots; thin crowded branches for air circulation
Common PestsCitrus psylla, leaf miner, scale insects, mealybugs, fruit fly
Common DiseasesCitrus canker, greening (HLB), gummosis, root rot, powdery mildew
Disease Prevention & RemediesControl psylla (HLB vector); copper fungicide for canker; good drainage; remove infected material
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April (India); triggered by cool dry period
Fruiting SeasonNovember–February; fruits ripen in 7–9 months after flowering
Days to Harvest210–270 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns orange; easy to peel; sweet aroma; slight give when pressed
Average Yield per Plant50–150 fruits per tree (mature); 20–60 kg
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; also cross-pollinated by bees
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile; single tree produces fruit; bee pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bee pollination improves fruit set and size
Propagation by SeedsPolyembryonic seeds; most seedlings are true-to-type; germinate in 2–4 weeks
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering more reliable
Propagation by GraftingT-budding or cleft grafting on Rangpur lime or rough lemon rootstock; fruits in 3–4 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; drip irrigation reduces water use by 40–50%
Soil Conservation PracticesMulching and cover crops reduce erosion; organic matter improves soil health
Organic Practices UsedVermicompost, neem cake, jeevamrutha; integrated pest management
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, marigold (pest deterrent), basil
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract bees; fruit feeds birds; supports orchard ecosystem
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Nagpur, Coorg, Darjeeling), China, Spain, USA
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, essential oil, zest for cooking
Processing UsesJuice, marmalade, essential oil (from peel), dried peel, candied peel
Market Value (Approx.)₹30–80 per kg; grafted plants ₹150–400
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismSymbol of prosperity and good luck in Chinese New Year; offered in Hindu worship
Traditional UsesPeel used in Ayurveda for digestive health; essential oil in aromatherapy; fruit in traditional medicine

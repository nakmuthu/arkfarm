Mango - Malkova

Mangifera indica 'Malgova'

Common Names: Malgova, Malkova, Malgoa

Local Name: Malgova (Tamil), Malgoa (Malayalam)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: South India (Tamil Nadu, Karnataka)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Malgova — large, round, premium dessert mango

Average Lifespan: 40–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge spreading tree with dense, rounded canopy
Plant Size10–15 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 15–30 cm long; new leaves reddish-copper turning dark green
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white in large terminal panicles (20–40 cm long)
Flower CharacteristicsBoth male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; mildly fragrant
FruitLarge, round to oblique, 350–600 g; thick skin with sweet, fiberless pulp
Fruit ColorGreen turning yellowish-green with red blush when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsLarge monoembryonic seed; oblong, fibrous husk
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; needs dry period for flowering
Temperature Range24–30°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement750–2500 mm annually; dry spell needed before flowering
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained alluvial or laterite soil
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; reduce watering during flowering
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees can be grown in large pots (50+ liters); will limit size
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (25–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, vermicompost
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center or modified leader system
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (July–August)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; tip-prune for compact growth
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealybug, leaf webber
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial canker, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper-based fungicides, proper spacing for air circulation, remove fallen debris
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–February (South India)
Fruiting SeasonApril–July
Days to Harvest100–130 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit shoulders fill out, skin color lightens, slight aroma develops
Average Yield per Plant100–200 fruits per tree (mature); alternate bearing common
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees)
Pollination NotesOnly 1–2% of flowers set fruit; wind and insects both contribute
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsPrimarily insect-pollinated; houseflies are major pollinators
Propagation by SeedsPossible but does not produce true-to-type; used for rootstock
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible but not common
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting or softwood grafting on polyembryonic rootstock (preferred method)
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate once established; deep roots access groundwater
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep root system prevents erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedPanchagavya, jeevamrutha, mulching with coconut fronds
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, turmeric, ginger (as intercrops when young)
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides habitat for birds, bats, and insects; large canopy offers shade
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsTamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala
Market UsesPremium table fruit, juice, pulp
Processing UsesPulp, juice, mango pickle, dried mango
Market Value (Approx.)₹100–300 per kg (premium variety)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismMango is the national fruit of India; symbol of love and prosperity
Traditional UsesLeaves used in Hindu ceremonies; wood for sacred fires; fruit in festivals
🍈 Nutritional Information
Calories60 kcal per 100g
Dietary Fiber1.6 g per 100g
Vitamin C36 mg per 100g
Vitamin A1082 IU per 100g
Iron0.16 mg per 100g
Potassium168 mg per 100g
AntioxidantsRich in beta-carotene, mangiferin, quercetin
Other Key NutrientsFolate, vitamin B6, copper, vitamin E
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepNot a primary use for mango
Immune SupportHigh vitamin C and A content boosts immunity
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesMangiferin compound shows anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects
Heart HealthFiber and potassium support cardiovascular health
Digestive HealthEnzymes like amylase aid digestion; fiber promotes gut health

Strawberry

Fragaria × ananassa

Common Names: Strawberry, Garden Strawberry

Local Name: Strawberry (Tamil), Jharberi (Hindi)

Family: Rosaceae

Origin: Hybrid origin (France, 18th century) — cross of F. virginiana and F. chiloensis

Plant Type: Perennial herbaceous plant

Variety: June-bearing, everbearing, and day-neutral types

Average Lifespan: 3–5 years (productive); replant every 3 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLow-growing, rosette-forming herbaceous perennial with runners (stolons)
Plant Size15–30 cm tall; spreads via runners up to 1 meter
LeavesTrifoliate (3 leaflets), serrated margins, bright green, hairy underneath
FlowersWhite, 5-petaled, 2–3 cm diameter with yellow center
Flower CharacteristicsBorne in clusters on erect stalks; both male and female parts present
FruitAccessory fruit (enlarged receptacle), 2–5 cm long; true fruits are the tiny achenes on surface
Fruit ColorBright red when ripe; white to green when unripe
Seed CharacteristicsTiny achenes (200+ per fruit) embedded on the surface of the receptacle
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; most roots in top 15 cm of soil
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTemperate to subtropical; cool winters and mild summers ideal
Temperature Range15–25°C ideal for fruiting; tolerates light frost
Rainfall Requirement600–1000 mm annually; consistent moisture needed
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam rich in organic matter; raised beds preferred
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily)
Spacing30–45 cm between plants; 60–90 cm between rows
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation preferred; avoid wetting fruit and foliage
Water RequirementModerate; 25–30 mm per week; consistent moisture critical during fruiting
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent in hanging baskets, strawberry pots, or 5–10 liter containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleAt planting, after first harvest, and early autumn
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, fish emulsion, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyRegular monitoring; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, diatomaceous earth for slugs
Mulching PracticeStraw mulch (hence the name) or plastic mulch to keep fruit clean and retain moisture
Training SystemMatted row, hill system, or raised bed system
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; remove old leaves and excess runners
How to PruneRemove runners for larger fruit; allow some runners for new plants; trim old foliage
Common PestsSlugs, aphids, spider mites, strawberry bud weevil, birds
Common DiseasesBotrytis gray mold, powdery mildew, verticillium wilt, leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, straw mulch, crop rotation, resistant varieties, avoid overhead watering
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April (India); spring in temperate regions
Fruiting SeasonMarch–May (India); everbearing types fruit spring through autumn
Days to Harvest28–35 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns fully red, fragrant, and slightly soft; pick with cap attached
Average Yield per Plant300–500 g per plant per season
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination; insect pollination improves fruit quality
Pollination NotesBees essential for uniform fruit shape; poor pollination causes misshapen berries
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bees improve fruit size and shape significantly
Propagation by SeedsPossible but slow; seeds need light to germinate; 2–3 weeks
Propagation by CuttingsNot applicable; propagated by runners (stolons) — root daughter plants
Propagation by GraftingNot applicable; division and runner propagation are standard methods
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation and mulching
Soil Conservation PracticesGround cover habit prevents erosion; raised beds improve drainage
Organic Practices UsedStraw mulching, companion planting, biological pest control
Companion PlantsBorage (attracts pollinators), thyme, lettuce, spinach, beans
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract bees and beneficial insects; ground cover supports soil life
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsUSA (California, Florida), Spain, Turkey, Mexico, India (Mahabaleshwar, Ooty)
Market UsesFresh fruit, frozen berries, juice
Processing UsesJam, ice cream, yogurt, smoothies, desserts, dried fruit
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–400 per kg (India); varies by season
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismSymbol of spring, romance, and sweetness; associated with Venus in Roman mythology
Traditional UsesLeaves used in herbal teas; fruit in traditional desserts and preserves worldwide

🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepNot a primary medicinal use; magnesium content may support relaxation

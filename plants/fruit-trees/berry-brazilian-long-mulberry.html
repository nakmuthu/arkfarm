Brazilian Long Mulberry

Morus nigra (Brazilian variety)

Common Names: Brazilian Long Mulberry, Black Mulberry (Brazilian), Amora Preta

Local Name: Amora (Portuguese/Brazilian)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: Western Asia (Morus nigra); Brazilian selection for tropical adaptation

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous to semi-evergreen tree

Variety: Brazilian selection — elongated dark fruit, adapted to tropical climates

Average Lifespan: 50–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large tree with broad, rounded crown; semi-evergreen in tropics
Plant Size8–15 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesAlternate, cordate, 8–15 cm long; rough upper surface, hairy beneath; dark green
FlowersGreenish catkins, 2–4 cm long; inconspicuous
Flower CharacteristicsMonoecious; wind-pollinated; female catkins elongated
FruitElongated syncarp, 3–6 cm long; intensely sweet-tart, juicy, staining
Fruit ColorDeep purple-black when ripe; very dark juice
Seed CharacteristicsTiny seeds within drupelets; inconspicuous
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots; very long-lived
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; adapted to warm, humid conditions
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; less cold-tolerant than M. alba; frost-sensitive when young
Rainfall Requirement800–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal dry periods
Soil TypeDeep, fertile, well-drained loamy soil; adaptable
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing6–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; supplemental during dry season
Water RequirementModerate; increase during fruiting
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees in large pots (40+ liters); will limit size significantly
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; spring and post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, FYM, bone meal, wood ash
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional pest issues; bird netting for fruit
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemOpen center or natural form
Pruning TimeAfter harvest or during dormancy
How to PruneShape canopy; remove dead and crossing branches; maintain accessible height
Common PestsBirds, fruit flies, scale insects
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, root rot in waterlogged soils
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, air circulation, remove fallen fruit promptly
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsAugust–October (Southern Hemisphere); February–April (Northern)
Fruiting SeasonOctober–January (Brazil); April–June (Northern Hemisphere)
Days to Harvest35–50 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black, very soft, intensely sweet, stains fingers
Average Yield per Plant30–70 kg per tree (mature)
Pollination TypeWind pollination; self-fertile
Pollination NotesMonoecious; single tree produces abundant fruit
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile monoecious tree; wind-pollinated
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; seedlings variable
Propagation by CuttingsHardwood cuttings in winter; moderate to good success
Propagation by GraftingCleft grafting on Morus alba rootstock for true-to-type plants
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; excellent shade tree
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; natural leaf mulch
Companion PlantsCoffee, cacao (as shade tree), leguminous cover crops
Biodiversity BenefitsAbundant fruit supports birds and wildlife; large canopy provides habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBrazil, South America, tropical Asia, Mediterranean
Market UsesFresh fruit, frozen pulp, juice
Processing UsesJuice, jam, wine, ice cream, smoothies, dried fruit
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–400 per kg; R$15–30 per kg (Brazil)

SymbolismPopular backyard fruit tree in Brazil; symbol of tropical abundance
Traditional UsesFruit widely consumed fresh and in juices; leaves used in folk medicine for diabetes management

Star Fruit (Regular)

Averrhoa carambola

Common Names: Star Fruit, Carambola, Five Finger Fruit, Five Corner Fruit

Local Name: Thambratham Pazham (Tamil), Kamrakh (Hindi), Kamaranga (Bengali)

Family: Oxalidaceae

Origin: Southeast Asia (Sri Lanka, Moluccas, Indonesia)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Regular (tart) type — classic carambola with tangy-sweet flavor and higher oxalic acid content

Average Lifespan: 20–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall to medium evergreen tree with bushy, spreading canopy
Plant Size5–10 meters tall; canopy spread 4–7 meters
LeavesAlternate, pinnately compound, 5–11 leaflets; sensitive to light and touch (nyctinastic movement)
FlowersSmall, pink to lilac-purple, 6–10 mm; borne in clusters on branches and trunk
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant; heterostylous; blooms in multiple flushes year-round
FruitOblong, 6–13 cm, prominently 5-ridged; waxy, crisp; tart to sweet-tart flavor with higher acidity than sweet types
Fruit ColorGreen turning yellow to golden-orange when ripe; translucent flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSmall, flat, brown seeds (3–12 per fruit); viable for short period
Root SystemShallow, spreading fibrous root system
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; damaged below 2°C
Rainfall Requirement1000–2000 mm annually
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy to sandy loam; adaptable to various soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily)
Spacing5–7 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture during fruiting
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (30–50 liters); fruits well in pots
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleEvery 2–3 months; balanced NPK
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, bone meal, fish emulsion
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor monthly; treat as needed
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, pyrethrin
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (5–8 cm) around base
Training SystemOpen center; maintain compact form for easy harvest
Pruning TimeAfter harvest flush; light pruning year-round
How to PruneRemove dead wood; thin interior; manage height
Common PestsFruit fly, scale insects, aphids, leaf roller, stink bugs
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, leaf spot, root rot, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, copper fungicides, sanitation, proper spacing
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in tropics; multiple flushes
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; 2–3 main harvest periods
Days to Harvest60–75 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns yellow-orange, ridges become translucent, slight give when pressed
Average Yield per Plant100–250 kg per tree per year (mature); very prolific
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination and insect pollination
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile; bees improve fruit set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; heterostylous; insect-pollinated
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 1–2 weeks; seedlings fruit in 4–5 years
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering effective; fruits in 2–3 years
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on seedling rootstock; preferred for named varieties
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation
Soil Conservation PracticesMulching and cover crops; shallow roots benefit from ground cover
Organic Practices UsedComposting, biological pest control, mulching
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, marigolds, basil
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract bees and butterflies; fruit eaten by birds
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsMalaysia, Taiwan, India, Thailand, Brazil, USA (Florida, Hawaii)
Market UsesFresh fruit, cooking ingredient, garnish, juice
Processing UsesJuice, pickles, chutneys, dried fruit, wine, vinegar
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–150 per kg
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismStar shape symbolizes good fortune; popular in Asian cuisine and decoration
Traditional UsesTart fruit used in cooking, chutneys, and pickles; juice as natural cleaning agent; leaves in folk medicine

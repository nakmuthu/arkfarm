Pakistan Mulberry

Morus alba 'Pakistan'

Common Names: Pakistan Mulberry, King Mulberry, Pakistan Long Mulberry

Local Name: Pakistan Shahtoot (Hindi/Urdu)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: Pakistan; selected cultivar of Morus alba

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous tree

Variety: Pakistan — very long fruits, 7–10 cm; the largest fruiting mulberry

Average Lifespan: 40–60 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitVigorous deciduous tree with upright to spreading form
Plant Size8–12 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesLarge, alternate, heart-shaped to lobed, 10–20 cm long; dark green, slightly rough
FlowersGreenish catkins, 3–5 cm long; inconspicuous
Flower CharacteristicsMonoecious; wind-pollinated; long female catkins develop into elongated fruit
FruitExceptionally long syncarp, 7–10 cm (up to 12 cm); sweet, honey-like flavor
Fruit ColorRed to dark maroon-black when fully ripe
Seed CharacteristicsTiny seeds within drupelets; barely noticeable
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical to warm temperate; needs warm summers for best fruit
Temperature Range18–38°C ideal; tolerates brief frost; less cold-hardy than standard mulberry
Rainfall Requirement500–1500 mm annually; drought-tolerant once established
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy to sandy loam; adaptable
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily); needs heat for best fruit production
Spacing5–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; regular watering during fruiting
Water RequirementModerate; increase during fruiting for larger fruit
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large pots (40+ liters) with regular pruning to control size
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year; spring, during fruiting, and post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, FYM, bone meal, potash-rich organic fertilizers
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for scale; bird netting to protect fruit
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch to retain moisture during fruiting
Training SystemOpen center; maintain manageable height for easy harvest
Pruning TimeLate winter while dormant; light summer pruning
How to PruneKeep height manageable; remove dead wood; thin for light and air
Common PestsBirds (major), scale insects, mealybugs
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, powdery mildew, root rot in wet soils
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, air circulation, remove fallen fruit
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April
Fruiting SeasonApril–June; extended harvest period
Days to Harvest35–55 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns dark red to black, elongates fully, softens, and is very sweet
Average Yield per Plant25–60 kg per tree (mature); prolific bearer
Pollination TypeWind pollination; self-fertile (monoecious)
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile; single tree produces abundant fruit
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsMonoecious and self-fertile; wind carries pollen from male to female catkins
Propagation by SeedsNot recommended; seedlings won't be true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsHardwood cuttings (20–30 cm) in winter; good success rate
Propagation by GraftingCleft or whip grafting on Morus alba seedling rootstock (preferred method)
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; deciduous leaves enrich soil
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; natural leaf mulch
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, herbs, shade-tolerant vegetables
Biodiversity BenefitsAbundant fruit feeds birds and wildlife; provides shade
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsPakistan, India, Middle East, California (USA), Australia
Market UsesFresh fruit (premium), dried fruit, specialty markets
Processing UsesDried mulberries, jam, juice, wine, desserts
Market Value (Approx.)₹200–500 per kg (premium variety); grafted plants ₹300–800
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismPrized as the "King of Mulberries" for its exceptional fruit size
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh as a delicacy; dried for winter consumption; highly valued in Pakistani and Indian cuisine

Longan - All Season

Dimocarpus longan 'All Season'

Common Names: All Season Longan, Everbearing Longan, Four Season Longan

Local Name: Puvatti Pazham (Tamil), Longan (Hindi/English)

Family: Sapindaceae

Origin: Thailand/Vietnam (selected for year-round fruiting trait)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: All Season — everbearing variety that flowers and fruits multiple times per year without cold induction

Average Lifespan: 40–60+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium evergreen tree with spreading, moderately dense canopy
Plant Size6–10 meters tall; canopy spread 5–8 meters
LeavesAlternate, pinnately compound, 4–8 pairs of leaflets, dark green, leathery
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white, in terminal panicles; functionally male, female, and bisexual on same panicle
Flower CharacteristicsFlowers multiple times per year; does not require cold induction unlike standard longan
FruitRound, 2–2.5 cm diameter; thin tan shell; translucent sweet flesh; slightly smaller than Diamond River
Fruit ColorLight tan to brown shell; translucent white flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSingle round, shiny black seed; not edible
Root SystemModerately deep with spreading lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; does not require cold period for flowering (key advantage)
Temperature Range22–35°C ideal; tolerates tropical heat; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1200–2500 mm annually; consistent moisture preferred
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily)
Spacing6–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; consistent moisture year-round for continuous fruiting
Water RequirementModerate to high; continuous fruiting demands consistent water supply
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (50+ liters); will fruit in pots
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleEvery 2–3 months due to continuous fruiting; balanced NPK with extra potassium
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, fish emulsion, bone meal, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyRegular monitoring; treat as needed year-round
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, pyrethrin, Beauveria bassiana
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch maintained year-round
Training SystemOpen center; manage size for easy harvest
Pruning TimeAfter each harvest flush; stagger pruning to maintain continuous production
How to PruneTip-prune after harvest to stimulate new flowering wood; remove dead and weak branches
Common PestsLongan stink bug, fruit borer, mites, scale insects
Common DiseasesWitches' broom, leaf spot, fruit rot, powdery mildew
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, copper fungicides, sanitation, remove infected panicles
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round; multiple flushes per year
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; 3–4 harvest cycles per year in tropical climates
Days to Harvest120–150 days from flowering per cycle
Harvest IndicatorsShell turns tan-brown, flesh becomes translucent and sweet, fruit detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant40–100 kg per tree per year (cumulative from multiple harvests)
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination and insect pollination
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile; bees improve fruit set; continuous flowering provides year-round pollinator food
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; produces functional male and bisexual flowers
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate quickly but seedlings may not retain everbearing trait
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering (marcotting) effective; fruits in 2–3 years
Propagation by GraftingCleft or veneer grafting on seedling rootstock; essential to maintain All Season trait
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate to high due to continuous fruiting
Soil Conservation PracticesEvergreen canopy provides year-round cover; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedComposting, biological pest control, cover cropping
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, lemongrass, basil
Biodiversity BenefitsYear-round flowering provides continuous food for pollinators
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsThailand, Vietnam, China, India (emerging), Australia
Market UsesFresh fruit year-round; off-season premium pricing
Processing UsesDried longan, canned fruit, beverages, desserts
Market Value (Approx.)₹200–600 per kg; premium during off-season months
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismLongan symbolizes good luck and fertility in Chinese culture; "Dragon Eye" name
Traditional UsesDried longan in TCM as blood tonic; fruit in festive desserts and soups

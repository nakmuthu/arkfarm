Jackfruit - Honey Red

Artocarpus heterophyllus 'Honey Red'

Common Names: Honey Red Jackfruit, Red Jackfruit

Local Name: Sivappu Palaa (Tamil), Lal Kathal (Hindi)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: Southeast Asia (selected cultivar with red-fleshed bulbs)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Honey Red — distinctive red-orange fleshed bulbs with intense honey-like sweetness

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge evergreen tree with broad, dense canopy
Plant Size10–18 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesAlternate, elliptic to obovate, 10–25 cm, dark green, glossy, leathery
FlowersMonoecious; tiny flowers on cauliflorous inflorescences on trunk and main branches
Flower CharacteristicsMale spikes cylindrical; female spikes barrel-shaped; borne on older wood
FruitMedium to large, 8–20 kg; oblong; bulbs are deep red-orange with honey-sweet flavor and firm texture
Fruit ColorGreen rind with blunt spines; deep red to crimson-orange bulbs inside
Seed CharacteristicsOval seeds (2–3 cm) within each bulb; edible when roasted or boiled
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root network
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; humid conditions preferred
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; frost-sensitive; tolerates brief heat up to 42°C
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained loamy or laterite soil rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; consistent moisture for young trees
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees only; needs ground planting for full production
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleQuarterly; increase potassium during fruiting for color development
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake, wood ash (potassium source), bone meal
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, Beauveria bassiana, garlic-chili spray
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch; recycle leaf litter
Training SystemModified central leader; keep lower branches accessible
Pruning TimePost-harvest (September–October)
How to PruneRemove dead and crossing branches; thin canopy for air and light
Common PestsShoot borer, fruit borer, mealybug, bark-eating caterpillar
Common DiseasesRhizopus fruit rot, anthracnose, pink disease, leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood sanitation, proper spacing, copper fungicides, remove infected fruits
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsJanuary–April
Fruiting SeasonMay–September
Days to Harvest100–150 days from pollination
Harvest IndicatorsHollow sound when tapped, strong sweet aroma, spines flatten, skin lightens
Average Yield per Plant80–150 fruits per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeWind and insect pollination
Pollination NotesMonoecious; wind is primary pollinator; good fruit set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsWind-pollinated; male and female inflorescences on same tree
Propagation by SeedsNot recommended; seedlings do not reliably produce red-fleshed fruit
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible with moderate success
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood or approach grafting essential to maintain red-flesh trait
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate once established
Soil Conservation PracticesLarge canopy prevents erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedComposting, jeevamrutha, biological pest management
Companion PlantsPepper, ginger, turmeric as understory crops
Biodiversity BenefitsHabitat for birds and insects; shade ecosystem
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsMalaysia, Thailand, Vietnam, India (emerging), Indonesia
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit; novelty/specialty market
Processing UsesDried fruit, chips, desserts, ice cream flavoring
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–500 per kg (premium due to rarity); grafted saplings highly valued
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismRed-fleshed jackfruit considered auspicious and rare in many cultures
Traditional UsesWood for furniture; seeds as food; latex as adhesive

Vitamin AHigher than yellow varieties due to carotenoid pigments

🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepNot a primary medicinal use
Immune SupportVitamin C and enhanced carotenoid content support immunity
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesLycopene and phenolic compounds show anti-inflammatory effects
Heart HealthPotassium and antioxidants support cardiovascular health
Digestive HealthFiber aids digestion; seeds used in traditional digestive remedies

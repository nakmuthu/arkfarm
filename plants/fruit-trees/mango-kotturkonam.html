Mango Kotturkonam

Mangifera indica 'Kotturkonam'

Common Names: Kotturkonam, Kottukonam Mango

Local Name: Kotturkonam Manga (Malayalam), Kotturkonam Maambazham (Tamil)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: Kotturkonam village, Thiruvananthapuram district, Kerala, India

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Kotturkonam — rare Kerala landrace with distinctive sweet-sour flavor and intense aroma

Average Lifespan: 50–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge, vigorous tree with broad, spreading canopy
Plant Size12–18 meters tall; canopy spread 10–15 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 18–30 cm long; new leaves reddish-purple turning deep green
FlowersSmall, yellowish-green in large terminal panicles (20–40 cm long)
Flower CharacteristicsBoth male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; mildly fragrant
FruitMedium to large, ovate-oblong, 250–450 g; moderately thick skin with juicy, aromatic pulp with slight fiber
Fruit ColorGreen turning yellowish-green with orange-yellow pulp when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsMedium monoembryonic seed; oblong with fibrous husk
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system; well-adapted to laterite soils
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; thrives in Kerala's warm, humid climate with monsoon rains
Temperature Range24–33°C ideal; tolerates up to 40°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1500–3000 mm annually; brief dry spell before flowering beneficial
Soil TypeLaterite soil, red loam, or well-drained alluvial soil
Soil pH Preference5.0–7.0 (tolerates acidic laterite soils)
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing10–12 meters between trees (large canopy)
Irrigation MethodBasin irrigation; supplemental watering during dry months (Jan–Mar)
Water RequirementModerate; well-adapted to monsoon climate
Can it Grow in Pots?Not ideal due to large size; grafted dwarf rootstock may allow container growing
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-monsoon, post-monsoon, pre-flowering
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (30–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, wood ash, vermicompost
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray, Bordeaux mixture
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch with coconut fronds, dried leaves; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center system; minimal training needed for seedling trees
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (July–August)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; thin canopy for air circulation
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, leaf webber, stone weevil
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial canker, sooty mold, die-back
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper fungicides, Bordeaux mixture, proper spacing, remove fallen debris
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–February (Kerala)
Fruiting SeasonApril–June
Days to Harvest100–130 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsSkin color lightens, fruit shoulders fill out, characteristic aroma develops
Average Yield per Plant150–300 fruits per tree (mature); alternate bearing tendency
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees)
Pollination NotesOnly 1–2% of flowers set fruit; humid conditions during flowering can reduce set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsPrimarily insect-pollinated; houseflies are major pollinators
Propagation by SeedsTraditionally seed-propagated (landrace); seedlings show some variation
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible but not common
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on local rootstock; ensures true-to-type plants
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; well-adapted to monsoon rainfall patterns
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep root system stabilizes laterite slopes; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedPanchagavya, jeevamrutha, mulching with coconut fronds, cow dung slurry
Companion PlantsCoconut, pepper vine, ginger, turmeric (traditional Kerala homestead model)
Biodiversity BenefitsHeritage tree supporting local biodiversity; habitat for birds, bats, and insects
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsThiruvananthapuram, Kollam, and Pathanamthitta districts of Kerala
Market UsesLocal premium table fruit, fresh consumption
Processing UsesMango pickle, mango juice, dried mango
Market Value (Approx.)₹80–200 per kg (local markets); limited commercial availability
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismHeritage mango variety representing Kerala's rich agrobiodiversity; named after its village of origin
Traditional UsesPrized in Kerala homesteads; mango leaves used in Hindu ceremonies; fruit in Vishu celebrations

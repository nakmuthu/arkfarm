Longan - Ruby

Dimocarpus longan 'Ruby'

Common Names: Ruby Longan, Red Longan, Red Dragon Eye

Local Name: Puvatti Pazham (Tamil), Longan (Hindi/English)

Family: Sapindaceae

Origin: Southeast Asia (rare cultivar selected for red skin pigmentation)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Ruby — distinctive red to reddish-brown skinned longan with sweet, aromatic flesh

Average Lifespan: 40–60+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium evergreen tree with dense, rounded canopy
Plant Size6–12 meters tall; canopy spread 5–8 meters
LeavesAlternate, pinnately compound, 4–8 pairs of leaflets, dark green, leathery; new growth may show reddish tinge
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white, in terminal panicles; functionally male, female, and bisexual
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant; attracts bees; requires cool period for flower induction
FruitRound, 2–3 cm diameter; distinctive red to reddish-brown shell; translucent sweet flesh
Fruit ColorRed to reddish-brown shell (unique among longan varieties); translucent white to pinkish flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSingle round, shiny dark seed; not edible
Root SystemModerately deep with spreading lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; needs brief cool period for flowering
Temperature Range20–32°C ideal; needs cool nights (10–15°C) for flower induction; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1200–2000 mm annually
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily); good light enhances red coloration
Spacing6–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; reduce watering before flowering
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture during fruit development
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (50+ liters); may fruit with proper care
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3–4 times per year; extra potassium for color development
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, fish emulsion, bone meal, wood ash (potassium)
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor monthly; treat during flowering and fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, pyrethrin
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (5–8 cm) to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemOpen center; maintain accessible canopy
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; tip-prune to encourage flowering wood
How to PruneRemove dead wood; tip-prune new growth; thin interior for light penetration (enhances red color)
Common PestsLongan stink bug, fruit borer, mites, scale insects
Common DiseasesWitches' broom, leaf spot, fruit rot, powdery mildew
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, copper fungicides, sanitation, remove infected material
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April (after cool period)
Fruiting SeasonJune–September
Days to Harvest130–160 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsShell develops full red-brown color, flesh becomes translucent and sweet, fruit detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant25–60 kg per tree per year (mature); slightly lower yield than standard varieties
Pollination TypeCross-pollination and self-pollination
Pollination NotesBenefits from cross-pollination with other longan varieties; bees are primary pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsProduces male and bisexual flowers; cross-pollination improves fruit set
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate easily but red-skin trait may not be reliably inherited
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering (marcotting) effective; maintains red-skin trait
Propagation by GraftingCleft or veneer grafting on seedling rootstock; essential to maintain Ruby characteristics
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation
Soil Conservation PracticesEvergreen canopy provides year-round cover; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedComposting, biological pest control, cover cropping
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, lemongrass, marigolds
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract pollinators; fruit eaten by birds and bats
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsThailand, Vietnam, China (limited); specialty/collector market worldwide
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit; novelty/specialty market; ornamental value
Processing UsesDried longan, fresh consumption; primarily sold as premium fresh fruit
Market Value (Approx.)₹400–800 per kg; commands premium due to rarity and unique appearance
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismRed color considered auspicious in Asian cultures; "Red Dragon Eye" is a collector's fruit
Traditional UsesSame TCM uses as standard longan; red variety considered especially potent as blood tonic

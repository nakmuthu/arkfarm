Jumbo Jamun / Bardoli

Syzygium cumini 'Bardoli'

Common Names: Jumbo Jamun, Bardoli Jamun, Giant Java Plum

Local Name: Bardoli Jamun (Gujarati/Hindi), Jumbo Naval (Tamil)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Bardoli, Gujarat, India; selected cultivar

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Bardoli — extra-large fruit (4–6 cm), high pulp-to-seed ratio, sweet

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge, vigorous evergreen tree with dense, spreading canopy
Plant Size12–20 meters tall; canopy spread 10–15 meters
LeavesOpposite, elliptic-oblong, 10–18 cm long; leathery, glossy dark green
FlowersSmall, fragrant, creamy-white, in dense clusters
Flower CharacteristicsBrush-like with numerous stamens; fragrant; attract bees and flies
FruitExtra-large oblong drupe, 4–6 cm long; the largest jamun variety; juicy, sweet pulp
Fruit ColorDeep purple to black when ripe; very juicy with dark staining juice
Seed CharacteristicsProportionally small seed; excellent pulp-to-seed ratio
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots; very stable
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; thrives in hot conditions
Temperature Range25–40°C ideal; tolerates heat well; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement800–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal drought
Soil TypeDeep, fertile alluvial or loamy soil; tolerates clay and waterlogging
Soil pH Preference5.5–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing10–12 meters between trees (large variety)
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; regular watering during fruiting for maximum fruit size
Water RequirementModerate during fruiting; drought-tolerant otherwise
Can it Grow in Pots?Not recommended due to vigorous growth; young grafted trees possible in very large containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year; pre-flowering, during fruiting, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (30–50 kg/tree/year), vermicompost, neem cake, potash
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; monitor during fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, fruit fly traps, pheromone traps
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch to retain moisture and support large fruit development
Training SystemModified central leader; maintain accessible height for harvest
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (August–September)
How to PruneRemove dead wood; thin canopy; control height for easier harvest
Common PestsFruit fly, leaf miner, white fly, bark borer
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, anthracnose, sooty mold, dieback
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood sanitation, copper fungicides, proper spacing for air circulation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May
Fruiting SeasonJune–August; heavy fruit clusters
Days to Harvest70–100 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit reaches full jumbo size, turns deep purple-black, very sweet and juicy
Average Yield per Plant80–250 kg per tree (mature); exceptionally heavy bearer
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects
Pollination NotesBees and flies are primary pollinators; self-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; fragrant flowers attract diverse pollinators
Propagation by SeedsPolyembryonic seeds; seedlings may not produce jumbo fruit — grafting essential
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood or veneer grafting on common jamun rootstock (essential); fruits in 4–5 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; excellent avenue and shade tree
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; panchagavya, jeevamrutha
Companion PlantsShade-tolerant crops, turmeric, ginger as intercrops
Biodiversity BenefitsAbundant fruit supports birds, bats, and wildlife; massive canopy provides habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsGujarat (Bardoli, Surat), Maharashtra, Rajasthan, India
Market UsesPremium fresh fruit, juice, specialty markets
Processing UsesJuice, jam, wine, vinegar, seed powder supplements
Market Value (Approx.)₹100–300 per kg (premium jumbo variety); grafted plants ₹400–1000
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismBardoli is famous for this jumbo variety; pride of Gujarat's fruit heritage
Traditional UsesFruit, bark, and seeds used in Ayurveda; seed powder for diabetes management; bark for oral health

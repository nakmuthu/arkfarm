Cherry - Grumichama

Eugenia brasiliensis

Common Names: Grumichama, Brazil Cherry, Grumixama, Spanish Cherry

Local Name: Grumixama (Portuguese/Tupi)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Coastal Atlantic Forest of Brazil (São Paulo to Santa Catarina)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Species type — dark purple fruit with sweet grape-cherry flavour; yellow-fruited form also exists

Average Lifespan: 40–60+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitAttractive, slow-growing evergreen tree with dense pyramidal canopy
Plant Size6–15 meters tall; canopy 4–8 meters wide
LeavesOpposite, elliptic, 6–12 cm long, glossy dark green; new growth reddish-bronze; leathery texture
FlowersWhite, 4-petaled, 2–2.5 cm across with numerous prominent stamens; showy and fragrant
Flower CharacteristicsProfuse blooming in spring; flowers borne along branches; attract bees
FruitRound berry, 1.5–2.5 cm diameter; smooth thin skin, soft juicy flesh with sweet grape-cherry flavour
Fruit ColorGreen turning deep purple-black when ripe (purple form); yellow form also available
Seed Characteristics1–3 round seeds per fruit; medium-sized
Root SystemDeep taproot with lateral roots; non-invasive
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; tolerates mild frost when mature
Temperature Range18–30°C ideal; mature trees tolerate brief frost to −3°C
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; prefers consistent moisture
Soil TypeRich, well-drained acidic soil; high organic matter preferred
Soil pH Preference5.0–6.5 (acidic)
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade; young plants benefit from some shade
Spacing5–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodRegular watering; drip irrigation preferred
Water RequirementModerate to high; keep soil consistently moist
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, when young (30+ liters); slow growth suits containers; will eventually need ground planting
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3–4 times per year during growing season
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, acidifying fertilizers, pine bark mulch, fish emulsion
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional scale; fruit fly traps during fruiting
Mulching PracticeThick acidic mulch (pine needles, bark) to maintain soil acidity and moisture
Training SystemNatural pyramidal form; minimal training needed
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting; light shaping any time
How to PruneMinimal pruning needed; remove dead or crossing branches; shape if desired
Common PestsFruit fly, birds, occasional scale insects
Common DiseasesGenerally disease-free; occasional leaf spot in very humid conditions
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; proper drainage; minimal intervention needed
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring (September–November in Southern Hemisphere)
Fruiting SeasonLate spring to early summer; 3–4 weeks after flowering
Days to Harvest21–30 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black; softens; sweet aroma; detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant10–30 kg per tree per year (mature)
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; insect-pollinated
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; self-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bees attracted to showy fragrant white flowers
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 4–8 weeks; seedlings slow-growing; fruit in 4–7 years
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; semi-hardwood cuttings with rooting hormone; low success rate
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on Eugenia rootstock; preferred for selected cultivars
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; adapted to Atlantic Forest rainfall
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots stabilize soil; dense canopy reduces erosion
Organic Practices UsedLow-input species; minimal chemical requirements
Companion PlantsOther Atlantic Forest species, shade-tolerant ground covers, ferns
Biodiversity BenefitsImportant food source for birds and wildlife; native to threatened Atlantic Forest biome
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsCoastal Brazil, Hawaii, Florida; niche rare fruit market worldwide
Market UsesFresh fruit, ornamental tree, rare fruit collections
Processing UsesJams, jellies, juice, wine, ice cream, liqueur
Market Value (Approx.)₹300–800 per kg (rare fruit premium); highly valued by collectors
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismTreasured native fruit of Brazil's Atlantic Forest; name from Tupi language "guamixama"
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh by indigenous Tupi people; bark used in traditional medicine; wood valued for construction

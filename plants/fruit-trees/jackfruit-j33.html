Jackfruit - J-33

Artocarpus heterophyllus 'J-33'

Common Names: Jackfruit, Kathal, Palaa Pazham

Local Name: Palaa Pazham (Tamil), Kathal (Hindi), Chakka (Malayalam)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: India (selected variety from Indian breeding programs)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: J-33 — high-yielding selection with firm, sweet, orange-yellow bulbs

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge evergreen tree with dense, spreading canopy
Plant Size10–20 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesAlternate, elliptic to obovate, 10–25 cm long, dark green, leathery, glossy
FlowersMonoecious; tiny flowers on cylindrical spikes (inflorescences) on trunk and main branches
Flower CharacteristicsMale spikes club-shaped, female spikes barrel-shaped; cauliflorous
FruitVery large compound fruit, 10–25 kg (J-33 averages 12–18 kg); oblong shape with firm sweet bulbs
Fruit ColorGreen rind with blunt spines; orange-yellow sweet bulbs inside
Seed CharacteristicsLarge oval seeds (2–3 cm) enclosed in each bulb; edible when cooked
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots; strong anchorage
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; warm and humid preferred
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 40°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1000–2400 mm annually; tolerates seasonal dry spells
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained alluvial or laterite soil; tolerates a range of soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–12 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; young trees need regular watering
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees only; requires ground planting for fruiting
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3–4 times per year; pre-monsoon, post-monsoon, and during fruiting
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (30–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, vermicompost, bone meal
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, Beauveria bassiana for borers
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; leaf litter recycling
Training SystemModified central leader; maintain lower branches for easy harvest
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (September–October)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased branches; thin canopy for light penetration
Common PestsShoot borer, fruit borer, mealybug, bark-eating caterpillar, spittle bug
Common DiseasesRhizopus fruit rot, leaf spot, pink disease, dieback
Disease Prevention & RemediesProper spacing, remove infected fruits, copper fungicides, good sanitation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–March (South India)
Fruiting SeasonApril–August; J-33 is known for consistent heavy bearing
Days to Harvest90–150 days from pollination
Harvest IndicatorsHollow sound when tapped, strong aroma, spines flatten, last leaf on stalk yellows
Average Yield per Plant150–250 fruits per tree per year (mature); J-33 is a prolific bearer
Pollination TypeWind and insect pollination
Pollination NotesMonoecious; male and female flowers on same tree; wind is primary pollinator
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsWind-pollinated primarily; gall midges also contribute
Propagation by SeedsSeeds viable for short period; germinate in 3–8 weeks; not true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible but slow
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood or approach grafting preferred for J-33 to maintain variety characteristics
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate once established; deep roots access groundwater
Soil Conservation PracticesLarge canopy reduces soil erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedJeevamrutha, panchagavya, composting fruit waste
Companion PlantsPepper vine, ginger, turmeric (as intercrops under canopy)
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides habitat for birds, bats, insects; large canopy offers shade ecosystem
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsKerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Assam, Tripura, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka
Market UsesFresh fruit, unripe vegetable, seeds as snack
Processing UsesChips, papad, jam, canned bulbs, flour from seeds, dried jackfruit
Market Value (Approx.)₹30–80 per kg (whole fruit); ₹200–400 per kg (ready-to-eat bulbs)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNational fruit of Bangladesh and Sri Lanka; symbol of abundance in South India
Traditional UsesWood used for furniture and musical instruments; unripe fruit as vegetable; latex as adhesive

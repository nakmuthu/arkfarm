Litchi

Litchi chinensis

Common Names: Litchi, Lychee, Lichee

Local Name: Litchi (Tamil), Litchi / Lichi (Hindi)

Family: Sapindaceae

Origin: Southern China (Guangdong, Fujian provinces)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Shahi, China, Bombai, Rose Scented, Calcuttia

Average Lifespan: 100+ years; some trees over 1000 years in China

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large evergreen tree with dense rounded canopy
Plant Size10–20 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesPinnately compound, 4–8 leaflets, 5–15 cm long, glossy dark green above, grey-green below
FlowersSmall, greenish-white to yellowish, borne in terminal panicles 10–30 cm long
Flower CharacteristicsApetalous (no petals); functionally male, female, and bisexual flowers on same panicle; fragrant
FruitOval to heart-shaped, 2.5–4 cm, rough bumpy rind enclosing translucent sweet flesh
Fruit ColorBright red to pinkish-red rind; white translucent flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSingle glossy brown seed; some varieties produce small "chicken tongue" seeds
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots; mycorrhizal associations
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical with distinct cool dry winter; needs winter chill for flowering
Temperature Range20–35°C growing; needs 10–15°C winter chill for 6–8 weeks to induce flowering
Rainfall Requirement1200–2000 mm annually; dry period before flowering beneficial
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained, slightly acidic soil rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.0–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily); shelter from strong winds
Spacing8–12 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or micro-sprinkler; reduce watering before flowering
Water RequirementModerate to high during fruit development; reduce in winter
Can it Grow in Pots?Possible in very large containers (50+ liters) but fruiting may be limited
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3–4 times per year; heavy feeding after harvest and before flowering
Recommended Organic FertilizersWell-rotted FYM, vermicompost, bone meal, neem cake
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor regularly; spray during flowering and fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, garlic extract, sticky traps for fruit borers
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen centre; maintain 3–4 main scaffold branches
Pruning TimeImmediately after harvest
How to PruneTip-prune after harvest to encourage new flush; remove dead and crossing branches
Common PestsLitchi mite, fruit borer, bark-eating caterpillar, mealy bug
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, leaf blight, dieback, fruit rot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, copper fungicide, remove infected parts, avoid overhead irrigation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–March (India); after winter chill period
Fruiting SeasonMay–June; short harvest window of 2–3 weeks
Days to Harvest60–80 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns bright red, bumps flatten slightly, sweet aroma develops
Average Yield per Plant80–150 kg per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeInsect pollination (bees, flies, wasps)
Pollination NotesCross-pollination between different flower types on same panicle; bees essential
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsComplex flowering with male, female, and bisexual phases; requires insect pollinators
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 1–2 weeks but seedlings take 8–12 years to fruit; not true to type
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering (marcotting) is the primary commercial method; roots in 8–12 weeks
Propagation by GraftingApproach grafting and veneer grafting on seedling rootstock
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate to high; efficient with drip irrigation
Soil Conservation PracticesLarge canopy prevents erosion; deep roots stabilize soil
Organic Practices UsedComposting, mulching, integrated pest management
Companion PlantsTurmeric, ginger as understorey; leguminous cover crops
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract diverse pollinators; fruit supports birds and bats
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsChina, India (Bihar, West Bengal), Vietnam, Thailand, Madagascar
Market UsesFresh fruit, canned, dried (litchi nuts)
Processing UsesCanned litchi, juice, wine, ice cream, dried fruit, squash
Market Value (Approx.)₹100–300 per kg (India); premium for Shahi variety
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismSymbol of romance and love in Chinese culture; imperial fruit of the Tang Dynasty
Traditional UsesPrized in Chinese medicine for tonifying qi; featured in Chinese poetry and art for over 2000 years
🍈 Nutritional Information
Calories66 kcal per 100g
Dietary Fiber1.3 g per 100g
Vitamin C71.5 mg per 100g (119% DV)
Vitamin ATrace amounts
Iron0.31 mg per 100g
Potassium171 mg per 100g
AntioxidantsRich in oligonol, polyphenols, proanthocyanidins
Other Key NutrientsCopper, phosphorus, B vitamins (B6, niacin, riboflavin, folate)
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepLitchi seed extract studied for calming properties in traditional Chinese medicine
Immune SupportVery high vitamin C content strongly supports immune function
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesOligonol (polyphenol) shows anti-inflammatory and anti-oxidant activity
Heart HealthPotassium and antioxidants support cardiovascular health
Digestive HealthFiber and water content aid digestion; seed used in TCM for digestive issues

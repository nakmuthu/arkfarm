Apple Wood

Limonia acidissima

Common Names: Wood Apple, Elephant Apple, Monkey Fruit, Curd Fruit

Local Name: Vilam Palam (Tamil), Kaitha / Kaith (Hindi), Belada Hannu (Kannada)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: Indian subcontinent and Sri Lanka

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous to semi-deciduous tree

Variety: Wood Apple — hard-shelled fruit with aromatic, tangy-sweet brown pulp

Average Lifespan: 50–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large, slow-growing tree with spreading, irregular canopy; armed with spines
Plant Size8–15 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesPinnately compound, 5–7 leaflets, each 2–4 cm long; aromatic when crushed; deciduous in dry season
FlowersSmall, 1–1.5 cm, dull red to greenish, in terminal or axillary panicles
Flower CharacteristicsBisexual, 5-petaled; mildly fragrant; inconspicuous but numerous
FruitRound, 5–9 cm diameter; extremely hard woody shell; brown, sticky, aromatic pulp inside
Fruit ColorGrey-green turning brownish when ripe; brown pulp inside
Seed CharacteristicsNumerous small, white, flat seeds (5–6 mm) embedded in sticky pulp
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots; extremely drought-hardy
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; thrives in hot, dry conditions; highly drought-tolerant
Temperature Range25–45°C ideal; tolerates extreme heat; frost-sensitive below 0°C
Rainfall Requirement500–1500 mm annually; thrives in semi-arid to dry tropical zones
Soil TypeHighly adaptable; grows in poor, rocky, laterite, and alkaline soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–8.5 (very tolerant)
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodMinimal irrigation needed; basin irrigation during establishment
Water RequirementVery low; one of the most drought-tolerant fruit trees
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees can be grown in large pots; slow growth makes it manageable
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule1–2 times per year; very low maintenance
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (10–20 kg/tree/year), wood ash, compost; thrives with minimal inputs
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional leaf-eating caterpillars; generally pest-free
Mulching PracticeOptional; leaf litter provides natural mulch
Training SystemNatural form; minimal training needed; remove thorny lower branches for access
Pruning TimeDuring dormancy (dry season); remove dead wood
How to PruneRemove dead branches and low thorny growth; thin canopy for light; wear protective gloves
Common PestsBark-eating caterpillar, leaf miner (rare); fruit bats
Common DiseasesPowdery mildew (rare), canker; generally very disease-resistant
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; rarely needs treatment; remove infected branches if any
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsApril–June (with new leaf flush)
Fruiting SeasonOctober–March (winter season)
Days to Harvest150–200 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsShell turns brownish-grey, fruit drops naturally, strong aromatic smell when cracked
Average Yield per Plant100–300 fruits per tree (mature); begins bearing at 7–10 years from seed
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects
Pollination NotesBees and flies are primary pollinators; good fruit set in dry conditions
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; bisexual flowers; self-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 1–3 weeks; most common propagation method; slow to fruit (7–10 years)
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; root suckers can be transplanted
Propagation by GraftingBudding or veneer grafting on seedling rootstock; reduces time to fruiting (4–5 years)
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; one of the most water-efficient fruit trees
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots stabilize degraded and rocky soils; excellent for wasteland reclamation
Organic Practices UsedNaturally organic; requires virtually no chemical inputs
Companion PlantsNeem, tamarind, palmyra palm (dryland agroforestry)
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides food for elephants, monkeys, and birds; thorny branches shelter nesting birds
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Tamil Nadu, AP, Karnataka, Maharashtra), Sri Lanka, Bangladesh
Market UsesFresh fruit pulp, beverages, chutney, traditional medicine
Processing UsesWood apple juice, chutney, jam, pickle; shell used as bowls; gum from bark
Market Value (Approx.)₹20–80 per kg; affordable rural fruit; processed products command higher prices
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismSacred to Lord Shiva in Hindu tradition; associated with resilience and endurance
Traditional UsesFruit pulp mixed with jaggery as traditional drink; bark gum used as adhesive; leaves in rituals

🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepNot a primary use
Immune SupportRich in minerals and antioxidants; traditional tonic for overall health

Passion Fruit

Passiflora edulis

Common Names: Passion Fruit, Purple Granadilla, Maracuja

Local Name: Krishna Phal (Hindi), Thokkudu Pandu (Telugu)

Family: Passifloraceae

Origin: Southern Brazil, Paraguay, Northern Argentina

Plant Type: Perennial climbing vine

Variety: Purple (Passiflora edulis) and Yellow (Passiflora edulis f. flavicarpa)

Average Lifespan: 5–7 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitVigorous climbing vine with tendrils, can grow 15–20 feet per year
Plant SizeVine length up to 10–15 meters
LeavesAlternate, deeply 3-lobed, serrated edges, 7–20 cm long, glossy dark green
FlowersStriking 5–7 cm diameter, white petals with purple-blue corona filaments
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant, complex structure with 5 stamens and 3 stigmas; blooms for only one day
FruitRound to oval, 4–8 cm diameter, thick rind enclosing juicy pulp with seeds
Fruit ColorPurple to dark purple when ripe (purple variety); yellow when ripe (yellow variety)
Seed CharacteristicsSmall, black, hard-coated seeds surrounded by aromatic orange pulp
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; sensitive to waterlogging
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range20–30°C ideal; can tolerate brief periods down to 2°C
Rainfall Requirement900–2000 mm annually; well-distributed
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference6.0–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing3–5 meters between plants; needs trellis support
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation preferred; consistent moisture needed
Water RequirementModerate to high; avoid waterlogging
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (20+ liters) with trellis support
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleEvery 4–6 weeks during growing season; reduce in winter
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, well-rotted manure, bone meal, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyAs needed; monitor regularly for pests
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, pyrethrin spray, insecticidal soap
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch (5–10 cm) to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemTrellis or pergola system; train main stem vertically, laterals horizontally
Pruning TimeAfter harvest season; early spring
How to PruneRemove dead/weak growth; cut back laterals to 30 cm from main stem
Common PestsFruit fly, aphids, mealybugs, spider mites, passion vine hopper
Common DiseasesFusarium wilt, brown spot, woodiness virus, collar rot
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, crop rotation, resistant rootstocks, remove infected plants promptly
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring to early autumn (varies by region)
Fruiting SeasonSummer to autumn; 60–80 days after flowering
Days to Harvest60–80 days from pollination
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns fully colored and drops from vine; slight wrinkling of skin
Average Yield per Plant15–20 kg per year (mature vine)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination required (purple variety); self-fertile (yellow variety)
Pollination NotesCarpenter bees are primary pollinators; hand pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsFlowers open for one day only; pollination must occur within hours of opening
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 10–20 days; soak 24 hours before sowing
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings with 3–4 nodes; root in 3–4 weeks
Propagation by GraftingWedge grafting onto disease-resistant rootstock for commercial production
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation
Soil Conservation PracticesGround cover beneath vines; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedCompost tea, companion planting, biological pest control
Companion PlantsMarigolds, basil, beans (nitrogen fixers)
Biodiversity BenefitsAttracts pollinators; flowers support carpenter bees and butterflies
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBrazil, Colombia, Ecuador, Kenya, India, Australia
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, pulp concentrate
Processing UsesJuice, jam, ice cream flavoring, cocktails, desserts
Market Value (Approx.)₹80–200 per kg (India); varies by season and region
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNamed by Spanish missionaries who saw the flower as a symbol of the Passion of Christ
Traditional UsesLeaves used in traditional medicine for anxiety and insomnia; fruit in beverages

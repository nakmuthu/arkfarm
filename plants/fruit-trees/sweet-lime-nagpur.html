Sweet Lime - Nagpur

Citrus limetta

Common Names: Sweet Lime, Mosambi, Sweet Lemon

Local Name: Sathukudi (Tamil), Mosambi (Hindi), Musambi (Marathi)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: India (Nagpur variety — Maharashtra)

Plant Type: Small evergreen tree, 5–8 meters tall

Variety: Nagpur — the most commercially popular Indian sweet lime

Average Lifespan: 20–30 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium-sized spreading tree with rounded canopy and few thorns
Plant Size5–8 meters tall; canopy spread 4–6 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, ovate-elliptic, 6–12 cm long; aromatic, glossy dark green with narrowly winged petioles
FlowersWhite, fragrant, 2–3 cm diameter; borne in axillary clusters
Flower Characteristics5-petaled, sweetly scented; blooms in multiple flushes per year
FruitRound to oblate, 6–8 cm diameter; smooth thin rind, sweet mild-flavored juice with low acidity
Fruit ColorGreen turning pale yellow-green when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsPolyembryonic; 3–8 small white seeds per fruit; some fruits nearly seedless
Root SystemModerately deep taproot with well-developed lateral root system
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; prefers hot, semi-arid conditions
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement750–1200 mm annually; dry period improves fruit sweetness
Soil TypeWell-drained black cotton soil, alluvial, or sandy loam
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing5–6 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation recommended; withhold water before flowering to induce bloom (bahar treatment)
Water RequirementModerate; stress irrigation used to regulate flowering
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (30+ liters); will produce fewer fruits
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (20–30 kg/tree/year), neem cake, vermicompost, bone meal
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during new flush and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, fish oil rosin soap, panchagavya, sticky traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (sugarcane trash, dried leaves) in basin area
Training SystemModified central leader; maintain 3–4 scaffold branches
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; remove dead and diseased wood
How to PruneLight pruning to remove water sprouts, dead branches, and improve canopy aeration
Common PestsCitrus psylla, leaf miner, aphids, whitefly, fruit sucking moth
Common DiseasesCitrus greening (HLB), gummosis, Phytophthora root rot, citrus canker
Disease Prevention & RemediesUse certified disease-free budwood, Bordeaux mixture, proper drainage, psylla control for HLB prevention
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsThree bahars — Ambe (Jan–Feb), Mrig (Jun–Jul), Hasta (Sep–Oct)
Fruiting SeasonMrig bahar harvest: November–January (most commercial); Ambe: May–June
Days to Harvest150–180 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns pale yellow-green; slight softening; TSS 8–10° Brix
Average Yield per Plant150–300 fruits per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination; parthenocarpy common
Pollination NotesInsect pollination improves fruit set; single tree sufficient
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsMaharashtra (Nagpur, Akola), Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Rajasthan
Market UsesFresh juice (most popular use), fresh fruit, hospital/convalescent food
Processing UsesJuice concentrate, squash, essential oil from peel, pectin extraction
Market Value (Approx.)₹30–80 per kg; India's most consumed citrus juice fruit

🌿 Medicinal Uses
Immune SupportRich vitamin C content supports immune function; commonly given during illness recovery
Digestive HealthMild, non-acidic juice is gentle on the stomach; recommended for patients with gastric issues
HydrationExcellent rehydration drink; high potassium and natural electrolytes
Heart HealthPotassium-rich juice supports blood pressure regulation

Cherry - Cedar Bay

Eugenia reinwardtiana

Common Names: Cedar Bay Cherry, Beach Cherry, Australian Brush Cherry

Local Name: Cedar Bay Cherry (English/Australian)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Tropical North Queensland (Australia), Papua New Guinea, Malesia

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen shrub to small tree

Variety: Native Australian species — compact fruiting shrub

Average Lifespan: 20–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitCompact, dense evergreen shrub or small tree; attractive ornamental form
Plant Size2–6 meters tall; compact canopy 2–4 meters wide
LeavesOpposite, elliptic to ovate, 3–8 cm long, glossy dark green; new growth pinkish-red; aromatic
FlowersWhite, 4-petaled, 1–1.5 cm across with prominent stamens; borne in leaf axils
Flower CharacteristicsLightly fragrant; attract native bees and insects; bloom in spring and summer
FruitOval to round berry, 1.5–2.5 cm long; thin skin, soft juicy flesh with mild sweet cherry flavour
Fruit ColorGreen turning bright red to deep crimson when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsSingle large seed per fruit; round, smooth
Root SystemFibrous, non-invasive; suitable for garden and container growing
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; does not tolerate frost
Rainfall Requirement1000–3000 mm annually; prefers consistent moisture
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to clay loam; tolerates coastal sandy soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5 (slightly acidic)
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade; tolerates understory conditions
Spacing2–4 meters between plants
Irrigation MethodRegular watering; drip irrigation preferred
Water RequirementModerate; keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container plant (15–25 liters); compact growth habit suits pots well
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year during growing season
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, slow-release native plant fertilizer, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional scale; eco-oil for mites
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch to mimic rainforest floor conditions
Training SystemNatural compact form; minimal training needed
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting; light shaping any time
How to PruneLight tip-pruning for shape; remove dead or crossing branches
Common PestsFruit fly (Queensland), birds, occasional scale insects
Common DiseasesGenerally disease-free; occasional leaf spot in humid conditions
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; avoid overhead watering; remove fallen fruit
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring to summer (September–February in Australia)
Fruiting SeasonSummer to early autumn; 4–6 weeks after flowering
Days to Harvest30–45 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep red to crimson; softens; sweet aroma
Average Yield per Plant3–10 kg per plant per year (mature)
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; insect-pollinated
Pollination NotesNative bees and stingless bees are primary pollinators in Australia
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; native insects and bees pollinate effectively
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 3–8 weeks; seedlings fruit in 3–5 years
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings with rooting hormone; moderate success rate
Propagation by GraftingCan be grafted on compatible Eugenia rootstock
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; adapted to tropical rainfall patterns
Soil Conservation PracticesNative species supports local ecosystem; prevents coastal erosion
Organic Practices UsedLow-input native species; minimal chemical requirements
Companion PlantsOther Australian rainforest species, native ground covers, ferns
Biodiversity BenefitsSupports native birds (cassowary food source), insects, and rainforest ecology
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsNorth Queensland (Australia), Papua New Guinea; niche market
Market UsesFresh fruit, native food (bush tucker), ornamental plant
Processing UsesJams, sauces, desserts; emerging bush food ingredient
Market Value (Approx.)AUD $15–30 per kg (premium bush food market)

SymbolismPart of Australian bush tucker heritage; valued by Indigenous communities
Traditional UsesTraditional food of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples; fruit eaten fresh from the bush

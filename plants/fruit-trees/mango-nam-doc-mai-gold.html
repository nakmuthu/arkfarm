Mango Nam Doc Mai Gold

Mangifera indica 'Nam Doc Mai'

Common Names: Nam Doc Mai, Nam Doc Mai Gold, Golden Nam Doc Mai

Local Name: น้ำดอกไม้ (Thai), Nam Doc Mai (English transliteration)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: Thailand

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Nam Doc Mai Gold — elongated, golden, fiberless Thai dessert mango

Average Lifespan: 40–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium-sized, upright to spreading tree with dense, dome-shaped canopy
Plant Size8–12 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 15–25 cm long; new flush coppery-red maturing to dark glossy green
FlowersSmall, pinkish-white in large terminal panicles (20–35 cm long)
Flower CharacteristicsBoth male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; mildly sweet fragrance
FruitElongated, slender, sigmoid-shaped, 200–400 g; thin skin with silky, fiberless, sweet pulp
Fruit ColorGreen turning bright golden-yellow when ripe; no blush
Seed CharacteristicsThin, flat monoembryonic seed; small relative to fruit size
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root network
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; requires distinct dry season for flowering
Temperature Range24–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 42°C; frost-sensitive below 2°C
Rainfall Requirement750–2000 mm annually; dry spell of 2–3 months triggers flowering
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained loamy or alluvial soil; tolerates laterite
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing7–9 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; withhold water during pre-flowering dry period
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container variety in 50+ liter pots; naturally compact
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering (Oct), post-fruit set (Feb), post-harvest (Jul)
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (20–40 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, vermicompost, fish emulsion
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray, sticky traps for fruit flies
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (rice straw, coconut husk) around base; keep 15 cm from trunk
Training SystemOpen center or modified central leader; tip-prune for compact form
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (July–September)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and inward-growing branches; tip-prune to encourage lateral branching
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealybug, thrips
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial black spot, stem-end rot
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper-based fungicides at flowering, proper spacing, remove fallen debris, bagging fruits
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsNovember–January (tropical); may vary by region
Fruiting SeasonMarch–June
Days to Harvest90–120 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsSkin turns golden-yellow, fruit softens slightly, sweet aroma develops at stem end
Average Yield per Plant80–150 fruits per tree (mature); consistent bearer
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees, wasps)
Pollination NotesOnly 1–3% of flowers set fruit; warm dry weather during flowering improves set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsPrimarily insect-pollinated; houseflies and blowflies are major pollinators in Thailand
Propagation by SeedsMonoembryonic; seedlings do not come true-to-type; used for rootstock only
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering (marcotting) possible but not preferred commercially
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting or cleft grafting on vigorous polyembryonic rootstock (preferred method)
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate once established; deep roots access subsoil moisture
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep root system prevents erosion; leaf litter enriches topsoil
Organic Practices UsedFruit bagging, panchagavya, jeevamrutha, mulching with rice straw
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, lemongrass, turmeric (as intercrops when young)
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides habitat for birds and insects; canopy offers shade for understory plants
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsThailand (Chachoengsao, Ratchaburi), Florida (USA), Australia, India
Market UsesPremium export table fruit, fresh-cut mango, dessert mango with sticky rice
Processing UsesDried mango, mango puree, frozen mango slices, mango juice
Market Value (Approx.)₹200–500 per kg (premium export variety); $3–8 USD per fruit in Western markets
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNam Doc Mai means "water of flowers" in Thai; considered the king of Thai mangoes
Traditional UsesServed with sticky rice (Khao Niao Mamuang) — Thailand's iconic dessert; gifted during Songkran festival

Calories60 kcal per 100g

AntioxidantsRich in beta-carotene, mangiferin, quercetin
Other Key NutrientsFolate, vitamin B6, copper, vitamin E

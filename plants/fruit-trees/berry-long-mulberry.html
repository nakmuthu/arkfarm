Long Mulberry

Morus alba (long fruit variety)

Common Names: Long Mulberry, Long Fruit Mulberry, Shahtoot Long

Local Name: Neelambu Shahtoot (Tamil), Lambi Shahtoot (Hindi)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: China; selected cultivar of Morus alba

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous tree

Variety: Long fruit selection — elongated fruits 3–5 cm

Average Lifespan: 40–60 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large deciduous tree with spreading canopy
Plant Size8–12 meters tall; canopy spread 6–10 meters
LeavesAlternate, simple, ovate to heart-shaped, 8–15 cm long; serrated; glossy green
FlowersGreenish catkins, 2–4 cm long; inconspicuous
Flower CharacteristicsMonoecious or dioecious; wind-pollinated; appear in spring with new leaves
FruitElongated syncarp, 3–5 cm long; sweet, juicy, cylindrical shape
Fruit ColorWhite to pinkish-purple when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsTiny seeds within drupelets; inconspicuous
Root SystemDeep taproot with spreading lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTemperate to subtropical; adaptable to various climates
Temperature Range15–35°C ideal; tolerates frost when dormant
Rainfall Requirement600–2000 mm annually; drought-tolerant once established
Soil TypeAdaptable; prefers deep, well-drained loamy soil
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing5–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; minimal once established
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large pots (30–50 liters) with regular pruning
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; spring and after fruiting
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, FYM, neem cake, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional scale or mealybug issues
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base to retain moisture
Training SystemOpen center or central leader
Pruning TimeLate winter while dormant
How to PruneShape canopy; remove dead and crossing branches; can be pruned hard
Common PestsScale insects, mealybugs, whiteflies, birds
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, powdery mildew, root rot in wet soils
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, air circulation, remove fallen debris
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April
Fruiting SeasonApril–June; fruits ripen progressively
Days to Harvest30–50 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit fully elongated, soft, sweet, and easily detaches
Average Yield per Plant20–40 kg per tree (mature)
Pollination TypeWind pollination
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile varieties available; some need male pollinator nearby
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsWind-pollinated; monoecious types are self-fertile
Propagation by SeedsPossible but not true-to-type; seedlings variable
Propagation by CuttingsHardwood cuttings in winter; roots easily
Propagation by GraftingCleft or whip grafting on seedling rootstock to maintain variety
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; very drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots stabilize soil; leaf litter enriches topsoil
Organic Practices UsedMinimal chemical inputs needed; natural leaf mulch
Companion PlantsShade-tolerant herbs, leguminous ground covers
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit attracts birds and wildlife; provides shade habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsChina, India, Pakistan, Turkey, Middle East
Market UsesFresh fruit, dried fruit, specialty markets
Processing UsesDried mulberries, jam, juice, wine, syrup
Market Value (Approx.)₹150–400 per kg (premium long-fruited variety)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismValued in Asian cultures for abundance and longevity
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh and dried; leaves for silkworm rearing; bark in traditional crafts

Sea Grape

Coccoloba uvifera

Common Names: Sea Grape, Baygrape, Uva de Playa

Local Name: Uva de Playa (Spanish), Sea Grape (English)

Family: Polygonaceae

Origin: Coastal tropical Americas (Caribbean, Central America, South Florida)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree/shrub

Variety: Species type — coastal fruit tree with grape-like clusters; highly salt and wind tolerant

Average Lifespan: 25–50+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSmall to medium evergreen tree or large shrub; often multi-trunked with spreading form
Plant Size4–8 meters tall; canopy spread 4–8 meters; wind-sculpted in coastal areas
LeavesLarge, round to kidney-shaped, 10–25 cm diameter, leathery, glossy green with red veins; turn red before dropping
FlowersSmall, white to cream, fragrant; borne on elongated racemes (15–25 cm)
Flower CharacteristicsDioecious (separate male and female trees); fragrant; attracts bees
FruitRound, 1.5–2 cm diameter; grape-like clusters; thin skin with sweet-tart pulp around single large seed
Fruit ColorGreen turning purple to dark purple when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsSingle large, hard seed per fruit; disproportionately large relative to fruit size
Root SystemDeep, extensive root system; excellent for sand stabilization
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical coastal; highly salt and wind tolerant
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; frost-sensitive below -1°C
Rainfall Requirement750–2000 mm annually; drought-tolerant once established
Soil TypeSandy, well-drained; thrives in poor, sandy coastal soils; salt-tolerant
Soil pH Preference6.0–8.0 (tolerates alkaline coastal soils)
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily); tolerates partial shade
Spacing4–6 meters between trees; can be planted as hedge
Irrigation MethodMinimal once established; supplemental during prolonged drought
Water RequirementLow; very drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers (30+ liters); makes attractive ornamental
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; light feeding; tolerates poor soils
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, seaweed extract, fish emulsion
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; very pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil if needed; generally pest-free
Mulching PracticeLight organic mulch; natural leaf litter sufficient
Training SystemNatural form; can be trained as hedge, screen, or single-trunk tree
Pruning TimeAny time; responds well to pruning
How to PruneShape as desired; remove dead wood; can be heavily pruned for hedge form
Common PestsGenerally pest-free; occasional scale insects, leaf notcher
Common DiseasesLeaf spot (minor); generally disease-resistant
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; rarely needs treatment
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in tropics; main flush spring to summer
Fruiting SeasonSummer to autumn; fruits ripen unevenly in clusters
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple, softens slightly, detaches easily from cluster
Average Yield per Plant10–30 kg per tree per year (female trees only)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination required (dioecious)
Pollination NotesNeed both male and female trees for fruit; bees are primary pollinators; 1 male per 5–6 females
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsDioecious; male and female flowers on separate trees; wind and insect pollination
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; sex unknown until flowering (3–4 years)
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings possible; allows sex selection
Propagation by GraftingAir layering effective; allows selection of female (fruiting) plants
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; extremely drought-tolerant
Soil Conservation PracticesExcellent for coastal erosion control; deep roots stabilize sand dunes
Organic Practices UsedNaturally low-maintenance; minimal inputs needed
Companion PlantsCoconut palm, sea almond, beach morning glory
Biodiversity BenefitsCritical coastal habitat; fruit eaten by birds and wildlife; nesting site for birds
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsCaribbean islands, South Florida, Central America, coastal tropical regions
Market UsesFresh fruit, jelly, wine; ornamental and landscaping
Processing UsesJelly, jam, wine, vinegar; wood for crafts
Market Value (Approx.)₹200–500 per kg (niche market); primarily local consumption
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismIconic coastal tree of the Caribbean; symbol of tropical beaches
Traditional UsesBark and roots used in traditional medicine for diarrhea; wood for charcoal; leaves for wrapping

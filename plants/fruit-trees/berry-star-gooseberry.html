Star Gooseberry

Phyllanthus acidus

Common Names: Star Gooseberry, Otaheite Gooseberry, Malay Gooseberry, Cermai

Local Name: Arunelli (Tamil), Harfarauri (Hindi), Chalmeri (Bengali)

Family: Phyllanthaceae

Origin: Madagascar; widely naturalized in tropical Asia

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous or semi-deciduous tree

Variety: Standard type — pale yellow, ribbed, sour fruit

Average Lifespan: 30–50 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium-sized tree with dense, spreading crown and drooping branches
Plant Size5–10 meters tall; canopy spread 4–6 meters
LeavesAlternate, ovate, 2–7 cm long; arranged on deciduous branchlets resembling pinnate leaves
FlowersTiny, pinkish-red, in dense clusters along older branches and trunk (cauliflorous)
Flower CharacteristicsMonoecious; male and female flowers in same cluster; inconspicuous
FruitSmall, 1–2.5 cm diameter, oblate with 6–8 ribs; waxy, crisp, very sour flesh
Fruit ColorPale yellow to greenish-white when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsSingle hard, ribbed stone containing 4–6 seeds
Root SystemDeep taproot with lateral spreading roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; does not tolerate frost
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal drought
Soil TypeAdaptable; grows in sandy, loamy, or clay soils with good drainage
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing6–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin irrigation; drought-tolerant once established
Water RequirementLow to moderate; very hardy once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees can be grown in large pots (40+ liters); will limit size
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; pre-monsoon and post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, compost, neem cake, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional caterpillar or scale infestations
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; leaf litter serves as natural mulch
Training SystemCentral leader or natural form
Pruning TimeAfter harvest or during dormancy
How to PruneRemove dead and crossing branches; thin canopy for light penetration
Common PestsCaterpillars, scale insects, fruit borers (generally minimal)
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, root rot in waterlogged conditions
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, avoid waterlogging, remove fallen debris
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May (tropical regions)
Fruiting SeasonJune–September; fruits in heavy clusters
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns pale yellow, becomes slightly translucent and waxy
Average Yield per Plant50–100 kg per tree (mature)
Pollination TypeWind and insect pollination
Pollination NotesMonoecious; single tree produces fruit; wind assists pollination
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile monoecious tree; wind and small insects pollinate
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; seedlings fruit in 4–6 years
Propagation by CuttingsHardwood cuttings possible; air layering more reliable
Propagation by GraftingAir layering preferred for faster fruiting; budding also used
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; very drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; deciduous leaves enrich soil
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; naturally hardy tree
Companion PlantsTurmeric, ginger, leguminous cover crops as intercrops
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides shade and habitat; fruit attracts birds
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia, Southeast Asia, Caribbean, Pacific Islands
Market UsesFresh fruit, pickles, chutneys, preserves
Processing UsesPickle, chutney, candied fruit, dried fruit, juice
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–100 per kg (local markets)

Sweet Lime - Bali

Citrus limetta (Bali variety)

Common Names: Bali Sweet Lime, Balinese Sweet Lime

Local Name: Sathukudi (Tamil), Jeruk Manis (Indonesian)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: Bali, Indonesia

Plant Type: Small evergreen tree, 4–6 meters tall

Variety: Bali selection — adapted to tropical island conditions, aromatic fruit

Average Lifespan: 20–25 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitCompact, upright to semi-spreading tree with dense canopy and sparse thorns
Plant Size4–6 meters tall; canopy spread 3–4 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, ovate-elliptic, 5–9 cm long; dark green, aromatic, with narrowly winged petioles
FlowersWhite, fragrant, 2–2.5 cm diameter; borne singly or in small axillary clusters
Flower Characteristics5-petaled, intensely fragrant; blooms profusely in warm humid conditions
FruitRound to slightly oblate, 5–7 cm diameter; thin smooth rind, sweet aromatic juice with very low acidity
Fruit ColorGreen turning pale yellow-green when ripe; slightly more golden than Nagpur variety
Seed CharacteristicsPolyembryonic; 2–6 small seeds per fruit; occasional seedless fruits
Root SystemModerate taproot with spreading lateral roots; tolerates volcanic soils well
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; thrives in warm, humid equatorial conditions
Temperature Range24–33°C ideal; frost-intolerant
Rainfall Requirement1500–2500 mm annually; tolerates high humidity
Soil TypeWell-drained volcanic, loamy, or sandy loam soil rich in minerals
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing4–5 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip or basin irrigation; natural rainfall often sufficient in Bali-like climates
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture but good drainage essential
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, suitable for large containers (25–30 liters); compact habit makes it pot-friendly
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleQuarterly during active growth; organic feeding preferred
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, neem cake, fish emulsion, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyMonthly monitoring; treat during new flush and fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, garlic-chili spray, yellow sticky traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (coconut husk, rice straw, dried leaves) around root zone
Training SystemOpen center system; maintain 3–4 well-spaced scaffold branches
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; light shaping year-round
How to PruneRemove dead wood, water sprouts, and crossing branches; maintain compact form
Common PestsCitrus leaf miner, aphids, scale insects, fruit fly, mealybugs
Common DiseasesCitrus canker, Phytophthora root rot, anthracnose, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesEnsure good drainage, copper-based fungicides, remove infected material, avoid waterlogging
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsYear-round in equatorial conditions; main flush during dry season
Fruiting SeasonYear-round; peak harvest 5–6 months after main flowering
Days to Harvest140–170 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns pale yellow-green; slight softening; sweet fragrance
Average Yield per Plant100–200 fruits per tree per year
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination; parthenocarpy occurs
Pollination NotesSingle tree sufficient; insect visitors improve fruit set
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBali, Java, and other Indonesian islands; niche variety in Indian home orchards
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, ceremonial offerings in Balinese culture
Processing UsesFresh juice, flavoring, essential oil from peel
Market Value (Approx.)₹50–120 per kg; premium as an imported variety

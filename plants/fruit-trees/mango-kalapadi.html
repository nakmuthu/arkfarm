Mango - Kalapadi

Mangifera indica 'Kalapadi'

Common Names: Kalapadi Mango, Kalapadi Maa

Local Name: Kalapadi Maa (Tamil)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: Tamil Nadu, India

Plant Type: Large evergreen tree, 10–15 meters tall

Variety: Kalapadi — traditional Tamil Nadu variety, medium-sized sweet fruit

Average Lifespan: 40–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge spreading tree with dense, rounded canopy
Plant Size10–15 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 15–30 cm long; new leaves coppery-red turning dark green
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white in large terminal panicles (20–35 cm long)
Flower CharacteristicsBoth male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; mildly fragrant; insect-pollinated
FruitMedium-sized, ovate-oblong, 200–350 g; smooth skin, sweet aromatic pulp with moderate fiber
Fruit ColorGreen turning yellow with orange blush when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsLarge monoembryonic seed; oblong with fibrous husk
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system; drought-tolerant once established
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; needs distinct dry period for flowering
Temperature Range24–30°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement750–2000 mm annually; dry spell before flowering essential
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained alluvial, laterite, or red loam soil
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; reduce watering during flowering
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees in large pots (50+ liters); will limit size and yield
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (25–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, vermicompost
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray, sticky traps for fruit flies
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; keep away from trunk to prevent collar rot
Training SystemOpen center or modified leader system
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (July–August)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; tip-prune for compact growth
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealybug, leaf webber
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial canker, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper-based fungicides, proper spacing for air circulation, remove fallen debris
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–February (South India)
Fruiting SeasonApril–June
Days to Harvest100–120 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit shoulders fill out, skin color lightens, slight aroma develops at stem end
Average Yield per Plant100–200 fruits per tree (mature); alternate bearing common
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees)
Pollination NotesOnly 1–2% of flowers set fruit; houseflies are major pollinators
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsTamil Nadu (Krishnagiri, Dharmapuri, Salem districts)
Market UsesTable fruit, juice, traditional pickle
Processing UsesPulp, juice, pickle, dried mango slices
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–100 per kg; popular in local Tamil Nadu markets

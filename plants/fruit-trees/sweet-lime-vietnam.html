Sweet Lime - Vietnam

Citrus limetta (Vietnam variety)

Common Names: Vietnam Sweet Lime, Vietnamese Mosambi

Local Name: Sathukudi (Tamil)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: Vietnam

Plant Type: Small evergreen tree, 4–6 meters tall

Variety: Vietnam selection — compact growth, prolific bearer

Average Lifespan: 20–25 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitCompact, semi-spreading tree with dense foliage and few thorns
Plant Size4–6 meters tall; canopy spread 3–5 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, ovate, 5–10 cm long; glossy dark green with mildly winged petioles
FlowersWhite, fragrant, 2–2.5 cm diameter; borne in leaf axils
Flower Characteristics5-petaled, sweetly scented; profuse blooming in tropical conditions
FruitRound, 5–7 cm diameter; thin smooth rind, sweet juice with mild flavor and very low acidity
Fruit ColorGreen turning pale greenish-yellow when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsPolyembryonic; few seeds per fruit; some fruits seedless
Root SystemModerate taproot with lateral fibrous roots; adapts well to various soil types
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; thrives in warm, humid conditions
Temperature Range22–35°C ideal; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1000–2000 mm annually; tolerates higher humidity than Nagpur variety
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy to sandy loam soil
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing4–5 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation or basin irrigation; consistent moisture preferred
Water RequirementModerate; regular watering during dry spells
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, well-suited for containers (25–30 liters) due to compact size
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleQuarterly application during active growth; reduce in cool months
Recommended Organic FertilizersVermicompost, neem cake, compost, fish meal, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor monthly; treat during new flush emergence
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, horticultural oil, yellow sticky traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (rice straw, dried leaves) around the root zone
Training SystemOpen center system; maintain balanced scaffold branches
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; light maintenance pruning year-round
How to PruneRemove dead wood, water sprouts, and inward-growing branches; maintain compact shape
Common PestsCitrus leaf miner, aphids, scale insects, mealybugs, fruit fly
Common DiseasesCitrus canker, Phytophthora root rot, sooty mold, anthracnose
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, copper fungicides, remove infected material, avoid overhead irrigation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMultiple flushes year-round in tropical conditions
Fruiting SeasonYear-round with peak production during cooler months
Days to Harvest140–170 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit lightens in color, slight give when pressed, sweet aroma
Average Yield per Plant100–250 fruits per tree per year
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination; parthenocarpy occurs
Pollination NotesSingle tree sufficient; bees enhance fruit set
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsVietnam, Southeast Asia; gaining popularity in Indian home gardens
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, ornamental garden tree
Processing UsesFresh juice, flavoring, essential oil from peel
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–100 per kg; premium for imported variety saplings

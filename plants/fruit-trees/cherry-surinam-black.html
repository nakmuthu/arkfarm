Cherry - Surinam Black

Eugenia uniflora (black variety)

Common Names: Surinam Cherry, Pitanga, Brazilian Cherry, Cayenne Cherry

Local Name: Pitanga (Portuguese/Tamil adopted)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Eastern South America (Brazil, Suriname, Guyana, Uruguay)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen shrub to small tree

Variety: Black variety — dark purple-black fruit with sweeter, less resinous flavour

Average Lifespan: 25–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitDense, compact evergreen shrub or small tree; excellent for hedging
Plant Size2–8 meters tall; spread 2–5 meters
LeavesOpposite, ovate-lanceolate, 3–7 cm long, glossy dark green; new leaves coppery-red; aromatic when crushed
FlowersWhite, 1–1.5 cm across, 4-petaled, with numerous prominent stamens; borne singly or in small clusters
Flower CharacteristicsFragrant; attract bees; bloom profusely in spring and sometimes again in autumn
FruitDistinctive ribbed (8-grooved) oblate berry, 2–4 cm diameter; thin skin, juicy aromatic flesh
Fruit ColorGreen turning deep purple to black when fully ripe (black variety)
Seed Characteristics1–3 round seeds per fruit; seeds should not be eaten (resinous)
Root SystemFibrous, moderately deep; adaptable to various soils
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; tolerates mild frost briefly
Temperature Range18–35°C ideal; tolerates brief frost to -3°C (mature plants)
Rainfall Requirement800–2000 mm annually; adaptable
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy to loamy; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing2–4 meters between plants; closer for hedging
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; moderate watering
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container plant (15–25 liters); responds well to pruning
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; spring, mid-summer, early autumn
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, fish emulsion, acidifying fertilizers
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional scale; fruit fly traps during fruiting
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemNatural form; excellent as formal or informal hedge
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting; shape as desired
How to PruneResponds excellently to pruning; hedge-trim or shape as topiary; tip-prune for bushiness
Common PestsCaribbean fruit fly, scale insects, birds
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, rust (rare); generally very disease-resistant
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; minimal intervention needed
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring (main flush); may flower again in autumn in tropics
Fruiting Season3–4 weeks after flowering; rapid fruit development
Days to Harvest21–28 days from flowering (very fast)
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black; softens; sweet aroma; falls easily from branch
Average Yield per Plant5–20 kg per plant per year
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; insect pollination
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; self-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bees attracted to fragrant white flowers
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 3–6 weeks; seedlings fruit in 2–4 years; fairly true-to-type
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings root in 6–8 weeks with rooting hormone
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on Eugenia rootstock for selected black varieties
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDense root system and hedge form prevent erosion
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; naturally pest-resistant
Companion PlantsOther Myrtaceae species, herbs, ground covers
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit attracts birds; flowers support bees; dense foliage provides nesting habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBrazil, Florida (USA), Hawaii, India, Israel, Philippines
Market UsesFresh fruit, ornamental hedge, landscaping
Processing UsesJams, jellies, juice, ice cream, liqueur
Market Value (Approx.)₹100–300 per kg (niche market); black variety commands premium
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNational fruit of Suriname; "Pitanga" from Tupi-Guarani meaning "red"
Traditional UsesLeaf tea used in Brazilian folk medicine for fever and flu; essential oil from leaves used as insect repellent

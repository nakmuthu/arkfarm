Jamun Killi Naval

Ardisia elliptica 'Killi Naval'

Common Names: Killi Naval, Jamun, Java Plum, Black Plum

Local Name: Killi Naval Pazham (Tamil), Jamun (Hindi)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Indian subcontinent; Tamil Nadu selection

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Killi Naval — Tamil Nadu local variety with small, sweet, intensely colored fruit

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge evergreen tree with dense, spreading canopy
Plant Size10–20 meters tall; canopy spread 8–15 meters
LeavesOpposite, simple, elliptic-oblong, 8–15 cm long; leathery, glossy dark green
FlowersSmall, fragrant, white to pinkish, in clusters at branch tips
Flower CharacteristicsNumerous stamens give brush-like appearance; fragrant; attract bees
FruitOblong drupe, 1.5–2.5 cm long; thin skin, juicy purple pulp, astringent-sweet
Fruit ColorDeep purple to black when ripe; stains tongue and teeth purple
Seed CharacteristicsSingle large seed (50% of fruit); green, oblong
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral roots; very stable
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; thrives in hot, humid conditions
Temperature Range25–38°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement800–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal drought
Soil TypeAdaptable; prefers deep alluvial or loamy soil; tolerates waterlogging
Soil pH Preference5.5–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing8–12 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin irrigation; minimal once established
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees in very large pots (50+ liters); not ideal long-term
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; pre-monsoon and post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (25–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, vermicompost
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; generally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for leaf miner; fruit fly traps during fruiting
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; leaf litter provides natural mulch
Training SystemModified central leader; natural form for mature trees
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (August–September)
How to PruneRemove dead and crossing branches; thin canopy for light and air
Common PestsLeaf miner, fruit fly, white fly, bark borer
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, anthracnose, sooty mold, dieback
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood sanitation, copper fungicides, remove infected parts
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May (South India)
Fruiting SeasonJune–August; coincides with monsoon
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black, softens, and develops sweet-astringent flavor
Average Yield per Plant50–150 kg per tree (mature)
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (bees, flies)
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile but cross-pollination improves yield; bees are primary pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsInsect-pollinated; fragrant flowers attract bees and flies
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 1–2 weeks (polyembryonic); seedlings fruit in 8–10 years
Propagation by CuttingsDifficult; air layering possible but slow
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood grafting or veneer grafting on seedling rootstock; fruits in 4–5 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; very drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep roots prevent erosion; excellent for riverbank stabilization
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; naturally hardy
Companion PlantsShade-tolerant crops, turmeric, ginger as intercrops
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit feeds birds, bats, and wildlife; large canopy provides habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsTamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, UP
Market UsesFresh fruit, juice, vinegar, wine
Processing UsesJuice, vinegar, wine, jam, seed powder (diabetes supplement)
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–120 per kg (seasonal)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismSacred tree in Hinduism; associated with Lord Krishna; planted near temples
Traditional UsesFruit, bark, and seeds used in Ayurveda and Siddha medicine; seed powder for diabetes management

Longan - Diamond River

Dimocarpus longan 'Diamond River'

Common Names: Diamond River Longan, Dragon Eye Fruit

Local Name: Puvatti Pazham (Tamil), Longan (Hindi/English)

Family: Sapindaceae

Origin: China/Vietnam (selected cultivar); species native to Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Diamond River — compact, self-pollinating variety with consistent bearing and sweet fruit

Average Lifespan: 40–60+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium evergreen tree with dense, rounded canopy; more compact than standard longan
Plant Size5–8 meters tall; canopy spread 4–6 meters (smaller than typical longan)
LeavesAlternate, pinnately compound, 4–8 pairs of leaflets, 6–15 cm each, dark green, leathery
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white, in large terminal panicles (20–40 cm); functionally male, female, and bisexual
Flower CharacteristicsSelf-pollinating; fragrant; attracts bees and other pollinators
FruitRound, 2–3 cm diameter; thin tan-brown shell; translucent white sweet flesh around single seed
Fruit ColorTan to light brown smooth shell; translucent white to pinkish flesh
Seed CharacteristicsSingle round, shiny black seed (resembling a dragon's eye); not edible
Root SystemModerately deep with spreading lateral roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; needs brief cool period to induce flowering
Temperature Range20–30°C ideal; needs cool nights (10–15°C) for flower induction; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement1200–2000 mm annually; dry period before flowering beneficial
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam to loam; rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (6–8 hours daily)
Spacing5–6 meters between trees (compact variety)
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation; reduce watering before flowering
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture during fruit development
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent in large containers (40–60 liters); compact habit suits container growing
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3–4 times per year; increase potassium during fruiting
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, fish emulsion, bone meal, seaweed extract
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor monthly; treat during flowering and fruiting
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, insecticidal soap, pyrethrin
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch (5–8 cm) to retain moisture
Training SystemOpen center; maintain compact form
Pruning TimeAfter harvest; tip-prune to maintain shape
How to PruneRemove dead wood; tip-prune new growth after harvest to encourage flowering wood
Common PestsLongan stink bug, fruit borer, mites, lychee erinose mite
Common DiseasesWitches' broom, leaf spot, fruit rot, powdery mildew
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation, copper fungicides, remove infected panicles, proper sanitation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsFebruary–April (after cool period)
Fruiting SeasonJune–September
Days to Harvest120–150 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsShell turns tan-brown, flesh becomes translucent and sweet, fruit detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant30–80 kg per tree per year (mature); consistent bearer
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination (Diamond River is self-fertile)
Pollination NotesDoes not require a second tree; bees improve fruit set
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; produces both male and bisexual flowers on same panicle
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 1–2 weeks; not true-to-type; seedlings take 6–8 years to fruit
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering (marcotting) is common and effective
Propagation by GraftingCleft or veneer grafting on seedling rootstock; fruits in 2–3 years
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; efficient with drip irrigation
Soil Conservation PracticesEvergreen canopy provides year-round cover; mulching prevents erosion
Organic Practices UsedComposting, biological pest control, cover cropping
Companion PlantsLeguminous cover crops, lemongrass, marigolds
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract bees and pollinators; fruit eaten by birds
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsChina (Guangdong, Fujian), Thailand, Vietnam, Australia, USA (Florida, Hawaii)
Market UsesFresh fruit, dried longan, canned in syrup
Processing UsesDried longan (gui yuan), longan honey, beverages, desserts, traditional medicine
Market Value (Approx.)₹200–500 per kg (fresh); dried longan commands higher prices
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismCalled "Dragon Eye" (龙眼) in Chinese; symbol of good luck and fertility
Traditional UsesDried longan used in Traditional Chinese Medicine as blood tonic; fruit in festive desserts

Cherry - Red Surinam

Eugenia uniflora (red variety)

Common Names: Red Surinam Cherry, Pitanga, Brazilian Cherry, Cayenne Cherry

Local Name: Pitanga (Portuguese), Cereza de Cayena (Spanish)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Eastern South America (Brazil, Suriname, Guyana, French Guiana)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen shrub to small tree

Variety: Red variety — bright red ribbed fruit with tangy, slightly resinous flavour

Average Lifespan: 25–40 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitDense, multi-branched evergreen shrub or small tree; commonly used as ornamental hedge
Plant Size2–8 meters tall; spread 2–5 meters
LeavesOpposite, ovate to lanceolate, 3–7 cm long, glossy dark green; new growth coppery-bronze; aromatic when crushed
FlowersWhite, 4-petaled, 1–1.5 cm across with numerous prominent stamens; borne singly or in small axillary clusters
Flower CharacteristicsMildly fragrant; attract bees and other pollinators; bloom profusely in spring with possible autumn flush
FruitDistinctive 8-ribbed oblate berry, 2–4 cm diameter; thin edible skin enclosing juicy, aromatic, slightly resinous flesh
Fruit ColorGreen turning orange then bright red when ripe
Seed Characteristics1–3 round seeds per fruit; resinous, not consumed
Root SystemFibrous, moderately deep; adaptable to various soil types
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to warm subtropical; tolerates mild frost briefly
Temperature Range18–35°C ideal; mature plants tolerate brief frost to −3°C
Rainfall Requirement800–2000 mm annually; adaptable to varying rainfall
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy to loamy soil; tolerates poor and slightly acidic soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing2–4 meters apart; closer (1–1.5 m) for hedging
Irrigation MethodDrip irrigation or hand watering; moderate needs
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container plant in 15–25 liter pots; responds well to pruning
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; spring, mid-summer, and early autumn
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost, fish emulsion, acidifying organic fertilizers
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional scale insects; fruit fly traps during fruiting
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemNatural form or formal hedge; responds well to shaping
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting season; light pruning any time
How to PruneTip-prune for bushiness; hedge-trim for formal shape; remove dead or crossing branches
Common PestsCaribbean fruit fly, scale insects, birds
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, rust (rare); generally very disease-resistant
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood air circulation; remove fallen fruit; minimal intervention needed
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsSpring (main flush); may flower again in autumn in tropical climates
Fruiting Season3–4 weeks after flowering; very rapid fruit development
Days to Harvest21–28 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns bright red; softens slightly; sweet aroma; detaches easily
Average Yield per Plant5–20 kg per plant per year (mature)
Pollination TypeSelf-fertile; insect-pollinated
Pollination NotesBees are primary pollinators; cross-pollination improves fruit set and yield
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; bees attracted to fragrant white flowers
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate in 3–6 weeks; seedlings fruit in 2–4 years; fairly true-to-type for red variety
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings with rooting hormone; root in 6–8 weeks
Propagation by GraftingVeneer grafting on Eugenia rootstock for selected cultivars
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; drought-tolerant once established
Soil Conservation PracticesDense root system and hedge form prevent soil erosion
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; naturally pest-resistant; ideal for organic gardens
Companion PlantsOther Myrtaceae species, herbs, ground covers
Biodiversity BenefitsFruit attracts birds; flowers support bees; dense foliage provides nesting habitat
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBrazil, Florida (USA), Hawaii, India, Philippines, Israel
Market UsesFresh fruit, ornamental hedge, landscaping
Processing UsesJams, jellies, juice, syrups, ice cream, liqueur
Market Value (Approx.)₹80–250 per kg (niche tropical fruit market)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNational fruit of Suriname; "Pitanga" from Tupi-Guarani meaning "red"
Traditional UsesLeaf tea used in Brazilian folk medicine for fever, flu, and stomach ailments; leaf essential oil used as insect repellent

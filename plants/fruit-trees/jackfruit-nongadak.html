Jackfruit - Nongadak

Artocarpus heterophyllus 'Nongadak'

Common Names: Nongadak Jackfruit, Meghalaya Jackfruit

Local Name: Nongadak (Khasi), Kathal (Hindi/Assamese)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: Meghalaya, Northeast India (indigenous selection from Garo and Khasi Hills)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Nongadak — Meghalaya selection with small to medium fruits, intensely sweet and aromatic bulbs

Average Lifespan: 60–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large evergreen tree; vigorous growth in humid hill conditions
Plant Size10–18 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesAlternate, elliptic, 10–22 cm, dark green, leathery, glossy
FlowersMonoecious; cauliflorous inflorescences on trunk and main branches
Flower CharacteristicsMale spikes cylindrical; female spikes barrel-shaped; borne on older wood
FruitSmall to medium, 5–12 kg; round to oblong; very sweet, aromatic, firm bulbs with minimal latex
Fruit ColorGreen rind with short spines; bright golden-yellow to orange bulbs
Seed CharacteristicsMedium oval seeds; edible when cooked; fewer seeds per fruit
Root SystemDeep taproot with lateral roots; adapted to hilly terrain
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateSubtropical to tropical; adapted to high-rainfall hill regions
Temperature Range20–32°C ideal; tolerates cooler nights of hill stations
Rainfall Requirement2000–4000 mm annually (Meghalaya is one of the wettest regions)
Soil TypeWell-drained acidic laterite or red loamy soil; hill slopes preferred
Soil pH Preference5.0–6.5 (adapted to acidic soils)
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade (6+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodRainfed in Meghalaya; supplemental irrigation in drier regions
Water RequirementModerate; naturally receives abundant rainfall in native habitat
Can it Grow in Pots?Young trees only; requires ground planting for fruiting
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule2–3 times per year; organic inputs preferred in traditional cultivation
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, compost, leaf mold, wood ash, bone meal
Pesticide FrequencyMinimal; traditional cultivation uses few chemicals
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, wood ash spray, tobacco decoction (traditional)
Mulching PracticeNatural leaf litter mulch; forest floor mimicry
Training SystemNatural form; minimal training in traditional orchards
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (September–October)
How to PruneRemove dead and diseased branches; light thinning for air circulation
Common PestsShoot borer, fruit borer, mealybug, bark caterpillar
Common DiseasesRhizopus rot, leaf spot, pink disease (exacerbated by high humidity)
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage on slopes, sanitation, copper fungicides, remove infected material
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsJanuary–March
Fruiting SeasonMay–August; peak during monsoon season
Days to Harvest100–140 days from pollination
Harvest IndicatorsHollow sound when tapped, sweet aroma, spines flatten, skin lightens
Average Yield per Plant80–150 fruits per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeWind and insect pollination
Pollination NotesMonoecious; wind is primary pollinator; good natural fruit set in humid conditions
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsWind-pollinated; high humidity aids pollen dispersal
Propagation by SeedsTraditional method in Meghalaya; seedlings show variability
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering possible; used by progressive farmers
Propagation by GraftingSoftwood grafting recommended to maintain Nongadak quality traits
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow (rainfed); naturally sustained by high rainfall
Soil Conservation PracticesGrown on hill slopes; root system prevents erosion; part of agroforestry systems
Organic Practices UsedTraditionally organic; minimal external inputs
Companion PlantsBetel nut, black pepper, turmeric, ginger in mixed orchards
Biodiversity BenefitsPart of diverse agroforestry; habitat for birds, insects, and small mammals
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsMeghalaya (Garo Hills, Khasi Hills), Assam, Tripura, Nagaland
Market UsesFresh fruit, local markets, tribal festivals; growing demand outside Northeast
Processing UsesDried jackfruit, chips, traditional preserves, wine (local)
Market Value (Approx.)₹40–120 per kg; premium for Nongadak variety in specialty markets
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismImportant food tree in Khasi and Garo tribal culture; part of traditional agroforestry
Traditional UsesFruit in tribal cuisine; wood for construction; seeds as staple food; bark for traditional dyes

Mango - Bangalore

Mangifera indica 'Bangalore'

Common Names: Bangalore Mango, Thothapuri, Totapuri, Collector, Kallamai

Local Name: Thothapuri (Tamil/Kannada), Totapuri (Hindi), Ginimoothi (Telugu)

Family: Anacardiaceae

Origin: Karnataka, India

Plant Type: Large evergreen tree, 10–15 meters tall

Variety: Thothapuri — distinctive parrot-beak shape, dual-purpose (table and processing)

Average Lifespan: 40–100+ years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLarge, vigorous tree with upright to spreading canopy
Plant Size10–15 meters tall; canopy spread 8–12 meters
LeavesSimple, alternate, lanceolate, 15–30 cm long; new leaves pinkish-red turning dark green
FlowersSmall, yellowish-white in large terminal panicles (20–40 cm long)
Flower CharacteristicsBoth male and bisexual flowers on same panicle; mildly fragrant
FruitMedium to large, 250–450 g; distinctive elongated shape with pointed beak (parrot-beak); firm flesh, mildly sweet with slight tang
Fruit ColorGreen turning bright yellow with green tinge when ripe; prominent beak tip
Seed CharacteristicsLarge polyembryonic seed; commonly used as rootstock for grafting other varieties
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system; very hardy and drought-tolerant
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; adaptable to various conditions
Temperature Range24–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 45°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement750–2500 mm annually; dry spell before flowering improves yield
Soil TypeAdaptable; grows in laterite, red loam, alluvial, and even rocky soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun (8+ hours daily)
Spacing8–10 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodBasin or drip irrigation; reduce watering during flowering
Water RequirementLow to moderate; very drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Young grafted trees in large pots (50+ liters); vigorous grower, will need root pruning
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer Schedule3 times per year — pre-flowering, post-fruit set, post-harvest
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM (25–50 kg/tree/year), neem cake, bone meal, vermicompost
Pesticide Frequency2–3 sprays during flowering and fruiting season
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil, panchagavya, garlic-chili spray, fruit fly traps
Mulching PracticeOrganic mulch around base; keep away from trunk
Training SystemOpen center or modified leader system
Pruning TimeAfter harvest (July–August)
How to PruneRemove dead, diseased, and crossing branches; tip-prune for compact growth; thin canopy center
Common PestsMango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealybug, leaf webber, stone weevil
Common DiseasesAnthracnose, powdery mildew, bacterial canker, sooty mold
Disease Prevention & RemediesCopper-based fungicides, proper spacing, remove fallen debris, good sanitation
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsDecember–February (South India)
Fruiting SeasonMarch–June; early to mid-season variety
Days to Harvest90–110 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns yellow-green; shoulders fill out; firm flesh; slight aroma at stem end
Average Yield per Plant200–400 fruits per tree (mature); relatively regular bearer
Pollination TypeCross-pollination by insects (flies, bees)
Pollination NotesGood fruit set compared to other varieties; houseflies are primary pollinators
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsKarnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Maharashtra
Market UsesProcessing (pulp industry mainstay), table fruit, pickle (raw mango)
Processing UsesMango pulp (Totapuri pulp is India's largest export), juice, canned slices, pickle, dried mango
Market Value (Approx.)₹30–80 per kg; lower than dessert varieties but huge volume; backbone of India's mango pulp export industry

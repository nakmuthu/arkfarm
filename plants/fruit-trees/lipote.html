Lipote

Syzygium polycephaloides

Common Names: Lipote, Philippine Lipote

Local Name: Lipote (Filipino/Tagalog)

Family: Myrtaceae

Origin: Philippines (endemic)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree

Variety: Wild species — small dark purple berry

Average Lifespan: 40–60 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium to large evergreen tree with dense rounded canopy
Plant Size10–20 meters tall
LeavesOpposite, elliptic to oblong, 8–15 cm long, glossy dark green, leathery
FlowersSmall, white to cream, clustered in dense terminal or axillary cymes
Flower CharacteristicsNumerous stamens give fluffy appearance; mildly fragrant; attract bees
FruitSmall round berry, 1–2 cm diameter, juicy flesh with mild sweet-tart flavour
Fruit ColorDark purple to almost black when ripe
Seed CharacteristicsSingle round seed per fruit; recalcitrant (short viability)
Root SystemDeep taproot with lateral spreading roots
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical lowland to mid-elevation; humid conditions
Temperature Range24–34°C ideal; frost-intolerant
Rainfall Requirement1500–3000 mm annually; prefers consistent moisture
Soil TypeRich, well-drained loamy to clay-loam soil
Soil pH Preference5.5–6.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing6–8 meters between trees
Irrigation MethodRain-fed in native habitat; supplemental watering in dry periods
Water RequirementModerate to high; prefers consistently moist soil
Can it Grow in Pots?Not ideal — grows too large; young plants possible in large containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleTwice yearly with organic matter
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, well-rotted manure, leaf mould
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil if needed
Mulching PracticeThick organic mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemNatural form; minimal training required
Pruning TimeAfter fruiting season
How to PruneLight pruning to remove dead branches and maintain shape
Common PestsFruit bats, birds (fruit predators rather than insect pests)
Common DiseasesLeaf spot, root rot in waterlogged conditions
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, avoid waterlogging, remove infected foliage
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsMarch–May
Fruiting SeasonJune–August
Days to Harvest60–90 days from flowering
Harvest IndicatorsFruit turns deep purple-black, softens slightly, sweet aroma
Average Yield per Plant30–60 kg per tree per year (mature tree)
Pollination TypeInsect pollination (bees, flies)
Pollination NotesSelf-fertile; bees are primary pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; insect-pollinated; flowers attract native bees
Propagation by SeedsFresh seeds germinate in 2–4 weeks; must be sown immediately (recalcitrant)
Propagation by CuttingsAir layering (marcotting) is the preferred method; roots in 6–10 weeks
Propagation by GraftingApproach grafting on Syzygium rootstock possible
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; adapted to tropical rainfall patterns
Soil Conservation PracticesDense canopy and root system prevent erosion
Organic Practices UsedMinimal inputs needed; grows naturally in forest settings
Companion PlantsOther native Philippine fruit trees, ferns, shade-tolerant crops
Biodiversity BenefitsImportant food source for Philippine fruit bats and birds; supports forest biodiversity
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsPhilippines (Luzon, Visayas, Mindanao); limited cultivation elsewhere
Market UsesFresh fruit, local markets, wine-making
Processing UsesJams, jellies, wine, vinegar
Market Value (Approx.)₱50–100 per kg (Philippines); rarely traded internationally
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismRepresents Philippine native biodiversity; valued as heritage fruit
Traditional UsesFruit eaten fresh or made into wine by indigenous communities; bark used in folk medicine

Curtain Creeper

Tarlmounia elliptica

Common Names: Curtain Creeper, Parda Bel, Green Curtain Vine

Local Name: Parda Kodi (Tamil), Parda Bel (Hindi)

Family: Asteraceae

Origin: India, Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Perennial woody climbing vine

Average Lifespan: Perennial; 10–15 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitFast-growing vine; long pendulous branches hang like a curtain
Plant SizeClimbs 6–10 meters; hanging branches 2–4 meters long
LeavesElliptic, 4–8 cm, glossy dark green; dense coverage
FlowersSmall, white, daisy-like; in clusters; mildly fragrant
Root SystemWoody, deep roots; anchors firmly once established
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical
Temperature Range18–38°C; frost-sensitive
Soil TypeAny well-drained soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Not ideal; best planted in ground near a wall or pergola
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleLight feeding 2–3 times a year; compost mulch annually
Pruning TimeAfter flowering; trim to control spread
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant; occasional aphids
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root easily; preferred method
Propagation by SeedsSeeds viable; germinate in 2–3 weeks
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; very low maintenance once established
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides dense green cover; reduces heat; attracts insects

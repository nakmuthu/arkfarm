Moses-in-the-Cradle

Tradescantia spathacea

Common Names: Moses-in-the-Cradle, Boat Lily, Oyster Plant, Rhoeo

Local Name: Moses-in-the-Cradle (Tamil/Hindi)

Family: Commelinaceae

Origin: Mexico, Belize, Guatemala

Plant Type: Perennial rosette-forming herb

Average Lifespan: Many years; spreads by offsets

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitRosette-forming; spreads by producing offsets at base
Plant Size30–60 cm tall; spreads to form dense clumps
LeavesSword-shaped, 20–35 cm long; glossy green above, rich purple beneath; stiff and upright
FlowersSmall, white, three-petaled; nestled in boat-shaped purple bracts at leaf axils — giving the "Moses in a cradle" appearance
Root SystemShallow fibrous; spreads by stolons producing new rosettes
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; very adaptable
Temperature Range15–35°C; frost-sensitive
Soil TypeWell-drained, moderately fertile soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to deep shade; purple color intensifies in brighter light
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container and hanging basket plant
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly during growing season; minimal feeding needed
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, diluted worm castings
Common PestsMealybugs, scale insects; generally pest-resistant
Common DiseasesRoot rot from overwatering; leaf spot in humid conditions
Pruning TimeRemove dead outer leaves; divide clumps when overcrowded
How to PrunePull off dead leaves at base; separate offsets for propagation
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsSeparate offsets from base and replant; roots within 1–2 weeks
💰 Economic Importance
Market UsesGround cover, border plant, indoor ornamental, container plant
Market Value (Approx.)₹50–200 per plant or clump
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNamed for the biblical story of Moses in the bulrushes; the white flowers nestled in purple bracts resemble a baby in a cradle
Traditional UsesUsed in traditional medicine in Mexico for respiratory conditions; widely used as ornamental ground cover in tropical gardens

Purple Heart

Tradescantia pallida

Common Names: Purple Heart, Purple Queen, Wandering Jew (purple)

Local Name: Ootha Ilai Chedi (Tamil)

Family: Commelinaceae

Origin: Eastern Mexico

Plant Type: Perennial herbaceous ground cover / trailing plant

Average Lifespan: Perennial; spreads indefinitely

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitLow-growing trailing or spreading succulent herb; 20–40 cm tall
Plant Size20–40 cm tall; spreads 60–90 cm wide
LeavesLance-shaped, 7–15 cm, deep purple on both sides; slightly succulent
FlowersSmall, 3-petalled, pink to magenta; 1–1.5 cm diameter
Root SystemShallow fibrous roots; stems root at nodes
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range15–35°C; best colour in full sun
Soil TypeWell-drained; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade; deeper purple in full sun
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes; excellent in hanging baskets and containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleLight feeding monthly; excess fertilizer reduces purple colour
Pruning TimePinch tips regularly to encourage bushy growth
Common PestsSpider mites, mealybugs; generally pest-resistant
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root easily in soil or water within 1–2 weeks
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; drought-tolerant ground cover
Soil Conservation PracticesExcellent ground cover; prevents soil erosion

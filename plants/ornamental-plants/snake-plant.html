Snake Plant

Sansevieria trifasciata

Common Names: Snake Plant, Mother-in-Law's Tongue, Saint George's Sword, Viper's Bowstring Hemp

Local Name: Pambu Kalli (Tamil), Nagphani (Hindi)

Family: Asparagaceae

Origin: West Africa (Nigeria to Congo)

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen succulent herb

Average Lifespan: 5–10 years; spreads indefinitely via rhizomes

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitUpright, clumping succulent; stiff sword-shaped leaves growing from a basal rosette
Plant Size30–120 cm tall depending on variety
LeavesStiff, upright, sword-shaped; dark green with light grey-green horizontal bands; yellow-edged in some varieties
FlowersSmall, tubular, white to cream; fragrant; on tall spikes; rarely flowers indoors
Root SystemShallow, spreading rhizomes; orange-yellow roots; drought-tolerant
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates a wide range of conditions
Temperature Range15–35°C; frost-sensitive below 5°C
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy or loamy soil; does not tolerate waterlogging
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.5
Sun RequirementLow light to bright indirect light; tolerates shade; avoid harsh direct sun
Water RequirementVery low; allow soil to dry completely between watering; overwatering causes root rot
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes; one of the best indoor container plants; thrives in neglect
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleOnce or twice a year with diluted balanced fertilizer; minimal feeding needed
Common PestsMealybugs, spider mites, scale insects; generally very pest-resistant
Common DiseasesRoot rot from overwatering; leaf spot in humid conditions
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsLeaf cuttings in water or soil; division of rhizomes; very easy to propagate
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; one of the most drought-tolerant ornamental plants
Biodiversity BenefitsNASA-listed air purifier; removes formaldehyde, benzene, and CO2; produces oxygen at night

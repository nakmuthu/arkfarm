Giant Pothos

Epipremnum aureum

Common Names: Giant Pothos, Golden Pothos, Devil's Ivy, Money Plant

Local Name: Money Plant (Tamil/Hindi), Pothos (common)

Family: Araceae

Origin: Mo'orea, French Polynesia; naturalized throughout tropics

Plant Type: Evergreen climbing or trailing vine

Average Lifespan: Essentially indefinite with proper care

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitVigorous climbing or trailing vine; leaves become very large (up to 1 meter) when climbing in tropical conditions
Plant SizeIndoors: 1–3 meters; outdoors in tropics: can climb 20+ meters
LeavesHeart-shaped, waxy, green with yellow variegation; juvenile leaves 10–15 cm; mature climbing leaves 30–100 cm, often fenestrated
Root SystemAerial roots at nodes for climbing; fibrous soil roots for nutrition
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; extremely adaptable
Temperature Range15–35°C; tolerates brief periods down to 10°C
Soil TypeWell-draining potting mix; tolerates a wide range of soil types
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementLow to bright indirect light; tolerates very low light conditions
Irrigation MethodWater when top 2–3 cm of soil is dry; very drought-tolerant
Water RequirementLow to moderate; one of the most drought-tolerant houseplants
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, ideal in pots, hanging baskets, or trained on a moss pole
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly during growing season; minimal in winter
Recommended Organic FertilizersDiluted worm castings, compost tea, fish emulsion
Common PestsMealybugs, scale insects, spider mites (rare)
Common DiseasesRoot rot (overwatering), bacterial leaf spot
Disease Prevention & RemediesWell-draining soil, avoid overwatering, good air circulation
Pruning TimeYear-round; trim to control size and encourage bushiness
How to PruneCut stems just below a node; cuttings can be propagated in water
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings with 1–2 nodes root easily in water or soil in 2–3 weeks; one of the easiest plants to propagate
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; one of the most water-efficient houseplants
Biodiversity BenefitsExcellent air purifier; removes benzene, formaldehyde, and carbon monoxide from indoor air
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsProduced globally; one of the most widely sold houseplants worldwide
Market UsesIndoor ornamental, office plant, hanging basket, vertical garden
Market Value (Approx.)₹50–300 per plant; very affordable and widely available
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismCalled "Money Plant" in India; believed to bring prosperity and good luck in feng shui
Traditional UsesWidely kept in homes and offices for good luck; caution — toxic to pets and children if ingested

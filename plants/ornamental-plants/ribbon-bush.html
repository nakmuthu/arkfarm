Ribbon Bush

Homalocladium platycladum

Common Names: Ribbon Bush, Tapeworm Plant, Centipede Plant, Solomon's Island Vine

Local Name: Ribbon Bush (Tamil/Hindi)

Family: Polygonaceae

Origin: Solomon Islands, Pacific

Plant Type: Evergreen ornamental shrub

Average Lifespan: Many years; long-lived perennial

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitUpright to arching shrub with flat, ribbon-like jointed stems (phylloclades) that function as leaves
Plant Size1–2 meters tall
LeavesTrue leaves tiny and quickly shed; photosynthesis performed by flat, bright green, jointed stem segments (phylloclades) 5–15 cm long
FlowersTiny, pink-white, borne along edges of stem segments; followed by small red-purple berries
Root SystemFibrous, moderately deep; well-anchored
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical and subtropical; warm and humid
Temperature Range15–35°C; frost-sensitive
Soil TypeWell-drained, moderately fertile soil
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementModerate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container plant with unique texture
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly during growing season
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, worm castings
Common PestsScale insects, mealybugs; generally pest-resistant
Common DiseasesRoot rot in waterlogged conditions
Pruning TimePrune to shape in spring; tolerates moderate pruning
How to PruneCut stems to desired shape; remove dead or damaged segments
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root easily in moist soil; 3–4 weeks
Propagation by SeedsSeeds from berries germinate readily
💰 Economic Importance
Market UsesNovelty ornamental, container plant, tropical garden accent
Market Value (Approx.)₹100–300 per plant in tropical nurseries

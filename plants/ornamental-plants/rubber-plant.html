Rubber Plant

Ficus elastica

Common Names: Rubber Plant, Rubber Fig, Rubber Tree, Indian Rubber Bush

Local Name: Rubber Maram (Tamil), Rubber Ka Ped (Hindi)

Family: Moraceae

Origin: Eastern Himalayas, Assam, Malaysia, Java

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen tree / ornamental shrub

Average Lifespan: Decades; up to 100 years in ground

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitUpright tree with broad spreading canopy; aerial roots in humid conditions
Plant SizeUp to 30–40 meters in wild; 1–3 meters as indoor/container plant
LeavesLarge, glossy, oval; 10–35 cm long; dark green (burgundy in some varieties)
Root SystemExtensive surface roots; produces aerial roots in high humidity
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; frost-sensitive
Temperature Range15–30°C; avoid cold drafts
Soil TypeWell-drained, fertile loamy soil
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementBright indirect light; tolerates low light indoors
Water RequirementModerate; allow top soil to dry between watering
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes; one of the most popular indoor foliage plants
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly during growing season; reduce in winter
Pruning TimeSpring; wear gloves — milky latex is a skin irritant
Common PestsSpider mites, scale insects, mealybugs
Common DiseasesRoot rot from overwatering; leaf spot
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings or air layering; root in 3–4 weeks
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Biodiversity BenefitsExcellent air purifier; removes formaldehyde and CO2

Wandering Jew

Tradescantia zebrina

Common Names: Wandering Jew, Inch Plant, Silver Inch Plant, Zebrina

Local Name: Wandering Jew (Tamil/Hindi)

Family: Commelinaceae

Origin: Mexico and Central America

Plant Type: Perennial trailing herb

Average Lifespan: Many years; essentially indefinite with propagation

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitTrailing and spreading; roots at nodes; fast-growing
Plant Size15–30 cm tall; spreads indefinitely as ground cover
LeavesOval, 5–8 cm long; upper surface green with two silver stripes; underside deep purple; waxy sheen
FlowersSmall, pink-purple, three-petaled; appear at stem tips
Root SystemShallow fibrous; roots readily at stem nodes
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; very adaptable
Temperature Range15–35°C; frost-sensitive
Soil TypeWell-draining potting mix; tolerates most soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementBright indirect light to partial shade; more light intensifies purple coloration
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant; allow to dry slightly between waterings
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, ideal in hanging baskets and pots
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly during growing season; minimal feeding needed
Recommended Organic FertilizersDiluted worm castings, compost tea
Common PestsSpider mites, aphids (in dry conditions)
Common DiseasesRoot rot from overwatering; leaf spot in humid conditions
Pruning TimeYear-round; pinch tips to encourage bushy growth
How to PrunePinch growing tips; cut back leggy stems; cuttings can be propagated
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root in water or soil within 1–2 weeks; one of the easiest plants to propagate
💰 Economic Importance
Market UsesGround cover, hanging basket, indoor ornamental, border plant
Market Value (Approx.)₹30–100 per cutting or small pot
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismNamed for its wandering growth habit; symbol of resilience and adaptability
Traditional UsesUsed in traditional medicine in Mexico for skin conditions; widely used as ornamental ground cover

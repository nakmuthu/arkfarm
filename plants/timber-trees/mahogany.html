Timber - Mahogany

Swietenia macrophylla

Common Names: Mahogany, Big-leaf Mahogany, Honduras Mahogany

Local Name: Mahogany (Tamil), Mahogany (Hindi)

Family: Meliaceae

Origin: Central and South America

Plant Type: Large deciduous tree, 30–40 meters tall

Variety: Swietenia macrophylla (Big-leaf); related species: S. mahagoni, S. humilis

Average Lifespan: 100–350 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitTall, straight-trunked deciduous tree with a broad, spreading crown
Plant Size30–40 meters tall; trunk diameter up to 2 meters
LeavesPinnately compound, 12–45 cm long, 3–6 pairs of leaflets; dark green, glossy
FlowersSmall, greenish-yellow, fragrant, borne in axillary panicles
Flower Characteristics5-petaled, 8–10 mm diameter; blooms during dry season
FruitLarge woody capsule, 12–22 cm long, pear-shaped, splits from base when ripe
Fruit ColorGrey-brown when mature
Seed CharacteristicsWinged seeds (samaras), 35–45 per capsule; wind-dispersed
Root SystemDeep taproot with extensive lateral root system; good wind resistance
BarkDark brown to grey, deeply fissured and scaly on mature trees
WoodReddish-brown heartwood; fine, even grain; excellent workability; naturally durable
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; prefers warm, humid conditions
Temperature Range20–35°C ideal; sensitive to frost; tolerates brief dry spells
Rainfall Requirement1000–2500 mm annually; tolerates seasonal drought
Soil TypeDeep, well-drained fertile soil; alluvial or volcanic soils preferred
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun; shade-tolerant when young
Spacing8–12 meters in plantations; wider in agroforestry systems
Irrigation MethodRain-fed in most regions; supplemental irrigation in dry areas during establishment
Water RequirementModerate to high during establishment; drought-tolerant once mature
Can it Grow in Pots?Only as a young seedling; not suitable for long-term container growing
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleAnnual application during first 5 years; minimal once established
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, farmyard manure, green manure cover crops
Pesticide FrequencyMonitor for shoot borer; preventive measures recommended
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem-based formulations for shoot borer management
Mulching PracticeMulch around young trees to retain moisture and suppress weeds
Training SystemSingle-stem training; remove lower branches to promote straight bole
Pruning TimeDry season; formative pruning in first 5–8 years
How to PruneRemove lower branches and competing leaders; maintain single straight trunk for timber quality
Common PestsMahogany shoot borer (Hypsipyla robusta/grandella) — major pest worldwide
Common DiseasesRoot rot in waterlogged soils; leaf spot; occasional canker
Disease Prevention & RemediesMixed planting to reduce shoot borer; good drainage; avoid monoculture plantations
💧 Environmental Impact
Carbon SequestrationExcellent carbon sink; large trees store significant amounts of carbon in wood
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep root system prevents erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides habitat for birds, insects, and epiphytes; supports forest ecosystem
Agroforestry PotentialExcellent shade tree for coffee, cacao, and other understory crops
Conservation StatusCITES Appendix II — regulated international trade due to overexploitation
Sustainable PracticesPlantation-grown mahogany reduces pressure on wild populations; FSC certification available
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsBrazil, Peru, Bolivia (native); India, Indonesia, Fiji, Philippines (planted)
Market UsesPremium furniture, cabinetry, boat building, musical instruments, decorative veneers
Processing UsesSawn timber, veneer, plywood; bark used in traditional medicine
Market Value (Approx.)₹2,000–5,000 per cubic foot for plantation-grown timber; wild mahogany commands premium prices
Harvest Cycle25–50 years for timber harvest depending on growing conditions
Timber QualitiesReddish-brown color, fine grain, excellent durability, easy to work, takes polish beautifully

Aloe Vera - Red

Aloe cameronii

Common Names: Red Aloe, Cameron's Ruwari Aloe

Local Name: சிவப்பு கற்றாழை (Tamil), Lal Ghritkumari (Hindi)

Family: Asphodelaceae

Origin: Zimbabwe, Malawi

Plant Type: Perennial succulent

Average Lifespan: Perennial; 5–20 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitRosette-forming succulent; turns vivid red in full sun or drought stress
Plant Size30–60 cm tall
LeavesFleshy, lanceolate; green in shade, turning copper-red to bright red in full sun
FlowersTubular, orange-red; on branched spikes; attractive to sunbirds
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; spreads via offsets
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates arid and semi-arid conditions
Temperature Range10–40°C
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy or rocky soil; does not tolerate waterlogging
Soil pH Preference6.5–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun for best red coloration; tolerates partial shade
Water RequirementVery low; drought-tolerant; water sparingly
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent ornamental container plant
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMinimal; occasional diluted fertilizer; excess nitrogen reduces red coloration
Common PestsRoot rot from overwatering; mealybugs occasionally
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsOffsets from base of plant; allow cut end to callous before planting
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; ideal for xeriscaping and water-wise gardens
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract sunbirds and bees; striking ornamental value in dry gardens
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesSimilar properties to Aloe vera; gel used for burns, skin conditions, and wound healing
Digestive HealthGel used for digestive support similar to common Aloe vera
Immune SupportPrimarily ornamental; medicinal properties similar to Aloe vera; turns red in full sun

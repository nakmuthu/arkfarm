Vetiver / Cuscus Grass

Chrysopogon zizanioides

Common Names: Vetiver, Cuscus Grass, Khus Grass

Local Name: வெட்டிவேர் (Tamil), Khus (Hindi)

Family: Poaceae

Origin: India

Plant Type: Perennial grass

Average Lifespan: Perennial; roots harvested after 12–18 months

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitDense, clumping perennial grass; forms large tufts
Plant Size1–2 meters tall
LeavesLong, narrow, stiff; bluish-green; aromatic when crushed
FlowersBrownish-purple spikelets on branched panicles
Root SystemDeep, spongy, aromatic roots; can reach 3–4 meters deep; excellent soil binder
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates extreme heat and drought
Temperature Range15–45°C
Soil TypeAdaptable to most soils; tolerates poor, acidic, and alkaline soils
Soil pH Preference4.0–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun
Water RequirementLow; highly drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in deep containers to accommodate long roots
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMinimal; occasional compost improves root yield
Common PestsVery pest-resistant; aromatic roots deter soil pests
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsClump division; split and replant rooted slips during monsoon season
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; one of the most drought-tolerant grasses; used for soil and water conservation
Biodiversity BenefitsPrevents soil erosion; deep roots improve soil structure; used in watershed management
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesRoots used for cooling the body, fever, and skin conditions; anti-inflammatory properties
Digestive HealthRoot decoction used for digestive issues, urinary disorders, and as a cooling drink in summer
Anxiety & SleepAromatic roots used in perfumery; vetiver essential oil used for stress relief and relaxation

Moringa Tree

Moringa oleifera

Common Names: Moringa, Drumstick Tree, Horseradish Tree, Ben Oil Tree

Local Name: Murungai (Tamil), Sahjan (Hindi), Munaga (Telugu)

Family: Moringaceae

Origin: Northwestern India (sub-Himalayan regions)

Plant Type: Fast-growing deciduous tree

Variety: PKM-1, PKM-2 (high-yielding), Jaffna, ODC-3

Average Lifespan: 20–30 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitFast-growing, slender deciduous tree; can grow 3–5 meters in first year
Plant Size5–12 meters tall; open, drooping canopy
LeavesTripinnately compound, small oval leaflets (1–2 cm), feathery appearance
FlowersFragrant, creamy-white, 2.5 cm across, in drooping panicles
Flower Characteristics5 unequal petals, mildly sweet fragrance; attracts bees
FruitLong, slender, triangular pods (drumsticks), 20–60 cm long
Fruit ColorGreen when young, turning brown when mature and dry
Seed CharacteristicsRound, 3-winged seeds (1 cm); 10–20 seeds per pod; contain ben oil
Root SystemThick, tuberous taproot; white, pungent (horseradish-like)
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; highly drought-tolerant
Temperature Range25–35°C ideal; tolerates up to 48°C; frost-sensitive
Rainfall Requirement250–1500 mm annually; thrives even in arid conditions
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy loam; tolerates poor soils; avoid waterlogging
Soil pH Preference6.3–7.0 (tolerates 5.0–9.0)
Sun RequirementFull sun (6+ hours daily)
Spacing3–5 meters for tree form; 1 meter for intensive leaf harvest
Irrigation MethodDrip or flood irrigation; minimal once established
Water RequirementLow; extremely drought-tolerant
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large pots (20+ liters); keep pruned for compact growth
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleAt planting and every 3–4 months; light feeding sufficient
Recommended Organic FertilizersFYM, vermicompost, neem cake; moringa is not a heavy feeder
Pesticide FrequencyRarely needed; moringa is naturally pest-resistant
Recommended Organic PesticidesNeem oil for occasional caterpillar or aphid issues
Mulching PracticeLight mulch to retain moisture in dry areas
Training SystemPollard or coppice for leaf production; single leader for pods
Pruning TimeAfter each harvest cycle; promotes branching
How to PruneCut back to 1–1.5 meters for bushy regrowth; tip-prune for branching
Common PestsPod borer, hairy caterpillar, bark caterpillar (generally minor)
Common DiseasesRoot rot (in waterlogged soil), dieback, fruit rot
Disease Prevention & RemediesEnsure good drainage; avoid overwatering; remove infected parts
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsJanuary–March (South India); can flower year-round in tropics
Fruiting SeasonMarch–June (main season); some varieties fruit twice a year
Days to HarvestPods: 60–90 days from flowering; Leaves: harvestable from 60 days after planting
Harvest IndicatorsPods snap easily when bent; tender green for cooking
Average Yield per Plant300–400 pods per tree per year (mature); 6–10 kg leaves per harvest
Pollination TypeSelf-pollination and insect pollination
Pollination NotesBees, sunbirds, and other insects are pollinators
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Pollination DetailsSelf-fertile; cross-pollination by insects improves yield
Propagation by SeedsDirect sowing or nursery; germinates in 5–12 days; fruits in 6–8 months
Propagation by CuttingsHardwood cuttings (1–1.5 m, 4–5 cm thick); plant directly; fruits in 6 months
Propagation by GraftingNot commonly practiced for moringa
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; one of the most water-efficient food trees
Soil Conservation PracticesDeep taproot prevents erosion; leaf litter enriches soil
Organic Practices UsedMoringa leaf extract used as natural plant growth enhancer
Companion PlantsLegumes, vegetables (moringa provides light shade)
Biodiversity BenefitsSeeds used for water purification; supports pollinators; agroforestry species
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsIndia (Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka), Philippines, Africa
Market UsesFresh drumsticks, moringa powder, moringa oil, supplements
Processing UsesLeaf powder, capsules, tea, oil extraction, water purification
Market Value (Approx.)₹20–60 per kg (drumsticks); ₹500–1500 per kg (leaf powder)
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismKnown as the "Miracle Tree" and "Tree of Life" for its nutritional density
Traditional UsesUsed in Siddha and Ayurvedic medicine for centuries; drumstick sambar is a South Indian staple
🍈 Nutritional Information
Calories64 kcal per 100g (leaves)
Dietary Fiber2.0 g per 100g
Vitamin C51.7 mg per 100g (leaves); 7x more than oranges (dried powder)
Vitamin A7564 IU per 100g (leaves); 4x more than carrots
Iron4.0 mg per 100g (leaves); 3x more than spinach
Potassium337 mg per 100g
AntioxidantsQuercetin, chlorogenic acid, beta-carotene, vitamin E
Other Key NutrientsCalcium (185 mg), protein (9.4 g), all essential amino acids
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anxiety & SleepLeaf extract shows adaptogenic properties; may reduce stress
Immune SupportExtremely high in vitamins A, C, and iron; boosts immunity significantly
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesIsothiocyanates in leaves have potent anti-inflammatory effects
Heart HealthMay lower cholesterol; leaf powder shows lipid-lowering effects in studies
Digestive HealthFiber and isothiocyanates support gut health; traditionally used for digestive issues

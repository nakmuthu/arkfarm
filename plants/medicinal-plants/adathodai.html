Adathodai / Malabar Nut

Justicia adhatoda

Common Names: Malabar Nut, Adathodai, Vasaka

Local Name: ஆடாதோடை (Tamil), Adusa / Vasaka (Hindi)

Family: Acanthaceae

Origin: South Asia

Plant Type: Perennial evergreen shrub

Average Lifespan: Perennial; long-lived shrub

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitErect, branching evergreen shrub
Plant Size1–2.5 meters tall
LeavesLarge, lanceolate, 10–20 cm long; dark green, bitter taste
FlowersWhite with purple markings; in dense spikes
Root SystemFibrous root system; well-anchored in soil
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates dry conditions
Temperature Range15–40°C
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy or sandy soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementLow to moderate; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly with compost; minimal fertilizer needed
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant; occasional leaf spot in humid conditions
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate readily; sow in well-drained seedling mix
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root easily; preferred method for propagation
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; drought-tolerant once established
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract bees and butterflies; provides dense cover for small birds
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Immune SupportLeaves used for respiratory conditions, asthma, and bronchitis; contains vasicine alkaloid
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesAnti-inflammatory and expectorant; used in cough syrups and herbal formulations
Digestive HealthUsed for fever, skin diseases, and as an antispasmodic in traditional medicine

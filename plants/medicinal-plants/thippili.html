Thippili / Indian Long Pepper

Piper longum

Common Names: Indian Long Pepper, Long Pepper, Thippili

Local Name: திப்பிலி (Tamil), Pipli (Hindi)

Family: Piperaceae

Origin: India, Nepal, Sri Lanka

Plant Type: Perennial climbing shrub

Average Lifespan: Perennial; 3–5 years productive life

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitClimbing or trailing shrub; stems root at nodes
Plant SizeClimbs 2–5 meters
LeavesOvate to cordate, alternate; dark green, glossy; aromatic
FlowersTiny; in cylindrical spikes; male and female on separate plants
Root SystemFibrous root system; adventitious roots at nodes for climbing
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical; humid; prefers forest understory conditions
Temperature Range20–35°C
Soil TypeWell-drained, fertile loamy soil rich in organic matter
Soil pH Preference5.5–7.0
Sun RequirementPartial shade; protect from harsh afternoon sun
Water RequirementModerate to high; consistent moisture; good drainage essential
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers with a trellis
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly with compost or vermicompost; mulch to retain moisture
Common PestsPhytophthora root rot; pollu beetle; leaf spot in humid conditions
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings with 2–3 nodes root easily in moist, shaded conditions
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageModerate; grows well under shade trees; suitable for agroforestry systems
Biodiversity BenefitsGrows as understory plant; fruits eaten by birds; supports forest biodiversity
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Immune SupportFruits used for respiratory conditions, asthma, and bronchitis; key ingredient in Trikatu formulation
Digestive HealthUsed for digestive issues, loss of appetite, and as a carminative in Ayurveda and Siddha
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesContains piperine; anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and bioavailability-enhancing properties

Aloe Vera

Aloe vera

Common Names: Aloe Vera, True Aloe, Barbados Aloe

Local Name: கற்றாழை (Tamil), Ghritkumari (Hindi)

Family: Asphodelaceae

Origin: Arabian Peninsula; cultivated worldwide

Plant Type: Perennial succulent

Average Lifespan: Perennial; 5–25 years

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitStemless or short-stemmed succulent; rosette-forming
Plant Size60–100 cm tall
LeavesThick, fleshy, lanceolate; grey-green with serrated margins; gel-filled interior
FlowersTubular, yellow to orange; on tall erect spikes
Root SystemShallow, fibrous root system; spreads laterally
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates arid conditions
Temperature Range13–40°C
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy or loamy soil; does not tolerate waterlogging
Soil pH Preference7.0–8.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementVery low; drought-tolerant; water sparingly
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, excellent container plant
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMinimal; occasional diluted liquid fertilizer in growing season
Common PestsRoot rot from overwatering; mealybugs and scale insects occasionally
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsOffsets (pups) from base of plant; allow cut end to dry before planting
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; stores water in leaves; ideal for water-scarce regions
Biodiversity BenefitsFlowers attract sunbirds and bees; low-maintenance ground cover
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesGel used for burns, wounds, sunburn, and skin conditions; anti-inflammatory and soothing
Digestive HealthJuice used for digestive health, constipation, and gut health; widely used in Ayurveda
Immune SupportRich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants; widely used in cosmetics and nutraceuticals

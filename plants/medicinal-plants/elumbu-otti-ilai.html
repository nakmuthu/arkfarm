Elumbu Otti Ilai / Bone Joint Leaves

Mukia maderaspatana

Common Names: Bone Joint Leaves, Madras Pea Pumpkin, Elumbu Otti Ilai

Local Name: எலும்பு ஒட்டி இலை (Tamil), Muktavan (Hindi)

Family: Cucurbitaceae

Origin: India, Africa, Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Annual/perennial climbing herb

Average Lifespan: Annual to short-lived perennial

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSlender, hairy climbing herb; uses tendrils to climb
Plant SizeClimbs 1–3 meters
LeavesDeeply lobed, rough-textured, hairy; resembles cucumber family leaves
FlowersSmall, yellow; male and female flowers on same plant
Root SystemFibrous root system; spreads via tendrils and runners
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; grows in disturbed areas and forest edges
Temperature Range20–38°C
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementModerate; consistent moisture preferred
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, with a trellis or support
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMonthly with compost or organic manure
Common PestsAphids, whiteflies; generally hardy in native conditions
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate readily; sow directly in well-drained soil
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings can be rooted in moist soil
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow to moderate; grows naturally in semi-arid regions
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides ground cover; small fruits eaten by birds and small animals
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesLeaves used for bone fractures and joint pain; applied as poultice to affected areas
Digestive HealthUsed for skin diseases, wounds, and as an anti-inflammatory in traditional Siddha medicine
Immune SupportWhole plant used in folk medicine for fever and as a general tonic

Maha Vilvam / Bael

Aegle marmelos

Common Names: Bael, Bengal Quince, Stone Apple, Maha Vilvam

Local Name: வில்வம் (Tamil), Bel / Bael (Hindi)

Family: Rutaceae

Origin: Indian subcontinent

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous tree

Average Lifespan: Perennial; can live for centuries

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitMedium-sized deciduous tree with spiny branches
Plant Size6–15 meters tall
LeavesTrifoliate, aromatic; sacred in Hindu worship; used in Shiva puja
FlowersGreenish-white, fragrant; in axillary clusters
Root SystemDeep taproot; highly drought-resistant
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates drought and waterlogging
Temperature Range10–45°C
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy or sandy soil; tolerates poor and alkaline soils
Soil pH Preference5.5–8.0
Sun RequirementFull sun
Water RequirementLow; highly drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, when young; needs open ground as it matures
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleAnnual compost application; minimal fertilizer needed
Common PestsFruit fly, leaf caterpillars; generally hardy and pest-resistant
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate readily; sow fresh seeds in well-drained mix
Propagation by CuttingsBudding and grafting used for true-to-type propagation
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; one of the most drought-tolerant fruit trees in India
Biodiversity BenefitsProvides shade and habitat; flowers attract bees; fruit eaten by wildlife
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Digestive HealthFruit used for digestive disorders, dysentery, and diarrhea; unripe fruit especially effective
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesLeaves and bark used for anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties
Immune SupportRich in vitamins and antioxidants; used in Ayurveda for overall health and immunity

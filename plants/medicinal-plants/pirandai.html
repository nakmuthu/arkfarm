Pirandai

Cissus quadrangularis

Common Names: Veldt Grape, Devil's Backbone, Bone Setter, Pirandai

Local Name: பிரண்டை (Tamil), Hadjod (Hindi)

Family: Vitaceae

Origin: Africa, Arabia, South Asia

Plant Type: Perennial succulent climber

Average Lifespan: Perennial; long-lived climber

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitSucculent climber with distinctive four-angled, jointed stems
Plant SizeClimbs 3–5 meters; stems 1–2 cm wide
LeavesSimple, ovate to cordate; deciduous; appear at nodes between stem segments
FlowersSmall, greenish-yellow; in cymes; followed by small red berries
Root SystemFibrous root system; tendrils for climbing; easily propagated from stem cuttings
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates heat and drought
Temperature Range20–40°C
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy or loamy soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Water RequirementLow; drought-tolerant; water sparingly
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, with a trellis or support structure
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMinimal; occasional compost; avoid overwatering
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant; stem rot from overwatering
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by CuttingsStem segments root very easily; allow cut end to dry before planting in well-drained soil
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; stores water in stems; ideal for dry gardens
Biodiversity BenefitsBerries eaten by birds; provides climbing cover for walls and fences
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesUsed for bone fractures and joint pain; accelerates bone healing; rich in calcium and vitamin C
Digestive HealthStems used for digestive issues, hemorrhoids, and as a digestive tonic in Siddha medicine
Immune SupportRich in antioxidants; used for weight management and metabolic health in traditional medicine

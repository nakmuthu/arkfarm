Vathanarayanan / Peacock Flower

Caesalpinia pulcherrima

Common Names: Peacock Flower, Pride of Barbados, Dwarf Poinciana

Local Name: மயில் கொன்றை / வாதநாராயணன் (Tamil), Gulmohar Lata (Hindi)

Family: Fabaceae

Origin: Tropical Americas; naturalized in tropics

Plant Type: Perennial shrub/small tree

Average Lifespan: Perennial; long-lived in tropical climates

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitErect, branching shrub or small tree with spiny stems
Plant Size2–4 meters tall
LeavesBipinnate, feathery; bright green leaflets
FlowersShowy orange-red or yellow; in terminal racemes; long red stamens
Root SystemDeep taproot; drought-resistant once established
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates heat and drought
Temperature Range20–40°C
Soil TypeWell-drained sandy or loamy soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun
Water RequirementLow; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, when young; needs large container as it matures
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleOccasional compost; minimal fertilizer needed
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant; occasional aphids or caterpillars
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate well after scarification; soak overnight before sowing
Propagation by CuttingsSemi-hardwood cuttings can be rooted with rooting hormone
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; highly drought-tolerant once established
Biodiversity BenefitsAttracts sunbirds, butterflies, and bees; excellent ornamental and wildlife plant
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesBark and leaves used in traditional medicine for fever and malaria
Digestive HealthUsed for menstrual disorders and as an emmenagogue in traditional practice
Immune SupportLeaf decoction used for respiratory conditions and skin diseases in folk medicine

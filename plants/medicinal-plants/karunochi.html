Karunochi / Chaste Tree

Vitex negundo

Common Names: Chaste Tree, Five-leaved Chaste Tree, Karunochi

Local Name: நொச்சி / கருநொச்சி (Tamil), Nirgundi (Hindi)

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: South and Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Perennial deciduous shrub/small tree

Average Lifespan: Perennial; long-lived shrub

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitErect, aromatic deciduous shrub or small tree
Plant Size2–5 meters tall
LeavesPalmately compound with 3–5 leaflets; aromatic, grey-green beneath
FlowersSmall, blue-purple; in terminal panicles; fragrant
Root SystemDeep, spreading root system; drought-resistant
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical to subtropical; tolerates dry and semi-arid conditions
Temperature Range15–40°C
Soil TypeWell-drained loamy or sandy soil; tolerates poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun
Water RequirementLow; drought-tolerant once established
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in large containers when young
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleOccasional compost; minimal fertilizer needed
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant due to aromatic foliage
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate readily; sow in well-drained seedling mix
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root easily during the growing season
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageLow; highly drought-tolerant; suitable for dry land planting
Biodiversity BenefitsAttracts bees and butterflies; aromatic leaves deter pests naturally
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesLeaves used for joint pain, arthritis, and headache; strong anti-inflammatory properties
Immune SupportUsed for fever and respiratory conditions; key herb in Siddha and Ayurveda
Digestive HealthLeaf smoke used for relief from headaches; leaf poultice applied for swellings

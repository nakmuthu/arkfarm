Pei Mirati

Anisomeles malabarica

Common Names: Pei Mirati, Malabar Catmint, Indian Catmint

Local Name: Pei Mirati (Tamil), Peimuratti (Tamil), Gobhi (Hindi)

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: South Asia and Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Annual or short-lived perennial herb

Average Lifespan: 1–2 years; self-seeds readily

🌿 Botanical Description
Growth HabitErect, branching herb with square stems (typical of Lamiaceae family)
Plant Size60–120 cm tall
LeavesOvate to lanceolate, 5–10 cm long, serrated margins, aromatic, soft-hairy; strong minty-camphor scent
FlowersSmall, tubular, pink to purple, borne in dense whorls along stem; typical mint-family flower structure
Flower CharacteristicsAttractive to bees and butterflies; blooms profusely in cooler months
Root SystemFibrous, shallow taproot
☀ Growing Conditions
ClimateTropical and subtropical; grows as a weed in disturbed areas
Temperature Range20–35°C; tolerates heat well
Rainfall Requirement600–1500 mm annually; drought-tolerant once established
Soil TypeWell-drained, moderately fertile soil; grows in poor soils
Soil pH Preference6.0–7.5
Sun RequirementFull sun to partial shade
Spacing30–50 cm between plants
Irrigation MethodMinimal irrigation; tolerates dry conditions
Water RequirementLow; drought-tolerant
Can it Grow in Pots?Yes, in medium-sized containers
🌾 Cultivation & Care
Fertilizer ScheduleMinimal; compost at planting is sufficient
Recommended Organic FertilizersCompost, vermicompost; avoid excess nitrogen which reduces medicinal potency
Common PestsGenerally pest-resistant due to aromatic oils; occasional aphids
Common DiseasesPowdery mildew in humid conditions; root rot if overwatered
Disease Prevention & RemediesGood drainage, adequate spacing for air circulation
Pruning TimeRegular pinching to encourage bushy growth and delay flowering
How to PrunePinch growing tips; harvest leaves before flowering for best medicinal quality
🌸 Flowering & Fruiting
Flowering MonthsOctober–February (cooler months in South India)
Pollination TypeInsect pollinated; bees and butterflies
Pollination NotesExcellent pollinator plant; attracts diverse bee species
🌱 Pollination & Propagation
Propagation by SeedsSeeds germinate readily in 1–2 weeks; self-seeds prolifically
Propagation by CuttingsStem cuttings root easily in moist soil
💧 Environmental Impact & Sustainability
Water UsageVery low; ideal for dry gardens and medicinal herb beds
Biodiversity BenefitsExcellent pollinator plant; attracts bees, butterflies, and beneficial insects
Companion PlantsOther medicinal herbs; repels some pests due to aromatic oils
💰 Economic Importance
Commercial Production RegionsTamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka (India); Sri Lanka, Southeast Asia
Market UsesTraditional medicine, Ayurvedic and Siddha formulations, herbal teas
Market Value (Approx.)Sold as dried herb in traditional medicine markets; ₹100–300 per kg dried
🌿 Cultural Significance
SymbolismImportant plant in Siddha medicine tradition of Tamil Nadu; used for centuries in traditional healing
Traditional UsesUsed in Siddha and Ayurvedic medicine for fever, rheumatism, and skin diseases; leaves applied as poultice for joint pain
🌿 Medicinal Uses
Anti-inflammatory PropertiesOvatodiolide compound shows significant anti-inflammatory activity; used for arthritis and joint pain
Immune SupportAntimicrobial properties; used traditionally to treat infections and fever
Digestive HealthLeaf decoction used for stomach disorders and intestinal worms
Anxiety & SleepMild sedative properties; used in traditional medicine for nervous disorders
